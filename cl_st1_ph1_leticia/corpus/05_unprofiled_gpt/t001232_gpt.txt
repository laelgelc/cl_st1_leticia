I told him I was genuinely glad he’d taken some time to think and reached a calmer place. He started talking about himself like he was the wind in that proverb about the wind, the sun and the man with the coat. So I picked up the metaphor and said that when he’d been blowing hard, all that pressure just made me cling onto my coat tighter, but if he was gentler, I’d naturally relax a bit. I told him, honestly, that it was the first sensible thing he’d said in a long time. He said that, even though the whole situation had been painful for him, he was actually glad I’d come to that understanding.
