We did one of those alligator farms as well while we were down there, which was quite good fun, but because it was spring break we just hadn’t clocked that basically the whole of America was going to be there at the same time. It was absolutely heaving everywhere. And the weather was just mad – up in northern Florida it was something like thirty degrees and about eight percent humidity, which was actually really nice, quite dry and bearable. Then as we drove down into the Keys it just cranked up and up until it was nearer forty degrees and felt like a hundred percent humidity. We honestly thought we were going to die if we didn’t get out of there, it was just oppressive, you couldn’t escape it. But as soon as we headed back up towards Miami again it all settled down and became really pleasant, back to that nice, warm but manageable sort of heat.
