they were incredible about honing everything, you know. The Marx Brothers would literally take the whole act on tour first – all the jokes, all the routines they were planning for the film. They’d do it night after night, tweak it, and only the bits that got the biggest laughs with real audiences actually made it into the movie. If something didn’t land, it was just gone.

And then you’ve got Irving Thalberg at MGM, this young studio head who was absolutely ruthless about quality. On something like *A Night at the Opera* he’d send the script back over and over – fifteen rewrites, that kind of thing. There’s this story about a writer who finally brings him yet another draft. Thalberg sits there, reads the whole thing without a flicker, totally stone-faced, and the poor guy’s dying inside, thinking it’s a disaster. And then Thalberg just looks up and says it’s got some of the funniest stuff he’s ever read.
