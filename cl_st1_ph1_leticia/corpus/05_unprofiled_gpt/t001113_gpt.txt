Last night we were talking about how hard it would be to have a daughter and just be constantly worrying about her safety, and it’s really stuck with me. It frustrates me that the default response is always, “teach your daughter to protect herself, teach her to be careful,” as if the burden should sit on her shoulders from the start. I don’t think it should work like that at all. Instead, we should be putting the effort into educating young men not to attack people in the first place, not to treat others as targets. That’s where the focus needs to be, and I feel very strongly about that.
