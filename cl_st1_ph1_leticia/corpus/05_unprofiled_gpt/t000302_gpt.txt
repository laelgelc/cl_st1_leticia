It was quite difficult to adjust when I came back here, because in the Middle East, in Oman, in the boarding school, you just shook hands all the time. You’d shake hands with the people in the shops, and every morning you’d go round the dorm and shake hands with every child. It was constant, really. Interestingly, though, when they came into lessons you didn’t shake hands; that just wasn’t done in the classroom. But in almost any other situation, especially around the dorms, you were always shaking hands.
