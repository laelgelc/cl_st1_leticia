It was actually quite an interesting idea behind it. The whole point of the exhibition was to challenge this notion that British industry is dead, you know, that nothing is made here anymore. They wanted to show that there’s still a lot of industry going on all over the country. So they got nine different photographers involved, and each one focused on a different region and had their own way of approaching the subject, different styles and perspectives. It’s not just staying in one place either, it’s going on a nationwide tour. Funny thing was, the venue itself was absolutely heaving, really busy, but in that particular exhibition there were hardly any visitors at all.
