he wrote *The Parade's Gone By* basically out of frustration, you know, because the silent film prints he was seeing were so bad, projected wrong, and just treated terribly. And on top of that, the cultural memory around them was that silent films were sort of amateurish, like people thought those filmmakers didn’t really know what they were doing. Even the filmmakers themselves sometimes bought into that idea. So Brownlow kind of went on this one‑man crusade to show that, no, these guys weren’t idiots at all, they were actually really skilled and serious about what they were making. He more or less devoted his whole life to these films, to defending them and restoring their reputation.
