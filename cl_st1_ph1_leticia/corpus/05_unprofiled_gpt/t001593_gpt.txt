We were driving through that old area, you know, where some of the houses have been knocked down and others rebuilt, so it all looked half-familiar, half-strange. I took a wrong turning, went into the street near where your grandma grew up, thinking I was heading the right way. It felt like I recognised bits of it, but it wasn’t quite how I remembered. Then this lorry flashed its lights at me and I suddenly realised I’d driven the wrong way down a one-way street. That’s when it clicked what I’d done, and I thought, “Trust me, in our own area as well.”
