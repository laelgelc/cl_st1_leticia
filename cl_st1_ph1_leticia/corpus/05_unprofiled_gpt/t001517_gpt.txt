I was watching that “Big Fat Gypsy Weddings” programme, the one where it was all about gypsies marrying non‑gypsies, and it really struck me how their whole set‑up works. In the gypsy culture they showed, the women just stay at home, clean, look after the children, that’s their entire role. Meanwhile the men seem to be out drinking, fighting, dealing with horses and so on. It’s such a stark division. It made me think that if those are the expectations, then the only way those mixed relationships are really likely to work is if it’s a gypsy woman marrying a non‑gypsy man. Because he probably wouldn’t mind her staying at home, he’d likely be quite pleased to have someone doing all that domestic stuff.
