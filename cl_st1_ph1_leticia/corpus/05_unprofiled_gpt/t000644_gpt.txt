That’s what my lecturer was getting at with this idea of “cyber provincialism” – you’ve got the potential to connect to everyone, but you end up in these cosy, closed circles, repeating the same local in‑jokes and cultural references.

He had this really funny way of illustrating it in his own work: he talked about taking something as daft and ordinary as a garden gnome and scaling it up to the size of the Statue of Liberty. So you’d have this ridiculous, gigantic gnome looming over everything, kind of poking fun at that small‑scale mindset.

I really liked that idea, so I grabbed it as the basis for my first art project, and then I used it again as the “obsession” theme when I did that creative education course.
