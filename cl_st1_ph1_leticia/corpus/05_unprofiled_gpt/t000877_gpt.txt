Well I suppose part of it is that I was on my own for such a long time before I married, and then I didn’t marry until my mid‑forties. So I had all that space to try lots of different things, to see what suited me, to follow my nose a bit. I was rather influenced by this book called *Anybody Can Do Anything*, about a woman who just kept changing jobs, always moving on to something more interesting, something a bit better. That really stayed with me, that sense that you don’t have to be fixed in one role for life, you can keep shifting and learning.

And of course, like anybody really, I’ve had my share of very big highs and very big lows. You come to see that they often go together somehow – if you reach for the really big highs, you do seem to risk the really big lows as well.
