If you just want to teach English as a foreign language at a university, that's different – if you've got an MA and loads of ESL teaching experience then you can do that, you can get a job teaching EFL at a uni. But for linguistics it's stricter: all the lecturers have PhDs, so you need that first.

And here becoming a professor is a really long process. You start off as a lecturer, then eventually you might become a senior lecturer, and after many years you might finally become a professor. In America it's not like that at all – anyone who teaches at a university is called a professor. So the word “professor” really depends on which country you're in, it doesn't mean exactly the same thing everywhere.
