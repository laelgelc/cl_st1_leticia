I was only twenty-three then, in 1956, and suddenly found myself in charge of a cypher office. We handled both Air Force and British government codes and cyphers, and some of it was extremely restricted material. Certain messages were only to be seen by the governor and the very top RAF officers, so for those I had to lock myself away completely to deal with them. It was quite a responsibility at that age. I remember with particular pleasure that the governor would send a car for me, with no number plate, just a silver crown. That little detail made me feel rather important at the time.
