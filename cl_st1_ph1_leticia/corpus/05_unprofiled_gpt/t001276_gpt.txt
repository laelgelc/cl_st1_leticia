In the seminar I only go through these things quite briefly, just to raise their awareness. I start with some of the basic pronunciation issues, like contrasting long and short vowels – for example “shot” versus “short”, or “chip” versus “cheap” – so they can hear how a tiny difference in sound can completely change the word. Then I move on to consonant clusters in words like “comfortable”, where there are a lot of consonants together and that can be tricky. After that I use the word “look” as an example, because learners often struggle not just with saying it, but with the phrasal uses: “look after”, “look for”, and so on, to show that even a very simple word can actually be quite difficult.
