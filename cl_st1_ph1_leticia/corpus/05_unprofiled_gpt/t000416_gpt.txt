If you’re a really knowledgeable mature student, you do have to be a bit careful not to come across as too challenging. Otherwise you risk alienating everyone. Some lecturers can actually feel threatened if you push too hard, or if it sounds like you’re trying to catch them out. And a lot of the other students, especially if they’ve never really thought about this stuff before, will just see intellectual challenge as you being argumentative. Part of the problem is that some people don’t actually understand what an argument is in an academic sense; they just hear “argument” and think “row” or “being difficult”, rather than seeing it as a way of testing ideas.
