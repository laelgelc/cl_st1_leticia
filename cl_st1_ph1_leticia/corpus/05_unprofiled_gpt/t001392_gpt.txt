I keep coming back to this model of personal learning networks where students are connecting with the business world before they formally enter it. 

Most of them are just out there trying to earn money in part‑time or holiday jobs, and those jobs usually have nothing to do with what they’re being educated or trained in. I don’t think that means they should stop studying while they’re on holiday, though. They can still be developing a personal learning network so that, regardless of the job, they’re finding mechanisms to build experience that’s somehow connected. 

From their point of view, they might not see that doing something community‑based, with a bit of responsibility or a small leadership role, is actually quite strongly connected to doing a role in business, like running a little project.
