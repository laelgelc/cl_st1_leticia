what I’ve really noticed is that the ones who treat academic work like it’s just descriptive writing, just “here’s what the book says,” they’re the ones who are going to fail. you can’t just list stuff, you have to be making an argument. what’s always worked for me is being really clear about what my argument actually is and why I’m taking that line, laying out the rationale properly. if I do that, I can still pass even if I haven’t read every single book on the list. it does feel a bit like I’m kidding myself and the assessors, because I know I can always go back and read the book later in my spare time.
