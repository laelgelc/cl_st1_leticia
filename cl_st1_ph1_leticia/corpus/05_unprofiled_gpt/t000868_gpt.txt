On my last cruise I met this really lovely, lively woman, very good-looking, in her fifties, who came along to my lectures and seemed to enjoy them. We ended up having dinner with her and her partner one evening. As we were talking, I began to notice her speech patterns. She kept saying “was you?” instead of “were you?”, and she did it quite consistently. She clearly wasn’t aware of it at all; it was just her normal London-area way of speaking. I found it rather quaint, actually.
