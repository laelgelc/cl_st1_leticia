I saw this post online from an American who was totally baffled by British people saying “it’s time for tea.” They’d decided we must all be completely obsessed with tea itself, like we were constantly stopping the day to have these big, formal tea gatherings. They described it almost like a ritual, as if everyone in Britain regularly sat down for some sort of special tea event whenever they said “tea.” Reading it, I just thought they’d completely misunderstood what was going on, especially in the North. I’m pretty sure what they were actually hearing was people using “tea” to mean the evening meal, not an actual tea-drinking ceremony at all.
