We had to graft then, proper forty‑eight hour weeks, and that was just normal. You’d be in on Saturdays as well, and the only bit of a break you’d get was a half‑day in the middle of the week. Getting there wasn’t easy either – no cars like you’ve got now. You’d be walking, or on your bike, or maybe jump on a tram, or even ride on the back of a wagon if you could. And now you hear people moaning about their hours when they’re sat in offices all day, hardly lifting a finger. They don’t know they’re born, really. They don’t realise how good they’ve got it compared to what we had to do.
