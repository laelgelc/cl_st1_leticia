I mean, the way I see it, you’d still pay for your first degree, but it’d be part of this properly structured “earn as you learn” setup. So you’re really coming to university to do your studies, that’s the core of it. But then, built into your holidays and what would normally be your free time, you’re actually going off to work in some partner organisation.

It wouldn’t just be a random job on the side; it would be part of the curriculum. You’d earn some money, which helps you pay your way, but you’d also be learning on the job. Then you’d come back and tell us what you’ve learned, and we’d look at the whole lot together: your exams, your portfolio from the workplace, your coursework, odd bits and pieces. That gives a much broader assessment of what you’ve actually learned.
