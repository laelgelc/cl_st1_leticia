So this is what I was trying to explain to people, and hardly anyone’s actually heard of it. There’s this translation memory software that translators use: you run the text through a machine and it basically chops everything up into little chunks, like sentence by sentence or phrase by phrase, and stores them. Then, when the same or a very similar sentence comes up again later, it just auto‑populates that bit for you. The translator can then either accept it or reject it, but it means they’re not re‑typing the same thing over and over. It makes the whole job much faster, but also more accurate and consistent, because repeated phrases are always the same. And it cuts costs too, because clients don’t get charged the full price again for repeated content.
