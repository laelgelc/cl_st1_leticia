we’d finished the business we were there for and gone out to lunch, and over the meal this colleague started telling me about the way the rail companies had to handle their insurance. He explained that every rail company was required to maintain a uniform level of annual insurance cover, and they all had to submit particular bits of information by certain set dates during the year. It was laid down quite clearly what had to be done and when. He then mentioned that one of the companies had actually missed one of these deadlines. That immediately worried me, because if there was some sort of problem with the renewal, it could mean there was a gap in cover. In that situation, if there was an accident, the people who were injured might not receive any compensation at all.
