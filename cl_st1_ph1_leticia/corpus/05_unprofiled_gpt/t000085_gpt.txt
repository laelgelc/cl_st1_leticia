Well apart from that very first placement in Henley-in-Arden, which was just a short early one, I’ve only ever actually had proper teaching jobs in city schools. The only real contrast I’ve had was the school I worked at abroad. There the behaviour in the classroom was manageable, really, that wasn’t the main issue. The pressure came more from the headteacher, who was quite difficult, and from very pushy parents rather than from the kids themselves.

I had a big class there, thirty-three of them, all mixed ability, and there wasn’t anyone else in the room with me, no additional teacher or assistant, so it was full-on. But I sort of chose that path on purpose. I’ve always deliberately gone for the more challenging schools because I think that’s where the better teachers are most needed. There doesn’t seem much point in putting all your effort into the easy places when it’s the tough ones that really need you.
