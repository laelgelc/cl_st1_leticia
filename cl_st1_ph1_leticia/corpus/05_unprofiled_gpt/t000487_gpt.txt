I got talked into moving my pension into this new superannuation scheme. On paper it looked better than what I already had, so I thought, “Why not?” and went ahead and transferred it all over. Only afterwards did I realise it wasn’t one of those things you could just leave alone; you had to actively manage it, keep an eye on it, make decisions. I really didn’t want that, so I tried to move it back to where it was before. By then, though, it was too late. I couldn’t reverse it, and I ended up stuck with two frozen pots instead. One of them is projected to pay me something like twelve pounds a year, and the other one about a hundred and twenty pounds a year.
