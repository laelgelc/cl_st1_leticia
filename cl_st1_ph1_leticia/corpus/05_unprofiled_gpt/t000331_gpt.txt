Sorry, let me give you a clearer example. Think of “the monkey kissed the lion” – that’s active. The passive version is “the lion was kissed by the monkey”. It’s the same event, but the subject and object have swapped places. What’s interesting is that children really struggle with that. Even when you say “the lion was kissed by the monkey”, they still tend to think it was the lion kissing the monkey, just because of the way the sentence is structured. It takes them quite a while to learn that the subject and object can be reversed.
