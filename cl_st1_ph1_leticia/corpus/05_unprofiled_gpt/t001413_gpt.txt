I was so close to just paying the £24 and getting it over with, because in the grand scheme of things it’s not a lot of money, is it? But I just thought, no, they’re in the wrong here and I’m not paying it. So I rang Citizens Advice and explained the whole thing, and they said no, under the new Consumer Rights Act they can’t do that. 

They pointed me to some template letters on their website, proper legal‑sounding ones, asserting my right to a full refund because the goods were faulty within thirty days. One of them was titled something like “notice before a county court claim” or similar. So I used that, adapted it, and sent it off, and in the letter I also asked them about independent dispute resolution, you know, what their process was for that, because I wanted it all done properly and by the book.
