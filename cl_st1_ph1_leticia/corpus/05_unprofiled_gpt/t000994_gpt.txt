I just thought, back then, what’s the point of saving for some distant future that might never actually happen? So I cashed in that three hundred and thirty-three pounds instead of leaving it there till I was sixty‑five. It felt logical at the time, like the world might end up a bit Mad Max and all that saving would be pointless. But then when I was about twenty‑eight I got automatically enrolled into a pension, six percent of my salary going in. Looking back, I can see that turning that down would’ve been a really bad move. I can’t really claim any wisdom for having it though, it just happened to me.
