You can't get a mortgage just on foundations because it has to actually resemble a house. The lenders need it to be at least a proper shell, something effectively inhabitable, before they'll consider it. That's why on things like Grand Designs they usually do it in stages. People tend to use their savings to buy the land first, then they take out loans specifically on the building work. As the house gets more completed, it gets revalued, and each time the value goes up they can then apply for a bigger mortgage, increasing it step by step as the project progresses.
