Fruit trees along a wall are such a good idea, you know. They really make use of the space and you can train them nicely, and they get that warmth from the wall as well. Our own apple trees have been a bit inconsistent though. This year most of the apples came from the Golden Delicious, and they were really rather poor quality, not very nice at all. The James Grieve hardly produced anything this time, but usually it’s superb – really large apples, absolutely lovely if you pick them at just the right moment, before anything gets at them or they fall off. 

Last year we had a really excellent crop from the cooking apple tree, do you remember? We had so many we were giving them away all over the place, including to you. This year, though, there were very few apples on that tree, almost nothing compared with before.
