in Israel Saturday is just treated as the Sabbath by pretty much everyone, even if they’re not religious, so they generally don’t do anything. For the religious people it’s much stricter: they’re not supposed to create or use things, so no cars, no turning lights on and off, all of that sort of thing. Because of that, almost everything is closed, public life just really shuts down. It’s not only a religious rule, it’s become part of the general culture, that sense of Saturday inactivity.
