When I got a new top set of first‑years, that first week I never did any maths with them at all. I used to say to them, “Look, I can’t control what you do anywhere else in school, that’s nothing to do with me. I only control what you do in my lessons.” And then I’d sit them down and say, “Right, you all know how to behave, don’t you? And you all know exactly how to misbehave as well.” I’d get them to admit that, get them talking about it, and then I’d say, “So, we’re agreed, you’re not going to play up for me in here, yes?” Once they’d actually said it, I’d hold them to it. I always felt if you did that in the first week, really hammered it home, it set the tone for the next five years with them. You didn’t need to go on and on after that, because they knew where they stood and what I expected every time they walked through the door.
