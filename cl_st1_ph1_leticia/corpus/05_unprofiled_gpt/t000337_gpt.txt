Russia really did feel quite scary at the time. Everything was so tightly controlled: we had specific destinations we were allowed to go to, there were checkpoints along the roads, and once we got to Moscow we had to be accompanied by Intourist guides. It never felt like you could just wander about freely. The food and drink in the summer didn’t help either – it was pretty grim, really. Mostly cabbages, and tea to drink, and none of the nice fresh fruit you’d hope for in hot weather. Poland, by contrast, felt much more prosperous. The fields looked better tended, the crops were bigger, and overall it just seemed a lot better off.
