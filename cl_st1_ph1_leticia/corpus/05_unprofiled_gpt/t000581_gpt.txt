So the first big one was Typhoon Bolaven in Mokpo, right after I’d arrived back in Korea. The school was shut for the kids, but they still ordered us in to “protect” the school, even though it was clearly dangerous outside. Crosses were falling off church steeples and smashing cars, roof tiles were flying, the power kept going out, there was heavy rain, sparks coming off electrical equipment, and the water in the street was really deep. 

I had to walk through all of that to get there, and when I arrived the place was practically empty. Then they told me there was no lunch, so I had to walk back through the storm, then back again, and then home again at the end of the day. It’s about twenty minutes each way, so I ended up trudging through this typhoon four times. It just felt incredibly dangerous and completely pointless, and I was really frustrated.
