When we got taken over by the parent company nearly three years ago, we actually stayed on our original contracts, which was quite good for us. We’ve always had twenty‑five days’ holiday plus the bank holidays. The parent company’s people don’t start on that; they get a lower amount and only build up to more days after they’ve done these really strict full calendar years of service. So it’s meant that, when we’re recruiting, we can honestly say our benefits package is better than if you went straight into the group. It’s been quite a useful selling point.
