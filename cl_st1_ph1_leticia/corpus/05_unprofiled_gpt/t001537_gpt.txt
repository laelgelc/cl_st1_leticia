Everyone basically does the same thing there: you study like mad all through school, then you go straight into university. For the boys there’s this extra bit where they have to do two or three years of military service, usually right in the middle of their degree, then they go back and finish uni. By the time they’re in their late twenties they’re supposed to be married with kids already. If you hit thirty and you’re not married people think you’re weird. So it’s just school, university, army, then marriage and children, with a huge amount of pressure.
