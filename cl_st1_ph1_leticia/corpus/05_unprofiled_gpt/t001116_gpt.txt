In that Bill Bailey sketch, he’s taking the phrase “X on acid” and showing how it’s actually really dull rather than exciting. Instead of some wild psychedelic trip, being on acid is portrayed as sitting there staring at a decorated plate for hours. He goes on about how you’d get fixated on something completely mundane, like the leg of a chair and a crossed leg, and then just repeat this one line over and over again – “makes it end, makes it end, makes it end” – for about an hour and a half.
