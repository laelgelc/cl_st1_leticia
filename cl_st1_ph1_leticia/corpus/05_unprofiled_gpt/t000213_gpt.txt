When you talk on the phone, especially when emotions are high, people forget the finer details and just latch onto whatever feels defensive or upsetting in that moment. They remember those bits, not the whole picture. With an email, everything you’ve said is there, fixed, and she can go back to it, reread it, and really think it over in her own time – sort of mentally chew it over. That’s exactly why you have to be very careful how you word it, so it says precisely what you mean.
