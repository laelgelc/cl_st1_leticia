I just don't buy into this idea that a “proper” holiday has to mean going away somewhere, jumping on a plane and ticking off another destination. For me, a holiday is about having time to do things you actually enjoy, the stuff you can't really get stuck into when you're working all the time. That could be going somewhere, but it could just as easily be staying put and having a stretch of days to work on projects or read or whatever it is you like.

What I really don't get is this competitive rush people seem to be in, like they're collecting countries as trophies. There was someone in work taking two very small kids off on a two‑week holiday, and to me that just sounds like incredibly hard work rather than some essential experience they all have to have. It's not that it's wrong, it just feels like people convince themselves it's necessary when maybe it isn't.
