well exactly, when you build a story you usually start by mapping time against the key acts, or the escalation points, so you know where things rise and shift instead of just having random fighting. Then you decide how much you’re going to lean on the protagonist’s character arc. To do that properly you have to pin down the core of the character – the underlying reason he does anything, the common thread that pulls him through the whole trajectory. Once you’ve got that spine, everything he does in the plot should follow from that, rather than just being noise.
