Well, and it’s not just about the academic merit of the place, is it, when you’re choosing a university. You’ve actually got to like the campus and the town, somewhere you want to live for three or four years. That’s part of it. And really, the whole idea of going to university in Europe, and internationally, is that there’s this element of geographical mobility built into the experience. You see it with all these students from China, from Vietnam – they come over imagining London, say, is going to be amazing, and then they get there and realise they actually hate it and would much rather be somewhere smaller and quieter. But they’re basically trapped, because they’ve had to pay all the fees up front and that locks them into the whole payment scheme, so they can’t just up sticks and move.
