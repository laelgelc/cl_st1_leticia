I really did feel incredibly lucky, you know, to actually own that house. After my mum died and I’d stayed on there, it ended up being mine. My guardian by then was in a council house with her husband, and she came round one day and asked if I wanted to sell it. I said no, flat out. I wasn’t going to let it go. 

Around that time, a school friend of mine who was working at Woolworths told me about this bloke she worked with, called Roger. It was 1970 and everyone had long hair, and there’s Roger with this great big handlebar moustache, completely out of step. Anyway, he’d decided he was going to buy a Triumph GT6 sports car, take it out to Rhodesia under sanctions after UDI, and flog it there for a profit.
