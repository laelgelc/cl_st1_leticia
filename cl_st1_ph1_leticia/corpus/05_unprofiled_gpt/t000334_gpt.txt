Well, I pretty much agree with what you’ve been saying. It feels like all the natural freedoms we used to just take for granted are being chipped away and controlled more and more. There’s this constant sense now that someone’s watching, or at least could be watching if they chose to.  

You see it in the way governments snoop on people. In the States, with all the surveillance that’s come out into the open, and here in the UK as well – all the monitoring, the data collection, the way they can track what you’re doing. And then on top of that you’ve got CCTV cameras everywhere, so even just walking down the street you’re on film half the time. It’s like the default assumption now is that everyone needs to be watched, just in case.

So yes, I do think a lot of our natural freedoms are being eroded, bit by bit. You can feel the space closing in – less privacy, less anonymity, more rules about where you can go and what you can do, and more systems in place to make sure you comply.  

But even with all that, we’re still relatively lucky compared to some places. Take China, for example. Over there, public gatherings are really tightly restricted. People can’t just get together in large groups without it being controlled or broken up. The state keeps an incredibly tight grip on what people can do in public, what they can say, how they can organise.

At the same time, though, expectations in China are changing. As people’s lives improve and they see more of the outside world, their sense of what freedom could look like is rising. That kind of rising expectation can be dangerous for a government that relies on very strict control. It makes you think that at some point there could be a revolution, or at least some major upheaval, because people won’t be satisfied forever with being so tightly managed.

For now, though, the government there still has very strong control. They’ve built a system that makes it hard for opposition or protest to really take off. So you’ve got this tension: increasing expectations of freedom on one side, and an extremely tight, watchful state on the other.
