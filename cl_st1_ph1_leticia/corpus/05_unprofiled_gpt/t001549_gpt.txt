In Korea and especially in China there are just phenomenal numbers of vacant high‑rise properties. Wealthy Chinese, because they can't take large sums of money out of the country, end up investing in these new tower blocks, and they just stand there empty. When we were in Mokpo we only ever saw one homeless person, and somehow that felt even sadder, because he was just alone, there wasn’t that kind of homeless community you sometimes see elsewhere.

It really made me wonder why he couldn’t just be given one of those many empty flats. There’s all this unused space, and then this one man just wandering around with nowhere to go. After a while we stopped seeing him around town, and I started to suspect he must have died that winter. It stuck with me, that contrast between all those empty apartments and this one person who clearly needed somewhere to live, and yet there was no way for him to get inside.
