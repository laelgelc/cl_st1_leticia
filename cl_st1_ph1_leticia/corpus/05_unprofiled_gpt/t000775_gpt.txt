There were actually three doctors and then me as the sort of junior at camp, which sounded a bit dodgy once you wrote it down. One of the kids was doing a postcard home and started to write that they were being looked after by “Dr [misleading name]”, and I suddenly thought, that’s going to sound really wrong to their parents. I made them change it, but even so, on parents’ day I still got these odd looks when everyone came in and the kids were calling me that. The kids loved giving strange nicknames anyway – there was a boy in my bunk called “Skittles” because he once won a big basketball game wearing loads of different coloured clothes.
