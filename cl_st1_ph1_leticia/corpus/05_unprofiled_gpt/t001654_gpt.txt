I think my grandmother probably had endometrial cancer, or possibly ovarian cancer, way back, but I’d never really joined the dots. My mother was always saying, “there’s no cancer in our family,” and I just absorbed that. So it never occurred to me that I might get cancer myself. On some subliminal level I must have believed I simply wasn’t prone to it. 

That fed into this whole sense of security I had about my own risk. I kind of accepted this apparently logical idea that if there was no family history, then there couldn’t be any sort of genetic component. Things like the BRCA gene, all of that, I just assumed didn’t apply to me, because I’d been told, over and over, that cancer wasn’t “in” our family.
