As I was saying, you can actually pay the AA or the RAC to go and check a car over for you before you buy it. Given how much money you’re spending, it might be worth doing that, just for the peace of mind. It’s a big investment, so having someone professional look it over makes sense. Years ago you didn’t really do that though; you’d just buy a copy of What Car? and read up on what to look for when you were buying. They’d have lists of things to check yourself, so you’d sort of go armed with that instead.
