It does work for me though, that sort of memorisation. There are just some things you have to learn by rote, and I’ve always found I’d rather just stand there and recite them endlessly until they stick. With A‑level English Lit, when we had those closed‑book exams, the teachers were very keen on us memorising a neat little bank of key quotations from each poem. But because they were poems, I just thought, well, I might as well learn the whole thing. So I did: whole set texts, entire poems, just going over and over them until I knew them by heart.
