If people don’t value education, they’re not going to accept it, and you can’t force it on them. A lot of the lack of achievement you see is because individuals don’t take the opportunities that are there. There are plenty of people who aren’t particularly academic who’ve still achieved a lot in life, because they’ve taken chances and worked at things. And there are also very intelligent people who just don’t care, who don’t bother, and so they don’t achieve very much at all despite their ability.

So yes, part of the wider situation is down to the government, but not all of it. If you grow up on a council estate with parents who don’t work, the odds are you’re not going to achieve much in life, and that isn’t really the government’s fault – it’s down to the individuals involved.
