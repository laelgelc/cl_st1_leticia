So a few minutes after he’d made the nine-nine-nine call, everything arrived at once – police car, ambulance, and then my mum turning up. She took one look and was just horrified, absolutely horrified, while everyone else was sort of laughing. The paramedics were brilliant, though. They were looking at my hands, which were really badly burned by then, and they got me into the ambulance and off to the hospital.

At the hospital the doctor had a proper look and said it was second-degree burns. They gave me painkillers, took my rings off before everything swelled too much, and then bandaged my hands up. He warned me there might be scarring and that I might even need skin grafts later on, which was a bit terrifying to hear.

Me and Mum were both in tears by that point. She kept asking, “Why did you do this? Why did you let this happen?” and I just couldn’t answer her. I was still drunk, completely confused, and the truth was I didn’t know. I didn’t even know who had set me on fire.
