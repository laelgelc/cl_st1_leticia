Once you got outside the UK you hit this very different expectation in most European countries that your degree subject has to match very closely with the job you go into. So with, say, a language degree you couldn’t easily become an accountant or go into marketing over there. Here, by contrast, we’ve got this long tradition, going back to when most degrees were in classics or theology, of treating graduates as general trainees who can enter a wide range of careers.
