I actually ended up going to Greece in July a couple of years ago, but only because Lou had a placement out there, otherwise I’d never have chosen that time. Even though it was a dry heat, it was still just too hot and really exhausting; you just felt wiped out all the time. People at the hotel and in the shops kept saying to us, “Don’t ever come in August,” and telling us to come before October instead, because after that everything shuts down.

In Pefkohori they start closing things at the beginning to the middle of October, and all the people who run the businesses head back to Chalkidiki. It’s a shame in a way, because Pefkohori itself is such a lovely little place. It’s a small, really beautiful coastal town with this gorgeous square and loads of little mini supermarkets dotted about.

One of the things I remember most is seeing all the kids trotting down to the beach with cold beer in those free cool bags they give you. It was just this really relaxed, happy scene, everyone wandering about, the sea right there, and the kids proudly carrying the drinks like it was the most normal thing in the world.
