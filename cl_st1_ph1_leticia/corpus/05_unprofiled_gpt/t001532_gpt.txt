A lot of our prisons are absolutely ancient and really ought to be shut down. They were designed in a completely different era, when nobody was thinking about things like family support and how important that is for stopping people from reoffending. Dartmoor’s a perfect example. It was deliberately built out in the middle of nowhere, as a sort of high‑security, remote place to keep people away from everyone and everything. But now it isn’t even full of the really dangerous, high‑risk prisoners it was meant for. It’s full of people who, with the right support, could actually be rehabilitated. Yet their families can’t realistically visit them because of where it is. That whole setup just doesn’t fit with what we now know helps people not to end up back inside.
