what I'm trying to get across is that project management itself is a generic skill set, and that’s what makes it powerful. A good project manager can operate at different levels and across different sectors because the core of the role isn’t being the technical expert, it’s managing the delivery. In something like engineering, you really do need that deep technical expertise to do the actual work. But in a lot of project roles, the project manager doesn’t have to personally know how to design the bridge or write the software. Instead, they’re responsible for bringing in the right specialists, coordinating them, and making sure they deliver what’s needed. So the expertise is in managing, not in the specific technical content.
