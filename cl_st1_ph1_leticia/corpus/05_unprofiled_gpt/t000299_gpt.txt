Feminism started off as a movement that was specifically against gender roles, right, and obviously it was mostly women in it because women were the ones being oppressed by those roles. That’s kind of the point: the group that’s most directly hurt is usually the one that organises. It’s the same with civil rights movements – they’re mostly led by minority ethnic groups, not white people, even though in theory everyone could support the cause. So the fact that feminism has mostly women in it doesn’t undermine it at all, or mean they can’t fight for the same broader goals. That’s why I don’t really get what your point is here.
