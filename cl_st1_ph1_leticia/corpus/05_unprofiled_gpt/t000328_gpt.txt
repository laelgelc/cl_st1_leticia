Chomsky in the sixties basically came along and said, look, it can’t just be that kids hear stuff and copy it, because they come out with sentences they’ve never heard before, and they *don’t* say loads of things that would be logically possible but are wrong. So there has to be some kind of underlying structure in the brain already, a sort of universal grammar. Then all children really do is set these parameters – like, is the language head‑first or head‑last, can you drop the subject or not, so English “I speak” versus just “speak.”
