in that essay I was thinking about how Pericles, in his famous funeral oration, actually uses the word *Eros* to talk about loving the city, loving Athens itself. And that really reminded me of Plato, where he says you have to have *Eros* for knowledge – that desire, that kind of longing, is what drives you towards wisdom. So I was trying to connect those two uses of *Eros*, and I ended up arguing that eroticism isn’t just about sex there, it’s really at the core of a lot of the deeper aspects of Athenian life.
