It's not just central to people’s thinking, it absolutely defines their motivations. Take Assad, for instance: he’s an Alawite, which is really closer to Shia than to Sunni. In that predominantly Sunni environment, they’re effectively treated as Shia. Yet if you go to Iran, many Shia there would look at the Alawites and say they’re practically pagans, not proper Muslims at all. So you’ve got this situation where, depending on where you stand, the same group is either embraced as Shia or rejected outright. And all of these distinctions, these fault lines, go right back to the seventh century.
