There are a few fairly basic theories in careers guidance, and they’re really all about how people make decisions. One that’s been very influential, and is used a lot in careers services, is psychologically based. It assumes that once you’ve reached a certain level academically, you become more independent and can make choices grounded in your own skills, your values, and what genuinely interests you. 

That’s the ideal model, if you like, and much of our practice is built around helping students clarify those things about themselves. But you also have to be realistic and recognise that not everyone actually makes decisions on that basis. Some students will appear to be going through that reflective, individual process, but in fact their choices are driven by quite different factors, and you have to keep that in mind when you’re working with them.
