In the last ten or eleven years I was really managing the staff who actually delivered the careers service, and only saw students myself for about ten percent of the time. That’s why I think it’s probably more interesting to talk about what a careers adviser does than what a manager does. Universities can define the careers role pretty much as they wish, so it varies a lot from place to place. By the time I retired this was actually the fourth university I’d worked in, so I’d seen quite a range of different approaches.
