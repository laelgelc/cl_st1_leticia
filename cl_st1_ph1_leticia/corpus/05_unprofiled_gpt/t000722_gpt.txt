I always thought that when I retired, we'd spend our days going off on little trips, visiting churches and cathedrals and places like that. It was sort of my picture of retirement, you know, nice day trips, pottering about. But somehow it never happened. The grandchildren came along, there was always some work to do, and in the end I never really retired properly at all. This is the first time I’ve actually felt free of it all – no lessons to plan, no groups to organise, no observations, no tests or endless student tracking hanging over me. It’s a very strange feeling. Did it feel like that for you when you finished?
