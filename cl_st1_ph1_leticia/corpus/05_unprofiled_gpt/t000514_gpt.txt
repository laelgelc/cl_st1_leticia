In my day, students were generally quite conscientious, and you were known well enough that any skiving or sudden disappearance was quickly noticed. With the small-group tutorial system, if you didn’t turn up, it was immediately obvious, and missing essays stood out just as clearly. There wasn’t a strict, formal register being flung around at every session, but that didn’t mean you could really get away with not showing up. Instead, the whole system seemed to rely far more on our own sense of responsibility and self-discipline to keep us attending and working.
