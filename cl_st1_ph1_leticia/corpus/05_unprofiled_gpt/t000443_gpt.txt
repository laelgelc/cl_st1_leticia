It must be really depressing for them up there, you know, because they’re already quite isolated just by being older and a bit frail, and then the weather’s just constantly awful on top of that. It’s not like they can just pop out easily anyway, and if every time you look outside it’s grey and windy and freezing, it can’t help your mood.

When we went up there in January it was just bitter, absolutely freezing and so windy. I had loads of layers on, like properly bundled up, and then a big coat over the top, hat, scarf, everything, and I was still cold. We were out trying to take photos and you can see in the pictures how grim it was, all of us huddled up against the wind.

Hannah wouldn’t even get out of the car, she just stayed inside with the heater on and took photos of us from through the car window while we were outside trying to take pictures. That kind of weather all the time, especially when you’re old and stuck there, it’s just got to feel really miserable.
