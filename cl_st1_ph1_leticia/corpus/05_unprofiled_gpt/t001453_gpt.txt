The problem is that the pipe numbers are all handwritten, and once the coating goes on, those numbers can get lost. Then somebody has to go back and rewrite them, which is where the human error creeps in. You’re asking people, often on low wages, to copy long strings of digits accurately, over and over again, and it’s easy to make mistakes. On top of that, some of the numerals in handwriting are really similar – like ones and sevens, and nines and fours – so they get mixed up quite a lot.
