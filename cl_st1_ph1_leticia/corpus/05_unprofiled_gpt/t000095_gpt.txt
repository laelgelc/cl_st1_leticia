When I watch a lot of films, what strikes me is how scriptwriters don’t just invent everything from nothing. They seem to take certain underlying truths, things that feel real or familiar, and then transplant them into their own constructed versions of reality. They build these worlds where the details might be exaggerated or altered, but at the core there’s something recognisable. I think audiences subconsciously latch onto that. They sense that element of truth, and that’s what makes the story believable enough for them to go along with it.
