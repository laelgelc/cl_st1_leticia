I suppose because of the way my career went, doing rather unusual jobs, I never really collected many friends. I was never involved with the WRAF in the usual welfare sense, and I was quite glad of that, really, because welfare didn’t appeal to me at all. The people I came to know best were four splendid flight lieutenants, flight commanders, when I somehow found myself in charge of WRAF officer training. I was really a beginner there, coming from an intelligence background, and they kept me firmly on the rails, and we had a great deal of fun together.
