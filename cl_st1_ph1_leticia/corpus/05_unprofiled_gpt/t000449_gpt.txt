I’m not saying I know what’s best for other people, but I just don’t think all nuns genuinely enjoy that kind of life. Following strict rules every single day can’t be appealing to everyone. I imagine some of them might be doing it mostly because they believe it’ll secure them a really good place in heaven. It’s like they’re choosing a life of deliberate hardship and self-denial for that reward. And if it turns out that heaven doesn’t actually exist, then it feels to me like that whole life of ascetic sacrifice wouldn’t really have been well spent.
