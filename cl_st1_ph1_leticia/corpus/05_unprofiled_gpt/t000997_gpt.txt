they can't ever give you a really precise figure for what you'll get in the future, because all the way along they’re sticking that money on the stock market. it’s all dependent on how those investments perform over the years, so any projection is only ever a guess based on assumptions.

then, when you get to retirement, what you usually do with that pension pot is you buy an annuity. that’s basically you handing over the lump sum to an insurance company and in return they promise to pay you a fixed amount every year for the rest of your life. so your savings turn into an annual income.

but that annuity payment is fixed – it doesn’t go up with inflation. so if prices keep rising, the real value of that income slowly gets eaten away. that’s different from my pension, which is index linked, so it rises with inflation and keeps its value over time.

telling your mum to get into the council pension early on was actually very good advice. it meant she built up more years of service in the scheme, which directly improves what she’ll get out of it. and if both of us are in that same pension scheme, it just makes life a lot easier for us in the long run.
