In the mainstream school I was working with this very low‑ability group, and in that class we had two girls, one from Thailand and one from Poland. Neither of them spoke much English at all, and at first I was really worried about how on earth I was going to manage with them, because they didn’t share a language with me or with each other. But they ended up sitting together, and gradually I could see them starting to try to communicate, using whatever bits of English and gestures they had. They began helping each other, and watching that happen was just brilliant.
