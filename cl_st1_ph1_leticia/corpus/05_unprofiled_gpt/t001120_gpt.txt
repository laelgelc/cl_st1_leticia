When I first got to the bank at about nine in the morning, the very first thing we had to do was deal with the post. All the customers’ cheques would arrive then, because they’d been sent on from a central clearing office, or sometimes the head office. The cheques were all sorted there first and then posted out to the various branches, to the banks on which they were actually drawn. So every morning our branch would receive this batch of post, full of cheques, waiting for us to open and process.
