I think, though, that while influence can be as complex as you’re saying, there really are cases where someone starts off in almost total thrall to another writer. I was just reading in the New York Review about Beckett, and how his early work was basically an enslavement to Joyce. That sort of thing does happen: you get this phase of near‑worship, where the younger writer can’t get out from under the older one’s shadow, and then there’s a gradual breaking free. That pattern—adoration followed by some kind of breakthrough—does seem to be a genuine, recurring story.
