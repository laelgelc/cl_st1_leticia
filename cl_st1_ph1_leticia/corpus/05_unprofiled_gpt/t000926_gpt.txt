the difference with what I’m thinking is it’s almost the opposite way round from what Ridley Scott did. With those projects, they started with masses of footage and then, afterwards, they sifted through and carved out this incredibly powerful narrative from that big pile of “days in the life”.

What I’m imagining is starting with one really strong, high‑quality “day in the life of” – something that already has some depth and coherence – and then letting that be the seed. From there it grows outward as other people who are connected to that person add their own days, their own conversations, their own goals and needs.

So instead of beginning with quantity and trying to find quality inside it, you begin with quality and let the quantity build around it organically. Ideally, as it scales up, you still keep that quality, and in the process you start to see much more clearly what kinds of resources people actually need.
