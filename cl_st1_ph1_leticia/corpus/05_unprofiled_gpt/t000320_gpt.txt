well I do do a tiny bit of planning but it’s really loose. I’ve got the PDFs of *English File Upper Intermediate* and I use that as a kind of flexible resource. At the beginning, with this particular student, we actually followed the book quite closely because that’s what she wanted, but over time we’ve moved away from that. Now I mostly just dip into it for topic ideas or a text or an exercise if I need one, and otherwise I just follow her lead and see what she wants to talk about or work on that day.

The main structure comes from this A4 sheet I use every lesson. I divide it into three sections. One section is for error corrections that I want to come back to at the end, so I don’t have to interrupt her flow all the time. Another section is for new vocabulary, often idioms, and I write the meanings there so it’s all in one place for her. The third section is for pronunciation notes.

With pronunciation, I write it in whatever way makes sense to that particular student. Some students prefer the proper phonetic symbols, so I’ll use the correct phones or whatever. Others don’t like phonetics at all, so I just write it how they’ll remember and understand it. At the end of the lesson they take that sheet away with them, and that’s basically their record of what we’ve done.

During the lesson I’m just doing on-the-spot correction if it feels appropriate, and if it doesn’t, I just note it for later on the sheet. It’s really simple, and honestly, it’s easy money.
