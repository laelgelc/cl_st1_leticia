Before I’d even gone back to France, before anyone had asked me, my mum had already promised our long‑term tenant that I’d basically be their kids’ little school adviser. She’d agreed I’d help them out with school in France, tutor them, explain how everything works, like some sort of built‑in guide. And I’m just there going, no. I don’t have the time, and I don’t want to do it. When I moved, no one was holding my hand or sorting everything for me; I just got on with it. I’m not spending my whole summer after finishing a year of uni traipsing over to the neighbours’ for hours every day with a ten‑year‑old. I’ve got my own life, and I’m not sacrificing it for something I never agreed to.
