We were walking round the school while they were going through all these criteria we didn’t meet, and they took us into different classrooms. We went into one of the higher-level science rooms, where they had this big project set up, and my one was straight in there, really interested, asking questions, getting involved. You could see he was good at that sort of thing, the maths and science side of it. Then we went into one of the top-year classrooms, and he just picked up this book off a desk and said, “Oh, I read that last week,” like it was nothing. It really showed how bright he was and how familiar he already was with the work.
