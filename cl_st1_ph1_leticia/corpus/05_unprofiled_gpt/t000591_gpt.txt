it was only like two or three months before they actually evacuated the whole town, about a hundred thousand people, because of the volcano risk, and we’d already done that bus journey out of there. The whole thing was just horrible – the ground was this unstable grey silt, so the road didn’t feel solid at all, and the bus itself was really crappy. Then on this really sharp bend, with about a hundred‑metre drop right next to us, one of the tyres actually started to come off. You could see the locals getting properly anxious, and that’s when you really know it’s bad, when they’re scared too. We were all just sitting there thinking, if we go over, that’s it, we’re dead, just hoping we’d somehow make it round and get off alive.
