Well, the way I see it, if you’re in a job and you’re unhappy, and you’re not getting the opportunities you’re clearly after, then something’s gone wrong somewhere. Either there’s a problem on your side, or there’s a problem on the employer’s side, and the two of you just can’t find that workable middle ground. And in the case I’m thinking of, I’d put a lot of that down to bad management. They were stuck in this really cold, hard, old-boy, old-school way of doing things, whereas I’ve always thought of “modern management” as something quite different, more constructive and balanced.
