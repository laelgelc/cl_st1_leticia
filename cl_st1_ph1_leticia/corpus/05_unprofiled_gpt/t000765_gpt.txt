I worked in OA – outdoor adventure – and we had about ten to twelve different activities running for the kids, who were mostly between eight and fifteen. We split the day into six periods, and there were seven of us on staff, so we’d work either on our own or in pairs depending on the activity. The children came round in their bunk groups, so each group would come to us for a session and do that particular activity once. They only repeated something if they specifically chose to. As staff we didn’t stick to one thing; we just led whatever activity was on the schedule for the children at that particular time.
