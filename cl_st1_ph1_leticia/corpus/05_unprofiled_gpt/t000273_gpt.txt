I worked at Swan, the girls’ school, for two years, and I used to get involved in all sorts of mad things there. Once they had this blind date competition, and I turned up dressed as a woman. I’d stuck three balloons down my front, had my wellies on, an apron, my hair all done up, and I carried a mop. The girls absolutely went wild when they saw me like that. Later on, though, they all started chasing me round with their compasses, trying to pop the balloons. That was really frightening, and it made me think how scary girls can be together, even worse than boys.
