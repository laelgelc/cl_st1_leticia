it’s right on the eastern edge of Snowdonia, this tiny village where my father’s family have been for hundreds of years. The name is something like “St Michael’s in the valley,” very small and tucked away. I went back there recently to see if I could find where my ancestors actually lived, and I managed to track down some of the farms they would have worked on. There’s this little church by the river, dating from the twelfth or thirteenth century. Inside, I found this extraordinary statement saying that my great-grandfather was born there in 1821, which was quite a discovery.
