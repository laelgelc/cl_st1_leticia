He should be paying under a letter of credit. Once the pipes arrive from Turkey and all the documents are in place – proof they’ve arrived, insurance details, certificate of origin, and acceptance – the bank forces payment. Normally that’s eighty-five percent of the four million pounds. At that point, he can literally see his pipes at the docks, with his name on them, all ready for him. That eighty-five percent is what pays the supplier and pays the docks to release them with the VAT. Then, once they’re onsite and laid, you get the thirty days’ credit and they pay the final fifteen percent. Usually, if they’re going to query anything, it’s that last fifteen percent.
