So I’d then move on to say there are lots of different varieties of English, not just internationally but even within Britain. That raises the key question for international business: whose English are we actually supposed to use? 

My own view is that different accents are absolutely fine, as long as you speak clearly and you tone down very strong regional features or dialect. I’d use my north‑east background as an example: there are things I just wouldn’t say in a business context, like a very dialectal phrase I might use at home. 

Not everybody speaks Queen’s English, and we shouldn’t expect them to. The point is intelligibility, not trying to erase people’s accents.
