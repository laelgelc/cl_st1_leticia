yeah so at MineCon in Las Vegas the people who were televising it all had these “occupy Minecraft” T-shirts on, and they were talking about how to bring the whole Occupy movement into Minecraft. That really showed me that the Minecraft community isn’t sealed off; it’s actually really connected to what’s going on in the real world. 

Like, there was this huge “RIP Steve Jobs” sign someone had built, and they’d done this giant iPod as well. So you could see how news from outside, like Steve Jobs’ death or Occupy Wall Street, actually comes into the Minecraft universe and becomes part of what people build and talk about. 

That’s why I started thinking about cyber provincialism in that way – not as people completely shutting themselves off, but as this idea that you can use Minecraft to get a community to look at real-world events and different cultures, while still being in this sort of separate, game-based “province” online.
