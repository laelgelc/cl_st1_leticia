I remember it just got worse over the day. At first I was just being really sick, properly throwing up, and then the diarrhoea started and there was blood in it. By the next day there was literally nothing left to come out, but I still had all the cramps and the pain, and I just felt awful. I ended up ringing my mum, like, “What do I do?” because I didn’t really know how serious it was, and she was like, “Go to the hospital, don’t mess around with that.”

So I went in, and the doctor there said it was probably some kind of bacterial infection. They gave me antibiotics, and once I started taking them it actually cleared up pretty quickly, which was such a relief. The whole thing cost about seventy soles in the end, including seeing the doctor and the prescription. In absolute terms it wasn’t loads of money, but over there money goes so much further than it does here in England, so it felt like quite a lot to hand over. It really made me aware of how expensive getting ill can feel when you’re living somewhere like Peru.
