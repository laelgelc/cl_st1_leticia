They're all out of copyright, so anyone can publish them, which is why you see so many different editions of the classics everywhere. What really bothers me is how U.S. copyright law works – it’s kind of insidious. Big corporations just keep pushing legislators to extend the term over and over, so things never properly fall into the public domain. In other countries it’s more straightforward: it’s the artist’s life plus a few years. But in the States, in 2015, the newest music that’s actually in the public domain is only from about 1920 to 1928.
