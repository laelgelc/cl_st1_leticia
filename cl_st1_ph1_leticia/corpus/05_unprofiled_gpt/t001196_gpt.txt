I suppose what really struck me was how special it all feels to us now, just seeing those planes, getting to go in them or even fly in them. It’s this big “wow” experience, something you tell people about afterwards. But then I was talking to my aunt’s sister, she’s eighty‑eight now, and she’d actually worked on those planes in the factory during the war. For her, it wasn’t some extraordinary treat, it was simply everyday life. That really brought home how easily we forget that side of it, that ordinary people were just working on them.
