I think the Chinese government handled that democracy movement in Hong Kong quite cleverly, really. While the whole world was watching they didn’t go in with a big heavy crackdown, they just sort of held back. Then, afterwards, once the attention had died down a bit, they brought in restrictions so that kind of thing couldn’t happen again, or at least not so easily. And as for working there, it’s impossible at my age. They’ve got that age sixty‑five rule, same as in mainland China, so that shuts the door.
