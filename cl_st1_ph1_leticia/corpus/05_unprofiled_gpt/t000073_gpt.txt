Instead, they were just preyed on by the boys, so the one place that might have been a refuge turned into a nightmare as well.

And then, looking at some of the families he was dealing with, particularly the lower‑status Sunni Pakistani ones, there was this whole background to it. Many of them were originally peasant farmers who’d been displaced and then sort of dumped here about thirty years ago. They had no real tradition of education or formal work in the first place. Now you’ve got these second‑ and third‑generation communities where that pattern’s carried on. The parents never had education, never had that kind of work, and so neither they nor the kids see any need for school. It’s just not part of their world.
