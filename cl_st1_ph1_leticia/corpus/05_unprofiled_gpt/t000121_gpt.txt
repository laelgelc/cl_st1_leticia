I was really just trying to make the point that he’s got himself into this very particular niche, this specific product area, and that’s where he’ll probably stay. As long as governments and the environmental agenda keep throwing money at that sort of thing, the company will keep needing people like him, even if he finds the work a bit dull. From the firm’s point of view, he’s selling exactly the kind of product they can market globally, so he’s safe.

For me, that’s not how I’m wired at all. Doing the same job, in the same narrow field, year after year would drive me mad unless I was absolutely passionate about it. I can’t see myself sticking at one tightly defined role for decades. I’d probably manage about five years before I’d be itching to move on and try something different.

That’s partly down to what one of my lecturers at university said. He always told us not to stay in any one job for more than five years. That stuck with me, and I suppose it’s shaped how I think about careers and not getting stuck in a rut.
