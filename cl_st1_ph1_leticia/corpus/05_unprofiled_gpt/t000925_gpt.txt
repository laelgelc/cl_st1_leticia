Do you remember those filmmaking projects back in the nineties, I think Ridley Scott was involved, where they got people all around the UK and then eventually around the world to film a “day in the life”? Everyone just shot their own normal day and sent the footage in, and then there was this huge process of sifting through all that material. From that mass of everyday clips they constructed a really strong, powerful narrative, just by carefully selecting and arranging what people had submitted. It was all about collecting quantity first – loads and loads of people’s days – and then editing it down into something coherent and meaningful. I’m thinking about that model of gathering raw lived experience and then shaping it into a story that really resonates.
