Some of the student loans that were taken out about ten years ago have actually been sold off to private companies now, so instead of dealing with the old government loan body, people suddenly find themselves dealing with these firms. And because it’s in their hands, they can just change the rules about how and when you repay. That’s what really worries me: if my own loan gets sold on, they could suddenly turn around and say I’ve got to pay it all back straightaway. I don’t think that should be allowed at all. At eighteen you just sign these agreements, you don’t really understand them, and you don’t have any real choice.
