he used to get all these presents at Christmas you know, people would turn up with turkeys and things like that, because he’d helped them out during the year. a lot of the time he’d do the work for free, or for hardly anything, especially if they didn’t have much money. it wasn’t just the accounts, either – he’d help them sort out their finances, get back on their feet, that kind of thing, and they were really grateful.

even now, when your grandad goes back over there, people still come up to him in the street and say, “oh, your father helped me so much,” or “he got me a job,” and they’ll tell him all these stories about what he did for them. he never made a big fuss about it himself, he just quietly did a lot of good through his work, very humble, didn’t go around bragging.

so he wasn’t just a good accountant, he was a good man, and people still remember him for that. it’s nice, really, to know that kind of thing runs in the family, that using your talents to help people is just sort of what we do.
