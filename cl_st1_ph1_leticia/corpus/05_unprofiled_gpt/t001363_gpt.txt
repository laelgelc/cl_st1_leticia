Since uni I’ve definitely got a lot more fearless about things like that. Before, I’d sit there agonising over every word of an email or application, rewriting and deleting and getting really nervous. This time I just thought, “Right, I’m going to go for it,” and I fired off a load of emails to people, asking about freelance checking and proofreading work. I even emailed Macmillan – you know, the big ELT publisher – without spending ages overthinking every sentence.

And then, after I’d sent one of them, I looked back and realised I’d actually written “Macmilla” instead of “Macmillan” in the message. I literally missed the N off the end. It’s so typical: I finally decide to be bold and just get on with it, and then I manage to misspell the name of the company. And when you’re applying for a proofreading job, trying to sell your proofreading skills, and you’ve done that in the email, it’s just so ridiculous. I was sitting there thinking, “Yeah, I’m good… honestly,” while staring at “Macmilla” in my sent folder.
