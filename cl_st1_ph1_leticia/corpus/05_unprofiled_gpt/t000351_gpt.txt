In the First World War, the medics discovered something really extraordinary with the badly wounded soldiers. The ones whose wounds had become infested with maggots actually ended up with much cleaner, better-healed injuries. What they realised was that the maggots were eating all the germs and bacteria in the wounds. Instead of making things worse, the maggots were effectively cleaning the wound out for them. By consuming the infected material, they left behind cleaner, healthier tissue, which then had a much better chance to heal properly.
