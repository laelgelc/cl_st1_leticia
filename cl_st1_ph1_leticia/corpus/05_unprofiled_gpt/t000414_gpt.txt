I just think universities have got it totally the wrong way round. They tend to shove the weakest, most limited lecturers in front of first‑year students, when actually that’s when you need the very best people. Those first encounters should be with really excellent, broad‑minded lecturers who can communicate to anyone, whether it’s ten people or two hundred.

For me, a good lecturer should be able to stand there and just give, regardless of how many students are in the room. It’s a bit like a DJ: a proper DJ doesn’t go, “Oh, there are only fifteen people here, I won’t bother.” They still put everything into it, they still try to create something exciting and generous for whoever’s turned up.

But a lot of lecturers just don’t seem able to be generous with what they know. It’s as if something in them is blocked. Their knowledge stays locked inside their heads rather than flowing outwards. And that’s such a waste, because students arrive at university expecting to be excited, and they’re met with people who can’t or won’t really share what they’ve got.
