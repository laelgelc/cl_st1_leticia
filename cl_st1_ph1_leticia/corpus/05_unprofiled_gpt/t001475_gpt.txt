They’re not so hung up on moral rules and dos and don’ts, they’re more about this inner mystical experience, trying to find God within yourself rather than somewhere out there. And if you look at some of the later Christian mystics in Germany, they were actually influenced by Eastern religions. You can see it in the way they started to reinterpret things. So instead of taking the Holy Spirit or the Holy Trinity literally, they treated them more metaphorically, as symbols of different aspects within the self, rather than separate beings floating around in the sky.
