It all got quite complicated for me because I’d been having assisted conception. They did this test where they inject a coloured fluid into your womb and then track it on an ultrasound, to see if there’s a blockage in the fallopian tubes stopping the eggs travelling down. Later on, when they operated, they found little drifts of cancer cells up my fallopian tubes, in one ovary, and around the outside of my uterus. Looking back, I now think that flushing process may have pushed endometrial cells through the tubes and out into my abdominal cavity, effectively spreading them around without anyone realising at the time.
