And she just wouldn’t let it go, you know. She carried that hatred around for years and years, and I kept saying to her, “Mum, you’ve got to let it go, it’s not doing you any good.” I meant in terms of her health, her mind, the way she was with people. It coloured everything, really. But she just held on to it, all that anger and hurt, far too long. 

When I was flying back, knowing she was dying, I decided I had to do something to get myself through the journey. So I booked a massage before I flew, just to calm myself down and try to relax a bit. I didn’t know what I was going back to, how long it would all take, so I thought, right, at least I can go into it a bit more relaxed.
