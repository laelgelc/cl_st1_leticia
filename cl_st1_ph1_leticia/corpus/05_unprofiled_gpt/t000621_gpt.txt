Well, I suppose really it was just common sense, wasn’t it, not putting your head out of the train window. You just didn’t do it. I can remember one particular journey when I was about seven, going on a steam train down to Dover. Then we went on the ship over to France, and it was full of army personnel and their wives, all going across. 

We were absolutely crammed into this tiny little cabin, my mother and I, hardly any room to move. I remember a steward coming along and bringing my mother a cup of tea, and when she got it, it was stone cold. I can still picture it, and she really didn’t like that at all. 

It makes me wonder, what did you have to eat as a child during the war years? Would you have had things like apple crumble when the apples were in season, or was that not really something you had then?
