yeah it was something that basically meant affordable housing, that was the idea of it, but I still can’t remember the exact term they used. When we were working on that riverside bridge, you could really see the difference. The nice riverside places, the ones they were going to sell for a decent amount of money, they had proper good materials, looked really smart, you could tell they’d spent money on them. Then the cheaper ones, the ones that were part of the planning permission, they just looked awful in comparison, really poor materials, a bit cheap and nasty. I remember someone on site actually saying to me, you know, “Don’t even think about buying one of those cheaper ones,” just because they were so badly done. It was really obvious they were cutting corners on those.
