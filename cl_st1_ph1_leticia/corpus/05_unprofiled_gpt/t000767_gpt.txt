At the start of the day we’d all just stand around and sort it out between us – who was getting which off‑periods, who was taking which kids – and then we’d write it all up on the whiteboard on the shed. There wasn’t some massive formal rota, it was more like, “Right, I’ll take these ones then,” and someone else would grab another group, and it just kind of worked. I really liked it, actually, because everyone got on and there weren’t any arguments about who had which children or who got what time off. It felt really relaxed but still organised enough that the day ran smoothly.
