oh yes, he rang just then – it was about those luggage labels. They’ve sent us ones that say “outward bound” on one side, you know, the proper Saga ones, so I was talking him through what to do with them. I said there was no letter in with them at all about the cabin upgrade – nothing confirming it, nothing about any extra cost or anything like that. Just the labels and the tickets, no explanation.

I was saying to him it actually makes sense for them, doesn’t it? They upgrade us, stick us in a better cabin, and then they can resell our old one to someone else. So they’re not really losing out by giving us the upgrade, they’re making a bit more money on the side. But it would’ve been nice to have it in writing, just to know exactly what’s what, especially about whether we’re meant to be paying anything extra.

Anyway, I told him the journey down to London was really good. The roads were very good all the way, and the weather actually improved as we went along – started off not so great, then brightened up nicely. By the time we got near London it was pretty decent. It was busy round Parliament and Downing Street, lots of people about, crowds everywhere in that bit, but apart from that it was fine. No real problems on the road at all.

I said to him, just put the labels on the suitcase now, and we’ll fill in the cabin number later once we’re absolutely sure what it is. No point fussing when they haven’t even bothered to put a proper letter in. The labels can go on, and we can sort the details when we’ve got them.

Oh, and I mentioned we’d done a little shop in Lidl – just a small one, picked up a few bits while we were out. So that’s all sorted as well.
