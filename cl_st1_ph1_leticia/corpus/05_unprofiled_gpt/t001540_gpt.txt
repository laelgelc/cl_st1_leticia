Some of them do go abroad, yeah, and that’s starting to open their minds up a bit. The more they travel, the more their perspective broadens, you can see it changing. But it’s slow compared to here. England changes really slowly in that way, doesn’t it?

Over there, the physical environment’s completely different. If a building is over forty years old, they just knock it down. It’s routine. They don’t really preserve anything, so literally nothing is older than forty years. That’s just normal to them.

Whereas here, the houses I grew up around are still standing, and there’s that church from when I was a kid – it’s like six hundred years old and it’s just there, part of everyday life. When I tell them that, they can’t really believe it, it doesn’t compute for them.
