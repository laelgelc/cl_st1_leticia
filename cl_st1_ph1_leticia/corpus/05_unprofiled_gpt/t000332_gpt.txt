In English, we really depend on the word order — subject, verb, object — to show who’s doing what to whom. So if I say “the monkey kissed the lion,” you know straightaway that the monkey is the one doing the kissing and the lion is the one being kissed, because of that order. But in some other languages, they don’t rely on that fixed order in the same way. Instead, they add little bits onto the words themselves — extra endings or morphemes — that actually mark which one is the doer and which one is the receiver. Because of that, they can move the words around in the sentence and it still means the same thing. So English works one way, and other languages work differently in that respect.
