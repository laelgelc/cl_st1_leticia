When you’re watching it, you can’t help constantly putting yourself in their position, even though you know it’s fantasy. It’s really powerful because it’s such a strong critique of how obsessed we are with reality TV. Everyone in the story is just glued to these massive screens, compulsively watching these kids kill each other. And you get the sense that if people just stopped watching, the whole thing could collapse, the violence could actually end. But there’s this really sick fascination that keeps them tuning in, and it really makes you wonder how far we actually are from something like that in real life.
