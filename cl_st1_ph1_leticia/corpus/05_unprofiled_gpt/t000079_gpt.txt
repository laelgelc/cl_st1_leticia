I just think with a lot of teachers there’s this attitude that they’re somehow owed a living. You hear them moaning about having to work a bit longer before they get their pensions, as if it’s some terrible injustice. And I’m sitting there thinking, hang on, in business people are routinely working longer hours, with far fewer benefits and much less security, and they just get on with it.

Teachers already get these really long holidays, regular hours, all of that structure. From what I’ve seen, having worked in both business and schools, teaching is actually not a bad deal at all. I’m not saying it’s easy, but compared with what a lot of people in the private sector put up with, it’s pretty cushy.

And then on top of that, some of them never really grow up. They’re supposed to be the adults, setting an example, but they’re still behaving like schoolkids themselves, just in staffroom form.
