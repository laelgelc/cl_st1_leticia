Samuel Pepys left his whole library to a member of his family, and then that person gave it to Magdalene College in Cambridge. In that library were his famous diaries, which he’d written in a special code. Someone became fascinated by this code and decided to try to crack it. Because Pepys had written very honestly, and sometimes not very nicely about people, it could have caused a lot of trouble at the time if it had been easily read. So decoding it became that person’s lifetime work.
