There are published resources for anything really, that’s the thing. When our school moved, they decided to kit us out and we were given this big whiteboard resource package. It was one of those widely used ones that everyone raves about – “oh it’s wonderful, it’s got everything ready-made, lesson plans, materials, all done for you.” On paper it sounded marvellous, because you think, well, that’ll save so much time and effort.

But when it came down to actually using it with my pupils it was completely useless. The reading level of the texts was fine – they were pitched at about five- to six-year-olds, which is where my kids were in terms of reading ability. The problem was the content. You just couldn’t use it with fifteen- and sixteen-year-olds; it was totally inappropriate for them. So even though it was supposedly this fantastic, widely used package, in reality it was no good at all for our particular group.
