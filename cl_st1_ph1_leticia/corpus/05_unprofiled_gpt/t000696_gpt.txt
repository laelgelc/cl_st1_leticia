So on my way to work, just after that roundabout I was talking about, there’s this horrible bit where the road suddenly narrows right down to basically a single car width, like a funnel, and then after the pedestrian crossing it opens out again. Because of that, most drivers actually tend to hang back when they see a bike going into it, and they let you go through first. Then once you’re past the crossing and the road widens again, they’ll overtake you there instead, which is much safer and kind of how it’s meant to work really.
