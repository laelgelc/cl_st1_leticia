Well, the cake decorating really started as a bit of a sideline. I do the sugar craft, the sugar flower work, and at first I didn’t particularly need another qualification, but the college said they’d pay the fees if I did the course, just to make my role look a bit more professional. So I thought, well, why not, and I signed up as a student as well as teaching there.

What’s been getting to me, though, is the other cake decorating teacher. She’s very old‑fashioned, absolutely rigid in the way she does things. Everything has to be done exactly her way, in the order she lays down, and she won’t really listen if students struggle or need to do it differently. She doesn’t adapt to their skills or pace at all, it’s like one size fits all and that’s that.

She also refuses to provide proper resources or adjust the work, even when it’s obvious people need a bit more support. It’s especially stark when you compare it with what I’m doing in my own classes, particularly with the special needs students. I try to be flexible, to meet them where they are, break things down, change the task if it’s not appropriate, that sort of thing. For me it’s all about what the student needs and what’s realistic for them.

So sitting there as a student in her class, knowing how I work in mine, it really highlights the contrast. It’s frustrating, because it doesn’t have to be that way.
