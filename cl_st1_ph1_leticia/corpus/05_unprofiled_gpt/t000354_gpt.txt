So Psalm ninety‑one says, you know, if you truly trust in God, in his word and in his character, then you really don’t need to be afraid, because he promises to protect you from harm and danger. It’s all there, that sense of him covering you, keeping you safe, and that fear doesn’t come from God at all. In theory, if I actually believed that properly, the fear should just go, because his protection is meant to be real, not just some nice idea.

But I still struggle with it so much in everyday life. I keep telling myself those verses, but then I get scared anyway, even with really ordinary things. Like, I can be properly frightened just going into the kitchen if I think there might be a spider there. It sounds silly when I say it out loud, especially next to all those big promises about angels and protection and not needing to fear the terror of the night and all that, but it’s true. I still jump and panic and feel my heart racing.

So I’m stuck in this weird place where I know what Psalm ninety‑one says, and I do try to cling to it, but I haven’t managed to live it out fully. The fear is still there, and it makes everything feel really stressful at the moment.
