well the thing is with the A fourteen they’ve been talking about doing something to it for years and years, it’s always been on the cards, but every time it gets to the point where they might actually do it someone kicks up a fuss and it all gets knocked back again. and it’s not even like it’s going through some huge built‑up area, it’s mostly out in the middle of nowhere, but you still get the protests and the objections and so on and then it all stalls.

and they don’t even need to do anything that dramatic to make a big difference, really. if they just took out all those nasty at‑grade junctions and replaced them with proper grade‑separated ones, flyovers and that, it would help an awful lot. just tidying it up like that would make it flow so much better without having to rebuild the whole thing from scratch.

but to actually push it through, especially with the locals and the environmental lot objecting, they’re going to have to find some extra money from somewhere, and that’s where I can see it going the same way as the others. some private company comes in, funds it, then operates and maintains it afterwards, probably on some P F I type deal. that’s how they’ll sell it, I reckon – “no extra cost to the taxpayer” and all that.

and knowing my lot, it’ll probably end up being people I work with who sit down and dream up exactly how to fund it and run it, working out the contracts and the tolls and the maintenance and everything.
