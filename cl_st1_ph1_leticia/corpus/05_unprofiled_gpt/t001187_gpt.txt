I just kept going from one person with a clipboard to another, saying, you know, “He’s Year Nine, he’s supposed to be running,” and every time they’d look down the list and his name wasn’t there, his school wasn’t there, nothing. 

In the end one of them said, “Well, we’ll put him down for the one o’clock,” so I thought, right, okay, we’re in, and they told us to come back about quarter past two. It was still pouring, we were already soaked, so I thought, I’m not walking the course in this, and we went off, moved the car a bit closer so at least we weren’t miles away.

We came back later to get his number, you know, the race bib, and it all started again. They’re looking at the list: “What school?” I tell them, and they say, “No, that school’s not on here at all.” Then somebody points out we’re actually at the wrong tent, wrong team altogether. I’d been standing there all that time under my umbrella, queuing and apologising, and we weren’t even with the right lot.

I could just see the bottom of the sign, the “S” at the bottom and “shire” at the top, but the middle bit was hidden by the tent, so I’d assumed it was ours. I ended up saying, “I’m so sorry, we’re in the wrong place,” and then being marched across the mud again to the people we should’ve been talking to in the first place, still apologising because by then it was about twenty past two.
