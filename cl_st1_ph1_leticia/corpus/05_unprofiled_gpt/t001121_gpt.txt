after we’d opened the post in the morning, all the cheques that had come in for our branch had to be dealt with. We’d start by sorting them into different compartments according to the customers’ surnames, so everything was in order. Then we had to “post” them, which meant entering every single cheque into the system using a double-entry method. For every debit there had to be a corresponding credit somewhere else. At the end of the day, the total debits had to match the total credits exactly, and nobody was allowed to go home until it all balanced.
