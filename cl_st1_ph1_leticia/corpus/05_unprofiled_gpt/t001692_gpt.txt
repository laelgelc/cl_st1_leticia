yeah well when I was an apprentice I worked out this little shortcut, like a jig, a special tool for doing those bearing housings. Instead of doing it the long, standard way, I could just fly through them, turn them out dead quick. Because of that jig I was making loads of parts, so the bonus money really piled up, especially when you think what the basic hourly rate was back then.

I had to hide the tool every night so no one else would twig what I was doing. Me and my cousin were in engineering together, and we were knocking so much work out we could stash some of it away. Then we’d slope off fishing while the foreman did us a favour and clocked us in and out. We made a lot of money that way. You couldn’t get away with anything like that now.
