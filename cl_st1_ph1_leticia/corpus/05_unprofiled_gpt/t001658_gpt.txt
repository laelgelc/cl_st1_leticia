I think a lot of what was going on in my head came from what I’d seen and heard over the years. My mother had had a really, really grim menopause, and other women I knew had awful symptoms too, so I had this picture that menopause could be extremely bad and still be “normal”. On top of that, I’d read and heard things about how menopause was supposed to be, and how doctors often downplayed women’s reports of bleeding.  

So when I started having heavy bleeding myself, I just slotted it into that framework. I assumed it must be within the normal bounds, however unpleasant, and I became very wary of making a fuss. I kept thinking that if I went to the doctor and said, “This is really bad,” they’d roll their eyes and think, “Oh yes, you and a thousand others,” and see me as overreacting.  

I worried I’d be seen as whinging if I complained too much, so I second-guessed myself and tried to convince myself that what I was experiencing was probably normal and not worth bothering them about.
