Yeah, the whole point of it is to see how your brain deals with language structure when you’ve got absolutely nothing to go on, no roots, no Latin or Germanic link, nothing you could have studied before. It’s literally just: can your brain pick up patterns and make sense of it on the spot. And it’s just pass or fail, there’s no grading or anything like that. Some people who are really highly educated, degrees and all that, completely bomb it. But then there was this bloke who only had GCSEs, and he’d just sat and taught himself eleven languages in his spare time – seven of them he speaks fluently – and he’s the one who’s just passed it.
