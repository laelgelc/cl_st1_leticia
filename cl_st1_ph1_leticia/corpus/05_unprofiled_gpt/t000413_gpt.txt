They make themselves inaccessible before the students even get into the lecture theatre, and that’s when they lose them. They might be absolutely brilliant, but if they’re shut off, their ideas just don’t get through. So the students are sitting there going, “well, I don’t get this, I’m not interested,” and of course they’re on Facebook. But I’ve seen it the other way round as well: if somebody speaks in a genuinely interesting way, you literally see the phones and laptops go down. People lean in, they listen. They come to university expecting that kind of excitement, not to be bored senseless, and they respond the moment someone actually gives it to them.
