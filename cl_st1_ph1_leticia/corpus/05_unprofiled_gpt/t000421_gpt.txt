I just find it amazing that so many of those people are still alive, given what they went through. Some of them somehow managed to come back and build really happy, fulfilled lives after experiencing absolutely horrific things in the war. 

But when you look back at the First World War, it was very different. A lot of those veterans never spoke about what they’d seen, because they just couldn’t cope with it, and the people around them didn’t really understand what was going on. Nobody had a proper idea of post‑traumatic stress then, or how deeply those experiences could affect someone.

So many of those men ended up in hospitals, but without any real treatment for what they were actually suffering from. They were just kind of left there, and that’s very sad.
