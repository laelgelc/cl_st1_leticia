I’d actually rather nobody used the expression “you know”. From a teaching point of view, if a student asked me what it means, I’d struggle to give a clear explanation, because it doesn’t really seem to do very much. It’s classed as a discourse marker, so it’s more about structuring what you’re saying than adding content. You could see it as a shortened version of “do you know what I mean?”, and in that sense it behaves a bit like a tag question at the end of a sentence.
