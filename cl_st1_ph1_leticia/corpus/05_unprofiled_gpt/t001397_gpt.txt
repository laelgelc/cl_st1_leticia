I’d actually gone along in my own black shift dress, because that’s the kind of thing that makes me happiest, really simple and straight, and I wanted her to see straightaway what I like. So when the personal shopper started bringing things in, the very first one she produced was this black dress that just fitted perfectly. It was exactly right on me, and of course I loved it.  

Then she started introducing colour, and one of them was this teal blue dress. It did suit me, I could see that, but it just didn’t feel like “me” in the same way. I’m so used to black that anything else feels a bit odd. At that point I was told, quite firmly, that I couldn’t wear black as the mother of the bride, so that perfect black dress had to be put to one side. I more or less earmarked it in my head as a separate little black dress to buy anyway, because it was just so good.  

After that she brought out a red dress. I can wear red, I’m fine with red normally, but this one just didn’t do anything for me at all, and I kept finding myself wanting to go back to that black dress.
