I had school dinners for ages, and the way they did it was really grim. They used to bring the food out in these big metal dishes, and you’d all be sat at a table with loads of other kids. The oldest kid on the table was in charge of serving everyone else, so they’d go round asking what you wanted. The mashed potato was the worst bit – it came sort of pre‑shaped, and they’d scoop it out like ice cream, these horrible scoops of mash. It really stuck with me, that whole system of being served like that.
