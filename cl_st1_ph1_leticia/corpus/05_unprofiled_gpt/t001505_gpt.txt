The trouble for the overseas students was that the government kept changing the regulations about their legal right to work in the UK, so there was this constant instability. Most of them desperately wanted to stay on and work here, and they were very optimistic when the post-study work visa came in, because it seemed to promise two years of work after their degree. But of course, that legal entitlement in itself didn’t make employers want to take them on. From the employers’ point of view, they’re generally recruiting graduates as a long‑term investment. They don’t really want somebody they know is going to have to leave after two years, so there was a real mismatch between what the students hoped for and what the labour market actually offered them.
