A lot of academics really do work very hard to give students feedback, and they care about it. But the thing is, if feedback is pages and pages long, students just get overwhelmed and don’t take it in. So the trick is making it really succinct and focused, otherwise it just becomes noise and they ignore it.

I actually did some research on this using metaphor cards around the idea of “evaluation.” What came out of it was fascinating. These academics, who don’t have backgrounds in marketing or accounting at all, were suddenly talking about evaluation in those kinds of terms – marketing, accountability, performance measures, that sort of thing.

And that’s not because they’ve secretly become marketers or accountants; it’s because this management discourse is so pervasive now that it’s started to shape the way they think and talk about their own work. They’ve basically absorbed the language and the frames without ever having set out to do that in the first place.
