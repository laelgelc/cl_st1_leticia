We set off at about three in the morning, it was absolutely freezing, and we were starting at something like two and a half thousand metres and going up to about four and a half. Because it was that weird twilight, you know, just before it gets properly light, the path was really indistinct, you could barely see where you were putting your feet.

At one point we saw this animal on the track ahead of us, and we just couldn’t make out what it was. It might’ve been a leopard, might’ve been a big dog, we honestly couldn’t tell, and that just put us completely on edge. Once you’ve seen something like that in that kind of half-light, your brain just goes into overdrive.

From then on I was really uneasy, just that feeling in the back of your mind that there’s something out there watching you while you’re trudging up this mountain in the dark. It made the whole ascent feel a lot longer and tenser than it probably was.
