I do see what you’re saying, and I’m not against using kinder, less judgmental language. But I still feel that, with depression specifically, changing the terminology is a very minor, surface-level tweak. The real issue for me is the stigma itself and the lack of really effective treatments for people who are suffering. We’ve been using words like “depression” and “patient” for so long that just rebranding them feels unlikely to produce much real change compared with tackling stigma and improving actual care.
