So once you’ve edited the material, it actually turns into a narrative case study. You’ve got clear characters, who they are, what their professions are, and a specific problem they’re facing. Then we map out the possible actions they can take, which often hinge on their relationships with others. 

For example, you might have the director of “Friends of Science” who is also a leading chiropractic academic. That person becomes a character in the case study, with their dual roles, the particular issue they’re dealing with, and the different paths they might choose in response.
