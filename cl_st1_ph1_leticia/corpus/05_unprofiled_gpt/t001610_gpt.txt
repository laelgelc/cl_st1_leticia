It's more like debates than proper rows, really, because everyone’s got such different views. It’s not like people storming out or anything, it’s just that as soon as you add more family, you get more of these clashes.

This time it was just me, Dad and my brother who went down – Mum stayed at home with the dog – to see Dad’s sister and her husband. His brother was there too, with his Hungarian partner. He’s such a character: dropped out of school, bit of a hippy, got his hair in braids now, and he’s living over in America.

Politically it’s all over the place. Dad’s kind of left, and I’m further left than he is. My aunt is completely right-wing, and so is this other relative. So you can imagine, once you get us all in the same room, it turns into these big debates. It’s not that I don’t like it, it’s just that I know exactly where everyone stands and it’s never going to line up neatly.
