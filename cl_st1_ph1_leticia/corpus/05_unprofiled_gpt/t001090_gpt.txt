we used to feel we had to work through lunch, and then stay on several hours after the official finishing time, just to prove how dedicated we were. that was the culture: if you actually took a break or went home on time, you somehow weren’t “properly” committed. I’ve really moved away from that now. I look at my very wealthy German friend who runs a successful asphalt business and works fewer than forty hours a week, and it completely undermines that old mentality. he’s proof you don’t need endless hours to be effective or successful.
