so I read this young adult book recently, it’s fiction but it really stuck with me. The main character is this very beautiful girl, but she’s not written to be especially likeable or sympathetic, which I thought was interesting. She ends up being gang raped by a group of local footballers, and they take pictures of it and share them online.

The whole story is about what happens after that – how consent works, or rather doesn’t, in that situation, and how social media makes everything so much worse because those images are everywhere. It really digs into the fallout: reputation, blame, how people talk about her, and how the boys are seen.

It’s not a comfortable read at all, but I think that’s why it’s so important. I honestly think books like that should be pushed more towards young people, especially young men, to get them thinking properly about consent and the consequences of what they do online.
