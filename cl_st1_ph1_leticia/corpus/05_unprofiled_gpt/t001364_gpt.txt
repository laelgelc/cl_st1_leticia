So this article was by this young Conservative bloke who’d been invited onto this BBC Three show that was meant to get young people talking about politics, you know, bridge that gap with all the disaffected youth and everything. And he was explaining how those kinds of programmes always have one person there basically as a stooge, whose job is to sit and defend themselves and their views while everyone else piles on for an hour. And he realised, in this really funny way, that that was him. He walked into the studio, looked round the panel thinking, “Who’s the poor sod who has to defend all the unpopular views?” and then went, “Oh… it’s me.”
