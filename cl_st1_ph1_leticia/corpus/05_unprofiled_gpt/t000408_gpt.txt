From what I’ve seen helping out with academic skills, the ones who really get what an academic argument looks like generally do fine, as long as they don’t try to be too clever about it. It’s the people who think it’s all just descriptive writing, blah blah blah, who end up failing, because there’s no real structure in their heads. So I reckon the key is to stick to basics: “my argument is… my rationale is…” and so on. They’ll usually pass you whether you’ve read that book in depth or not. You can always go back and read properly later; right now it’s about getting through the assignment.
