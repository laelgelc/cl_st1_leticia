I’m not exactly saying we should just ignore schools altogether, but I don’t think they’re the main thing. To me, it comes down much more to the person. If you really want to do something, you’ll find a way to do it, whatever school you went to. There are loads of people who’ve been through public or private schools and still end up in completely ordinary jobs, nothing special at all. So the school can’t be the magic ingredient. It feels like that drive and motivation, that push to go after what you want, is something you’ve either got or you haven’t. I don’t really think you can properly teach that in a classroom, however good the school is.
