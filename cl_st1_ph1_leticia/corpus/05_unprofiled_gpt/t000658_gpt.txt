They’d put all these children’s essays into little plastic envelopes and mounted them up in the history museum, a whole long row of them along the wall. I remember standing there looking at them, and there were these women who’d actually brought chairs so they could sit and read them all. They were going along saying, “What a shame,” really sadly, and the two of us just sort of smiled at each other. And then the roof went, you know, and they had absolutely no idea.
