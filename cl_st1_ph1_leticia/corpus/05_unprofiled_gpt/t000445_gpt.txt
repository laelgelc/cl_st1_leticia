I was the first one in my family to go to university, which was a big step for us. After that I went into industry, became a plant manager, and then ended up as the boss in Saudi. I was running what at the time was the biggest smelter in the world, and that meant I used to travel all over the place, all over the world really. My dad was amazed by what I’d done, but sadly he died before he could see me reach that final job. He’d often regretted never being allowed to go to grammar school and really realise his own potential.
