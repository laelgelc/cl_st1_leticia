They were literally coming into class without really using their books at all. The class book just stayed at school because, you know, carrying it around wasn’t considered cool. So instead of a proper workbook or something they could look back on, they just had these few scrappy A4 sheets, printed or photocopied, half‑stuck into the book. And they never went back to them. There was no sense of “this is what we did last week” or “this shows how far I’ve come.” They had no proper record of their work, no clear sense of progress, and nothing concrete to support their learning in the moment.
