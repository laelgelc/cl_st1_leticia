His new album’s really interesting, actually – for someone who’s Jewish, it’s full of all these really Christian‑sounding, quite biblical references, very New Testament‑y in places. He’s such an interesting character, but he still comes across as pretty depressed. There’s that line about asking an ex‑lover, who absolutely hates him, to “hate me less,” which is just so bleak and self‑lacerating. It really fits with that whole sense of him wrestling with faith and guilt and love all at once, in this really heavy, melancholy way.
