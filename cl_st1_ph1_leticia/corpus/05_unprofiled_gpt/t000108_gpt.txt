Once we’d worked out what was going on, it was Friday afternoon, so I just thought, right, we’ve got to deal with this properly. I sat down and emailed everybody, explained what we thought it was, what the symptoms were, and I put an NHS link in there so they could read it all for themselves and not just take my word for it.

Then we went round both buildings and literally wiped down everything we could think of with antibacterial wipes – door handles, desks, phones, the lot – just to try and stop it spreading any further. And I spoke to the finance director and said, look, I know we don’t have a sick pay policy, but I want these people paid, including the one I told not to come in. It wasn’t their fault, and I didn’t want anyone feeling they had to drag themselves in and pass it on just because they’d lose money.
