it was this incredible piece in the turbine hall by this Chinese artist whose name I can never remember, he’d been imprisoned for something like forty years for speaking out against the Chinese regime and is apparently now on the run. the whole floor was covered in these tiny grains – I can’t even remember if it was sand or rice – but each one had been hand‑painted by Chinese workers. they’d actually used lead paint on them, which of course health and safety didn’t like at all. at first you could walk on it, so people were literally treading all over these millions of painstakingly painted grains, and then they decided you couldn’t walk on it, and in the end they just took the whole thing away. it sort of turned into this very stark statement about Westerners trampling on Eastern labour and all the time and effort that goes into it.
