they do grow a lot of what they can there, but just not enough to match how much people actually eat. like, Koreans eat loads of pork, but I’ve never seen a pig anywhere, so I just assume all of that must be imported from China or somewhere like that. what you do see everywhere is these little local farms, any bit of free land they’ve got they’ll be growing on it. but it’s always the same sort of stuff – pumpkin, courgette, chillies, green onion, that kind of thing. and they use it in a really straightforward way, just for food, not loads of different variations or fancy recipes, just the basic vegetables going straight into what they cook every day.
