I just think a lot of these parents completely duck their responsibility, and you can see the consequences all around us. Look at those riots in the summer – you could pick out exactly which of our ex‑pupils would have been right in the middle of it, no question. And then you get these parents on television or in the papers saying, “Well, I can’t possibly know where my fifteen‑ or sixteen‑year‑old is at eleven o’clock at night,” as if that’s perfectly reasonable. Or they come out with, “There’s nothing else for them to do,” as though boredom is an excuse for smashing up shops. It’s that total refusal to accept that you are meant to know where your children are and what they’re doing, and to set some boundaries.
