On the following Monday it was worse, because Mum had gone away and I was on another twelve-hour shift, this time as the only trained nurse on duty so I couldn’t just leave work. I really didn’t know what to do with Winnie. In the end I asked this woman I know, who’s got dogs and walks them when she goes to collect her children from school, if she would take Winnie out as well. I felt it was a real imposition, especially as she didn’t know Winnie and Winnie didn’t know her, but I had no other option.
