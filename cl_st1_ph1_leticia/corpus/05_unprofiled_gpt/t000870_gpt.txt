yes it was pretty awful really, and what made it worse was that I did try to flag it up at the time. I went to this young doctor twice, telling him I had slurred speech and I was struggling to find words, and he just dismissed it, didn’t take it seriously at all. then of course I went on to have a stroke – only a small one, a TIA, but still a stroke – and it all came back to that.

and then, as if that wasn’t enough, when the company medical insurance was changing over from one scheme to another, he actually recorded the stroke as having happened before the new scheme started. that wasn’t true, but because he put it down like that, we lost our private health insurance. so there was the health side of it, and then this financial knock-on as well.

it’s left me feeling that an older, more experienced doctor would have handled the whole thing much more humanely, both medically and in terms of how they dealt with the insurance situation. that experience really shook my confidence in him.
