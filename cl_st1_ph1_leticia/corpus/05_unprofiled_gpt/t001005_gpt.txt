I think I’d actually be more up for having a child if it wasn’t this lifelong, open‑ended commitment. Imagine if the deal was: you have them for, say, five years. In that time you care for them, you bond with them, you teach them everything you can, and then they move on to live with someone else. You’d still have some role in their life, but it wouldn’t be this permanent, all‑consuming responsibility. A system like that would also make it much easier to control how many children there are overall. It’d help keep population growth in check, which matters when we’ve only got finite resources.
