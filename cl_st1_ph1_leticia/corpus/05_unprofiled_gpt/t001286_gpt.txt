it reads “Alaikum rafour Yazid Ibn Sufyan.” “Alaikum” is a vocative expression, addressing people directly, and “rafour” comes from an Arabic verb meaning to pardon or forgive. Yazid Ibn Sufyan was the son of Muawiya, who was the second caliph of the Umayyad empire. We know that Yazid sailed along that coast in 672 AD, so he must have stopped at the harbour at Knidos and written his name there, on this already destroyed Byzantine church. So it’s essentially his little piece of Arabic graffiti.
