So after all that, I ended up coming up with a third idea, kind of as a response to the vandalism and the stealing that had gone on with the previous map. The thought was: if most of the basic stuff isn’t really “yours” to lose, people might not get so upset, and it might feel more like everyone’s working together. So I suggested we make almost everything communal – like dirt, wood, sand, meat, all the everyday resources – and just have people share and use them freely. Then only the really valuable things, the rare resources, would be properly private and hidden away. That way you’d still have a sense of ownership over the special stuff, but the bulk of what you needed would be part of a shared pool, which I hoped would encourage more of a community spirit.
