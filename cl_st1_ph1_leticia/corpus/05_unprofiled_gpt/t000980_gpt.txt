Me and [teammate] were actually playing well as well, we had a strong five-man midfield, we were on top for big spells, but it doesn’t matter if every time they go forward they score.

We ended up losing 4–1, and it felt like one of those games where we basically had to take every single chance we got, otherwise we were never going to get anything out of it. You can’t miss a thing when you know the back line’s going to gift them goals.

What really does my head in is knowing we’ve actually got someone who’d be the perfect centre back, proper ideal for it, but we keep him up top because he’s “too important” in attack. And then we watch these clowns at the back implode every week. It just leaves you feeling completely helpless, like no matter how well I play, or how well a couple of us play, we’re doomed to lose anyway.
