I think with a lot of stuff that gets published there’s this constant trade‑off between quality and content. If the content’s strong enough, or controversial enough, it’ll get through even if the actual writing is pretty awful. You see it all the time in the tabloids – they’re not exactly paragons of beautiful prose, but they’ll print anything if it’s got a punchy angle or some scandal in it.

Because I read so much more now, and a much wider range of things than I used to, I notice that a lot more. I can tell when something’s clunky or oddly written, even if the idea behind it is interesting. And I do think that just reading more has rubbed off on me a bit and made my own writing better.
