like in the past you’d have these tiny tribes of, like, fifty people, and each one would literally have its own language, completely separate from the next tribe over. but now loads of those tribal languages are dying out. you see it in places like Papua New Guinea, where there used to be this huge diversity of little local languages. people are shifting to the big, national or global ones instead, because Spanish or English are seen as more prestigious. they’re associated with higher education and higher social class, so there’s this pressure to drop the local language and adopt those.
