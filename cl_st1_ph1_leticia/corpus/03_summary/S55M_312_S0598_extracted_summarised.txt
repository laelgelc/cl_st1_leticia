The speaker responds sympathetically to someone saying they never got Christmas stockings, explains that they and their family still get stockings from their parents but that the tradition has adapted as they grew older—from leaving out biscuits, a carrot and brandy for Santa and having their parents fill stockings in their rooms at night, to now giving the treats directly to their parents and taking their stockings to their parents’ room to be filled overnight—and notes that stockings contain small, practical items like underwear and pyjamas.
