The speaker describes how, just off the courtyard in Sponza Palace in Dubrovnik, there is a room commemorating victims of the 1991–1992 siege with photographs and artifacts that starkly show the realities of war, and explains that although Croatia may appear as a buzzing young country on the coast, inland areas like Vukovar and surrounding Serb villages still bear visible war damage and an underlying emotional divide, with Croats and Serbs now living separately despite having once lived harmoniously together.
