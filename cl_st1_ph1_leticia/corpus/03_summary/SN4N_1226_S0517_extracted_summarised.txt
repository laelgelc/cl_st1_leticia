The speaker describes how, in heavy rain and mud at a cross-country event where they knew no one, they repeatedly asked different officials with clipboards to register a Year Nine boy whose name and school did not appear on the list, were nonetheless checked in for a 1 p.m. race and told to return at 2:15 p.m., chose not to walk the course because of the bad weather, moved the car closer, then came back to collect the race bib only to be told again that the boy’s school was not on the organiser’s list and that they were actually at the wrong team’s tent.
