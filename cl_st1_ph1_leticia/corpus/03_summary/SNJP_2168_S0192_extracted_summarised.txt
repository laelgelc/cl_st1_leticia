The speaker describes encountering a banded sea krait, which they call the most poisonous snake in the world, on three occasions in the Andaman Isles—once swimming around their feet when they didn't know what it was, once curled up while scuba diving, and once surfacing for air—remarking on its beauty and explaining, in reference to David Attenborough, that its bite can kill a person within about ten seconds to a minute due to a powerful neurotoxin needed to quickly immobilise fast-swimming prey.
