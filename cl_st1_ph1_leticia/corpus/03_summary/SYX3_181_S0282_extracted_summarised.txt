The speaker recounts that when she was about thirty her next-door neighbour, a highly strung and very anxious woman with OCD whom doctors dismissed for years as hysterical and neurotic despite longstanding symptoms, was eventually diagnosed with stage four ovarian cancer and died, which the speaker finds frightening given how clear breast and cervical cancer checks are by comparison, how unaware she was until recently of vulval and vaginal cancers or their symptoms, and how easily ovarian cancer symptoms can be misattributed by doctors to conditions like IBS or to a woman’s age without triggering further tests such as blood tests or ultrasounds.
