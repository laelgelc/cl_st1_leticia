The speaker explains that one pretext for the massacre and expulsion of the Jews was the accusation that they shaved silver coins, contrasts this with the discovery of a perfect silver coin deliberately placed in a niche in the partially demolished structure, notes that the site was filled with rubble and later excavated and refilled, and recounts that a historian who studied the immigrant tower presented their so‑called synagogue at a German conference where German historians and archaeologists said it sounded exactly like a similar building in northern Germany, which the speaker found pleasing.
