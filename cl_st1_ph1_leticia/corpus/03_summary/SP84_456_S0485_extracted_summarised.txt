The speaker explains that the inscription reads “Alaikum rafour Yazid Ibn Sufyan,” clarifies that “Alaikum” is a vocative expression and “rafour” comes from an Arabic verb meaning to pardon or forgive, identifies Yazid Ibn Sufyan as the son of Muawiya, the second caliph of the Umayyad empire, and states that Yazid sailed along that coast in 672 AD, must have stopped at the harbour at Knidos, and written his name on the already destroyed Byzantine church.
