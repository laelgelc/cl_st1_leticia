The speaker explains that their neighbour’s ovarian cancer symptoms were misinterpreted by doctors as signs of neuroticism and hysteria until the disease reached stage four, then reflects on how frightening it is that, unlike breast and cervical cancer, many women (including the speaker) are unaware of the symptoms of vulval and vaginal cancers, and notes that vague symptoms of ovarian cancer are often dismissed as conditions like IBS or age-related issues, leading doctors not to order tests or scans because they follow the most statistically likely, non-cancer explanation.
