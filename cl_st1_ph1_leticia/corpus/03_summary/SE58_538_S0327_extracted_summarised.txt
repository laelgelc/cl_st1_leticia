The speaker describes a wonderful exhibition of works by the somewhat forgotten sixteenth-century portrait artist Moroni, notes that it cannot be seen together with another exhibition, explains that the portraits are psychologically incisive and amazing, and then discusses options for getting the other person into the gallery—either by arranging an introduction to a man who can get them in at any time or by using the official rule that friends can enter for free only on Fridays at nine o'clock.
