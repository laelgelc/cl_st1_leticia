The speaker recounts that during the war the family, living in eastern Poland, were taken prisoner by Russians who nearly shot the father because he was a land agent in uniform, but spared him after the mother begged for his life; the family was sent to Siberia where they suffered hardships including having no shoes and a baby dying on a train, then after the Russians became allies the Polish men, including the father, formed an army that went to North Africa and fought up through Italy at Monte Cassino while the women, including the mother, travelled through Kazakhstan to North Africa to work in a hospital, and eventually the parents were reunited with the help of the Red Cross among many displaced people.
