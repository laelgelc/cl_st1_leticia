The speaker explains that they tell their first-year and mature students to raise their hands whenever they feel genuinely confused or excited and ask the lecturer about misconceptions on the topic, arguing that this question stimulates multi-way dialogue, helps everyone learn regardless of prior knowledge, reverses the usual “tell me what I want to hear” dynamic, and would be especially good for the addressee because they need intellectual challenge and may otherwise be bored by classmates who know little about the subject.
