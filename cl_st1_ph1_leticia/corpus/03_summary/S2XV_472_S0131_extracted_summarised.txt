The speaker describes a case of a barely sixteen-year-old girl with special needs who, after her grandmother could no longer cope, was given her own flat by the council—a decision the speaker questions as unsafe and inappropriate—and contrasts this with how the girl behaved well at school due to clear boundaries, before broadening out to explain that many similar pupils arrived from mainstream schools having been excluded from classrooms, unused to learning environments, and surprised to be asked to do any work.
