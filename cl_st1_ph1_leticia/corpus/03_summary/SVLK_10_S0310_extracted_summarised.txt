The speaker explains that after moving to another university when her children were about six and seven, she describes how careers advisers in universities are each allocated a portfolio of academic departments, build relationships with departmental staff, may sometimes teach assessed careers modules funded by departments, and in all cases provide both individual and group sessions for students on core topics such as marketing themselves, finding employers, interviews, selection tests, networking, and creative job hunting, with additional sessions added in response to changes in the job market.
