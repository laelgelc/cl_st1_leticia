The speaker explains that studying large, complex historical topics like the suffragettes and world wars can better engage students’ curiosity by offering multiple angles of interest (politics, strategy, etc.), contrasts this with their own experience of history being narrowly sliced into limited topics that didn’t spark curiosity, describes how this led them to merely copy from the board rather than think analytically, notes they are not good at memorisation and were not taught that skill, and concludes that this was a shame given the cost of their education.
