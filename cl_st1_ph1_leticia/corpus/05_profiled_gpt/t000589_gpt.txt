so in the end we just said forget the guide and decided to go up the mountain on our own instead. we had to set off at something like three in the morning, when it was absolutely freezing, and we were already starting from about two and a half thousand metres, going up to around four and a half thousand. it was that horrible cold where you can see your breath and your fingers don’t really work properly, and it’s still basically night.

as we were going up in that sort of twilight – you know, where you can’t quite see properly and everything looks a bit dodgy – we were following this really indistinct path. you could just about make it out, but not clearly, and then we saw this animal on the trail ahead of us. we couldn’t tell exactly what it was, just this shape moving in the half-light. it might’ve been a leopard, or maybe just a really big dog, but we honestly didn’t know.

the not knowing was the worst bit. from that moment I was completely on edge. every little sound put me on alert, and I just had that feeling that something was out there watching us while we were trying to get up this mountain in the dark.
