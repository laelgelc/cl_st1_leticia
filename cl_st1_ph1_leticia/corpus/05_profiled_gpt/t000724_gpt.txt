Well, once I decided to take the early retirement, the good thing was I could cut my hours right down but still get the same money, which suited me very nicely. I timed it just before they changed all the rules about retirement age and what you could get, so I was quite lucky in that sense. I remember thinking I’d spent more time in that place than I had at home over the years, twenty‑five years of my life, but oddly enough, when I actually walked away, I felt nothing at all. It was very strange, really. You’d think I’d be in bits, but I wasn’t.

Funny thing is, later on when I left the other job – the more relaxed one – I felt much more emotional about that. It started off very easygoing there, and then bit by bit they pulled me into doing more and more hours, so it crept up on me. Still, it had a nicer feel to it somehow, so when I left that place I really did feel it, more than I ever did after those twenty‑five years.

And then there was that other place I went to for a year – I was really unhappy there. It was a very weird year. I just didn’t settle, and I think it showed. One of the women I worked with said something about how fed up I was, and it got back to my old boss. Next thing I knew, he was on the phone trying to poach me back. That made me laugh a bit, because I’d only been gone a short while and already they were trying to reel me in again.
