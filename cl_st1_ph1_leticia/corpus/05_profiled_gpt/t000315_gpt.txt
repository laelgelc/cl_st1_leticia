What strikes me is how long that’s been around. And then you and I are here, both immigrants, both “foreign,” but somehow we’re not being lumped in with that stereotype because we’re white, or British, or teaching English, or whatever it is that makes us supposedly “good” for the economy.

I find that really uncomfortable, because if a Syrian from a war zone isn’t welcome here, why on earth should I feel welcome? I’m as much an immigrant as they are, just with a different passport and skin colour.
