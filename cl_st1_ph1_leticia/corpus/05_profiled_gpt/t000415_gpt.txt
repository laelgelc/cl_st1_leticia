and that’s what I mean about the way education’s set up at the moment – it’s a totally different learning mechanism. Instead of you working things out from your own perspective, you’ve got lecturers standing there who’ve already pre–selected all the examples and the bits they think are interesting. They’ve done all the working out and then just feed you the edited highlights.

What I’d really like is for students to push back a bit more and actually question that. Sit there in a lecture and say, “Right, how does this apply to my area? How does this fit with what I’m interested in?” rather than just passively taking it all in.

If more people did that – properly questioned the material and the lecturer – I honestly think it would make the whole lecture better for everyone in the room.
