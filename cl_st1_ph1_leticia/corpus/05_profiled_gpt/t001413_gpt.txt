I was this close to just paying the twenty-four quid, because in the grand scheme of things it’s not a lot of money, is it? But I just thought, no, they’re in the wrong and I’m not paying it on principle. So I rang Citizens Advice, because of this new Consumer Rights Act that came in on the first of October, and I wanted to be sure.  

They said no, they can’t do that – not within thirty days when the goods are faulty – and that we’re entitled to a full refund. They pointed me to these template letters on their website, spelling out your rights and what you want the company to do.  

So we used one of those, the one that’s titled something like “notice before county court claim” or whatever it’s called, basically warning them that if they don’t sort it out we’ll take it further, and sent that off asserting our right to a full refund.
