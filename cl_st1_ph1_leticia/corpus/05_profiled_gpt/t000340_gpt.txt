They told her in the hospital it would be three weeks before they could take the stitches out, which she found absolutely horrifying because of her training. She actually rang us from the hospital, but only after she’d already phoned her coach first. He immediately swung into action and arranged for her to see a sports doctor, who took the stitches out within ten days and had her back in the pool swimming again. Even in that first week, when she really shouldn’t have been doing much, she was on the turbo trainer cycling indoors with one leg, just to try and keep going with her training programme for the championships, though she did fall a bit behind.
