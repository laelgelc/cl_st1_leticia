In Israel, Saturday really is treated as the Sabbath, even by lots of people who wouldn’t call themselves religious at all. It’s just taken for granted that you don’t really do anything then, it’s a day of stopping. For people who are properly religious it goes much further – they’re not supposed to create or use things, so no driving cars, no switching lights on and off, that kind of thing. Because of that, almost everything is shut, and that sense of Saturday inactivity is a normal part of the culture.
