I’m not exactly saying we should just ignore schools altogether; it’s more that I think your own drive matters more than where you went. If somebody really wants to do something, I feel like they’ll find a way to do it, whatever school they were at. You get loads of kids coming out of public or private schools who just end up in completely ordinary jobs anyway, so it’s not some magic ticket. To me it’s much more about what’s inside you. That sort of drive and motivation, I don’t really think you can teach it in a classroom. It’s kind of an innate thing. You’ve either got that push in you or you haven’t, and that makes more difference than the school label.
