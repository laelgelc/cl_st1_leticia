I think a lot of what fed into it for me was what I'd seen and heard over the years. My mum had had absolutely dreadful menopausal symptoms, really, really bad, and there were other women I knew who’d had a horrible time of it as well. So in my head that set a sort of benchmark of what “normal” could look like.

On top of that, there was all the stuff I’d read and heard about the menopause, and this sense that doctors tend to play things down when women talk about bleeding. So when I started having this heavy bleeding myself, I just slotted it into that picture and thought, well, this is probably par for the course.

I was really worried that if I went to the doctor and made a fuss I’d be seen as overreacting, or just whinging. That was very much in my mind – that they’d think, oh here we go, another one complaining about the same old thing. So instead of seeing it as something that needed looking at, I kept telling myself it must be within normal bounds and I should just put up with it.
