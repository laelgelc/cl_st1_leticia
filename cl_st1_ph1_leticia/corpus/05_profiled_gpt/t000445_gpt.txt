I was the first one in my family to get to university, which was quite something given where we’d all started. From there I went into industry, became a plant manager and then ended up as the boss out in Saudi. I was running what was, at the time, the biggest smelter in the world and it meant I travelled all over the place, seeing parts of the world my parents could hardly have imagined. My dad was amazed by how far I’d gone, but he died before I reached that final role. He’d often regretted never being allowed to go to grammar school and make the most of his own abilities.
