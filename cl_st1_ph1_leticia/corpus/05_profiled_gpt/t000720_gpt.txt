Well, the oxygen cylinder just hadn’t been properly filled or checked, and they admitted liability for that, so she ended up with quite a lot of compensation money. But it never made her happy. She just kept buying houses, doing them up, changing her mind, moving again – she never really settled anywhere. Bit by bit, the money just went, and in the end she had to go back to an ordinary working life.

It always seemed to me she was trying to fill the gap, you know, compensate for losing her husband with holidays and spending and all that, but of course it doesn’t work. You can’t spend your way out of that kind of loss.

Her son’s twenty-six now, and he’s only just moved into his first flat with his girlfriend. They’ve been living together for several years already, so it’s not new in that sense, but it’s their first proper place of their own.
