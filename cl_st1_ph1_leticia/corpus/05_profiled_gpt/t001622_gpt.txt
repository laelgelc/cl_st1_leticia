I was talking to him about his design and said, look, you don’t need that extra line saying “serving such-and-such and the surrounding area.” If you’ve already got “’s premier plumbers since nineteen fifty-five” up there, that clearly implies you cover the area, doesn’t it? So that extra line is just redundant. By dropping it, you clean the message up and you also free up a bit of space in the layout, which is really what you want. Then you can use that space to make the important elements larger and more effective.
