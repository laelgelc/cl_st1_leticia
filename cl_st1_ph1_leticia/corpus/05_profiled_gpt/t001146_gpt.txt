it just got worse and worse over the day, like at first it was just being really sick and then it turned into diarrhoea and then there was blood in it, and by the end there was literally nothing left to come out but it still felt like I needed to go, and it was just this horrible pain. I ended up ringing my mum because I didn’t really know what to do, and she was like, you need to go to the hospital, don’t mess around with it. So I went in and saw a doctor there, and he said it was probably some kind of bacterial infection. They put me on antibiotics, and luckily that sorted it out pretty quickly once I started taking them.

The whole thing, with the consultation and the antibiotics, was about seventy soles, which doesn’t sound like a lot if you think in pounds, but over there money goes so much further than it does in England, so it felt like a big chunk at the time. It really made me aware of how different the cost of things is, and how even something that seems relatively cheap to us is actually quite a lot in Peru.
