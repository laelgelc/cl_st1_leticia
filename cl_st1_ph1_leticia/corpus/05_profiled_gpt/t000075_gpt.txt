Honestly, you look at something like the Jeremy Kyle show and you just think, that is their absolute aspiration now, isn’t it? To go on telly, sit on that sofa, have their entire private life dragged out in public, all the dirty washing aired, and somehow that’s seen as success. It’s not even just that they’re willing to do it, it’s that they *want* to, they think it’s something to be proud of. And the broadcasters are just as bad, sticking it on in the middle of the day and calling it entertainment. It’s irresponsible, really, because it feeds that whole “look at me, look at me” culture and never mind any sense of dignity or privacy.

It all ties in with what we see at school. I remember taking a group on a trip and there was this one girl, real handful at the best of times. Before we went, we’d been absolutely clear with parents: mobiles off, there’d be one contact number, and we’d call if there was a problem. We explained exactly how communication would work while we were away. But her mum just kept ringing and ringing, ignoring everything we’d said. Half the time she couldn’t be reached properly anyway because she didn’t even have a landline any more – bills hadn’t been paid, so it’d been cut off.

When we got back, she was in the car park, guns blazing, straight away. After all that information in advance, after all the rules laid out, she still came storming up, furious, as if we’d done something wrong. No attempt to listen, no acknowledgement that we’d been clear from the start about how things would be handled. Just this aggressive confrontation, right there in front of everyone. And you just think, if that’s what the kids see day in, day out, no wonder they think that kind of behaviour’s normal – and no wonder they think being loud and dramatic in public is the way to live.
