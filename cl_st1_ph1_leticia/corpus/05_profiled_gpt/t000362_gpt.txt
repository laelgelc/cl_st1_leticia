from about October through to March it was absolutely freezing in the shop, and my boss insisted on keeping the door open the whole time because he was obsessed with getting people in off the street. so we’d all just be standing there shivering while this icy wind blew straight through, and there was nothing we could do about it because he was convinced an open door meant more customers.

when it finally snowed properly and it was just ridiculous, we were at last allowed to shut the door. we even put up this little sign that we’d copied from somewhere else saying something like “we’re just keeping warm”, just to justify it, which made me laugh because we were literally only doing what any sane person would have done weeks before.

it was around then that I discovered my favourite baked potato topping. someone mentioned that loads of the students used to order chicken curry and cheese, and I thought I’d give it a try. so you’ve got this really hot baked potato, loads of butter, a pile of cheese, and then this mildly spicy chicken curry on top. the curry wasn’t too hot, just enough of a kick, and the whole lot together was just gorgeous. it became my go‑to combination after that, especially when we were so cold all the time.
