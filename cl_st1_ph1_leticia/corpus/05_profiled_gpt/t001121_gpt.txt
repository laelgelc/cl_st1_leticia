We had to open the post first thing, and it was full of cheques that had come in for our customers. We’d then sort all those cheques into different compartments according to the customers’ surnames, so everything was in some kind of order. After that, we had to “post” them, as we called it, using a double-entry system. Every debit had to have a corresponding credit, absolutely everything matched up. Nobody was allowed to leave the bank at the end of the day until the debit and credit sides balanced perfectly.
