It's more than just central to their thinking, really; it underpins so much of what people do and how they see the world. Take Assad, for instance: he’s an Alawite, which is a group that’s doctrinally much closer to the Shia than to the Sunnis. In the Iranian Shia view, though, many would regard the Alawites as virtually pagans, not proper Muslims at all. Yet in that predominantly Sunni region, they’re treated politically and strategically as Shia allies. All these fine religious and sectarian distinctions, which still drive loyalties and hatreds today, go right back to the seventh century, and they’ve never really gone away.
