Well, I suppose really it was just common sense, wasn’t it, not putting your head out of a train window. We were always told that. I remember quite clearly going on a steam train when I was about seven, going down to Dover, and then getting on the ship to France. The train was full of army personnel and their wives, all going out, and it felt very busy and rather serious to me as a child.

On the ship we were crammed into this tiny little cabin, my mother and I, hardly any space at all. A steward came round and brought me a cup of tea, stone cold, and I absolutely hated it – I can still remember the taste. It was all very uncomfortable and strange.

What I wonder is, what sort of food did you have as a child in the war years? Would you have had things like apple crumble when the apples were in season, or was that not really around then?
