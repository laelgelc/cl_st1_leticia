When I finished my first degree, my very last exam was in Quranic Arabic. For that, and for the course generally, I’d had to memorise an extraordinary number of languages and dialects, and I did, quite successfully. But immediately after that exam I went into the common room, picked up The Times, and found myself automatically memorising the articles, line after line, without intending to. It was as if the memorisation mechanism just kept running on its own. That was the moment I thought, “this is not right, something’s gone wrong here.”
