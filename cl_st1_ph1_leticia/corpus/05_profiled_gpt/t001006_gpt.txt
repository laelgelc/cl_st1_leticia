I wouldn't turn round to someone and say they have no idea what something’s like, whether it’s travelling or doing a masters or a PhD. If you know people who’ve done it, you’ve talked to them, you’ve watched programmes about it, you’ve got some inclination, some idea, even if it’s a bit skewed. What gets to me is that people with children often talk about it in this very black‑and‑white, dismissive way. It’s that tone of “you just don’t know” that I wouldn’t use myself.
