Everyone basically goes through the same routine there: really intense schooling, then straight into university. For the boys, on top of that, there’s two or three years of military service, usually slotted in the middle of their degree. By the time they’re in their late twenties, they’re expected to be married with children already. If a man hits thirty and he’s still not married, people think he’s a bit strange. So there’s this massive social pressure, and life just becomes this fixed sequence: school, university, army, then marriage and kids.
