She just held onto it for far, far too long, all that hatred and hurt about him, and I kept saying to her, “You’ve got to let this go, Mum, it’s not doing you any good.” I meant in every way – her health, her head, her relationships with everyone around her – but she just couldn’t seem to put it down. It was like a knot she carried for decades. 

When I knew she was dying and I had to fly back, I decided, almost oddly, to book myself a massage before the flight. I remember thinking, I’ve got this long journey ahead and I don’t even know what I’m going back to, how bad things will be, so I need to be as relaxed as I can. It felt like the only small, practical thing I could do for myself in the middle of all that uncertainty.
