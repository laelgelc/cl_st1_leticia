I felt incredibly lucky, really, to have the house in my name at that age. My guardian by then was living in a council place with her husband, and she came round one day and asked if I wanted to sell it. I said no, I wasn’t going to do that. Around that time a school friend of mine, who was working at Woolworths, told me about this chap he worked with, Roger. Now, this was 1970, everyone had long hair, and Roger turns up with this great big handlebar moustache, completely out of step with the fashion. Anyway, Roger had this scheme: he was going to buy a Triumph GT6 sports car, ship it off to Rhodesia, where they were under sanctions after UDI, and flog it there at a profit.
