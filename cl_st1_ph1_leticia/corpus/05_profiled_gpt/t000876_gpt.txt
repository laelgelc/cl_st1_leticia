I was absolutely furious, really, and terribly hurt that they wouldn’t marry us in church after everything I’d done over the years. All those sacrifices, all that time and energy, and then to be told no, just like that. It felt as though none of it counted for anything. To me it summed up how these institutions have become so bound up in their own rules that they’ve lost sight of people and of simple Christian kindness.

It isn’t just the church, either. I see the same sort of irrational bureaucracy in the NHS, and even in the most ordinary places. I remember at a garden centre restaurant, I only wanted a very simple meal, less than what was on the set menu, and yet because of their rigid pricing rules I had to pay more. It’s that same mentality: rules first, people second.
