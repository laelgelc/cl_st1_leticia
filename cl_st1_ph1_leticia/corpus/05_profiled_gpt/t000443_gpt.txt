I just think when you’re already old and a bit frail, you’re kind of isolated anyway, and then the weather up there just makes it ten times worse. It’s pretty much constantly grim, and that must get really depressing for them, you know, stuck inside most of the time. 

When we went up in January it really hit me how harsh it is. It was absolutely bitter, the sort of cold that just cuts straight through you. The wind was howling, and even with loads of layers on, plus a big coat, hat and scarf, I was still freezing. We’ve got these photos from that trip where I’m outside trying to take pictures, and you can see how bundled up I am, and I still look cold. 

Meanwhile Hannah just stayed in the car with the heater on, sensibly enough, and was taking photos of us from through the car window instead. That sort of sums it up really – if it’s that rough for us on a short visit, it’s hard not to think how wearing it must be for them living with that all the time.
