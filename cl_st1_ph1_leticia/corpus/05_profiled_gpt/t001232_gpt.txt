I told him I was actually glad he’d done some thinking and got himself to a calmer place. He came out with this thing about feeling like the wind in that proverb, you know, the one with the wind and the sun and the man with the coat. So I picked up on it and said, “Well, that’s exactly what you’ve been like. When you’ve blown and blown at me, I’ve just pulled my coat tighter and dug my heels in. But when you’re gentle, I can actually relax a bit.” I said to him that was the first sensible thing he’d said in a long time. And he said he was glad, that even though it hurt, it had brought me to that understanding.
