I saw this post online from an American and it really made me laugh. They were saying that British people are absolutely obsessed with tea, and that every time someone said “it’s time for tea” everyone would stop what they were doing and gather round. They thought it was like this big ritual, almost like a proper ceremony, happening all the time. But I’m pretty sure they’d just misunderstood what people up north mean when they say “tea.” They weren’t talking about everyone sitting there with fancy teapots and cups; it was just the normal evening meal. So when people said “time for tea,” they just meant dinner, not some weird tea-drinking gathering.
