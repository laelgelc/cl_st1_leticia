yeah they really worked it, the Marx Brothers, it wasn’t just turn up and be funny. They’d take the whole thing out on tour first, all the routines they were planning to use in the films, and they’d just hone it. Night after night, the stuff that got the biggest laughs stayed in, and anything that didn’t land just got dropped. By the time it reached the camera it was absolutely nailed.

And then you’ve got Irving Thalberg at MGM, this young studio head who was absolutely ruthless about quality. On something like *A Night at the Opera* he’d happily send a script back fifteen times, just “rewrite it, rewrite it,” until he thought it was right. There’s this poor writer handing in yet another draft, and Thalberg just sits there, completely stone-faced, reading the whole thing in silence. No smile, no chuckle, nothing, so the writer’s dying inside. Then he finally looks up and says it’s got some of the funniest things he’s ever read.
