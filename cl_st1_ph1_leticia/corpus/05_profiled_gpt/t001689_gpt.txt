So the idea at the outset was really quite modest. The notion was that the two sisters, their two brothers‑in‑law, and me would share a little holiday place in France. My brother‑in‑law was going to stump up the money for the purchase, and I was to provide the elbow‑grease, doing the renovation work. That way we’d all have the benefit of a bolt‑hole over there without it costing everyone a fortune.

We went along to the estate agent’s, but the farmer who actually owned the place we were meant to see was busy and couldn’t get there until six o’clock. While we were waiting around, the agent said, more or less, “Well, look, there’s another property I could show you if you like.” It turned out to be a farm with a water mill, barns, a house and a stream. We said, “Oh, go on then, we’ll have a look.”
