As you get older you really do reach a point where you just don’t care what people think anymore. You think, this is who I am, these are my gifts, and you’re either going to accept me as I am or not at all. But what really hurts is when people who’ve leaned on those gifts – whether it’s the academic side, or the spiritual, or even the more metaphysical things – turn round later and say you’re egotistical or full of yourself. They’ve benefited from it, they’ve come to you for it, and then suddenly you’re “self‑important”. That’s very hurtful, because they damn well used that gift enough.
