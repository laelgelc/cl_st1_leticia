Well I said to her, you know, I really liked that group, they were a nice bunch, so I was quite happy to stay on with them until they actually finished. It felt better to see it through properly, rather than chopping and changing. And then, after that, I ended up covering more classes for somebody else who’d had an operation and was off for a while. So I sort of drifted back in again, really, even though I’d supposedly finished.

I do still enjoy the atmosphere at the college – I like being in that environment, with the students and everything going on – but it’s nothing like it used to be. The funding cuts have really bitten. We’ve gone from having plenty of resources, you know, you didn’t think twice about photocopying or getting basic materials, to this ridiculous situation where there’s hardly anything. People are struggling just to get the simplest things for their lessons. It used to feel properly equipped, and now it’s all severe restrictions and making do, which is a shame, because the teaching side of it is still something I really love.
