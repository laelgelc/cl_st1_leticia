When I was about thirty, our next-door neighbour had this long history of symptoms. She was very highly strung, very anxious, had OCD, and the doctors just wrote her off for years as hysterical and neurotic. All those symptoms were interpreted through that lens. Eventually she was diagnosed with stage four ovarian cancer and by then, really, she was done for. I find that absolutely bloody scary. With breast cancer, you’re told to look for a lump; with cervical cancer, you go for a smear. But I’d never even heard of vulval or vaginal cancer until recently, let alone their symptoms. And ovarian symptoms are so vague they’re easily dismissed as IBS or “your age” instead of prompting blood tests or an ultrasound.
