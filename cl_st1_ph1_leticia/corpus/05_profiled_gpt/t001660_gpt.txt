There was this survey by Ovarian Cancer Action, and they talked about it on Woman’s Hour, and what really struck me was the difference between how men and women are treated. If a man has prostate cancer, everybody expects there to be a whole conversation about his erectile function, his continence, his libido, his sex life generally. It’s all factored into his treatment and follow‑up, as if it’s entirely normal and necessary to address those aspects. But for women with gynaecological cancers, there’s nothing comparable. We don’t have those parallel conversations or any proper counselling about whether, or how, we’re going to carry on our sex lives afterwards.
