Well there’s lots of ways you can do it, aren’t there. You’ve got your private pensions and then you’ve got whatever workplace scheme you’re in. If you opt out of a workplace one, really you’re only going to end up with the basic state pension later on, and that’s not much to live on.

What winds me up about the way it is now is you’ve got people who’ve never put anything in, and they end up getting pension credit, and someone who’s paid in all their life doesn’t get that top‑up because they’ve “got their own”. It feels a bit back to front.

I honestly think at some point they’ll stop that pension credit for people who had the chance to go into a workplace pension and just didn’t bother. They’ll say, “Well, you chose not to, so tough.”
