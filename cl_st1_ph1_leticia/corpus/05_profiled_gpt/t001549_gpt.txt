In Korea, and especially in China, there are these phenomenal numbers of empty high-rise flats just sitting there. A lot of wealthy Chinese can’t take large amounts of money out of the country, so they just put it into property instead, buying up new tower blocks that then stand completely vacant. It’s such a strange contrast, all those unused apartments.

When we were in Mokpo, we only ever saw one homeless guy. That almost made it feel even sadder, because he didn’t have any sort of homeless community around him; he was just on his own. It really made me think, why can’t he just be given one of those empty flats? There are all these places standing empty while he’s out on the street.

After a while, we stopped seeing him around town. I’ve always suspected he might have died that winter, because he just seemed to vanish. It stuck with me, that idea of this one man alone on the streets while so many flats nearby were completely unused.
