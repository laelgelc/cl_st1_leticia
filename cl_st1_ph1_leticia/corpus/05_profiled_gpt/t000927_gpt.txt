I think that’s a brilliant structure actually, it really makes sense to me. Over the course of a week you’d have this group of people working around a shared problem, and as they’re talking and interacting they’d naturally identify two or three concrete resources they actually need to move things forward. Then, on the Sunday, you’d do a really clear summary video that pulls it all together – here’s the problem, here are the specific resources that have emerged as necessary.

That way, anyone who comes along later doesn’t have to wade through hours of material. They can just watch that one video, get an immediate feel for what the issue is, what’s already been discussed, and what’s actually needed. From there they can decide how they want to contribute – whether that’s offering something they already do, or co‑creating a new solution with the people who are already involved.

It’s got that lovely dual purpose then: they’re helping to solve a real problem with a real group, but at the same time they’re potentially growing their own business or services by joining in. They can see a gap, step into it, and develop something alongside others. So the week’s conversation becomes this really practical way of surfacing needs, and the Sunday video is the doorway for new people to come in and help meet them.
