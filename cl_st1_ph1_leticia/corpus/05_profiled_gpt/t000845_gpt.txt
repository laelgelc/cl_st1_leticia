They were all up in one of the bedrooms in the residential block, these Liverpool women, drinking vodka, and he decided to go along to be friendly. He’d already had quite a lot to drink himself, and in his head he was being one of the lads, you know, “man of the people”, fitting in with them. At some point he got a bit too familiar and used this term that he insisted was an affectionate Scouse expression for women. In fact, they thought it was rude, not endearing at all. Crucially, they weren’t nearly as drunk as he was, so they clocked exactly what he’d said and how he’d behaved. Afterwards they went through the proper channels and made an official complaint about him and his behaviour that night.
