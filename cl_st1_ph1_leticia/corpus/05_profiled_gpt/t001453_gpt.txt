The problem is that all the pipe numbers are handwritten, and once they’re coated the original numbers can be lost, so they have to be written on again. That’s where the human error creeps in. You’ve got people on really low wages, stood there copying these long strings of digits all day, and it’s easy to miswrite them. On top of that, some of the numbers just look too similar when they’re handwritten – things like ones and sevens, and nines and fours. So it’s almost built in that mistakes will happen with the identification.
