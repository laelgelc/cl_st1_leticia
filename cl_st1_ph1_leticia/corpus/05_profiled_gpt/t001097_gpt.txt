Only three of us can really read music properly, but we still have a lot of fun with it. The others manage with their colour‑marked notes, but it gets quite tricky once you’ve got loads of bells going, up to twelve at a time. All the notes are written in one straight line on a single clef, so when everyone’s ringing away it’s hard to pick out which note is yours. With that many bells sounding together, and everything crowded onto one line, finding your own bit can be a real challenge.
