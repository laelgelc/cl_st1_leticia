I remember coming down to milk the goats when we had that really deep snow. We couldn’t get the car out at all, so I was on foot every time. I used to meet the doctor regularly, he was walking his rounds as well, and we’d have a bit of a joke about it each time we passed. Eventually I’d get along to that stretch of road near the water tower, where there aren’t any trees, and that’s where it was worst. The snow had blown right over the road, must have been about fifteen feet deep across it. It was an incredible sight, really, the whole road just buried under this massive wall of snow.
