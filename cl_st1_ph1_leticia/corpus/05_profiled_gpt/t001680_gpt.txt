He just found he couldn’t bear it any longer, really – the politics and this endless reporting. He knows he’s good at delivering a clearly defined project, where there’s a start and an end and a proper boundary, but the way things are set up now he was spending all his time writing reports instead of actually doing the work. In the end he just gave it up. He’s sixty now and he’s quite sure he never wants to go back to Lloyds in that sort of role again. Ideally he’d like to find some other part‑time work where he can still use his skills, but nothing’s come up so far.
