They’re just thinking “I’m helping out” or “I’m doing this thing over the holidays”. But actually, that’s really quite heavily connected to doing a role in business.

What I’m trying to get at, strategically, is that students should be building their personal learning network so that whatever they’re doing – even if it’s just a holiday job or some local community project – they’re consciously developing experience that ties back into what they’re studying. They don’t have to stop studying just because they’re on holiday or working. If we can help them see that these small roles of responsibility are part of learning how business works, then we’re starting to link education and the real world properly.
