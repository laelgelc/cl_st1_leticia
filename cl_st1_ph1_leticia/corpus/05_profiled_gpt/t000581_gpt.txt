yeah so the first one was Typhoon Bolaven, in Mokpo, literally just after I’d arrived back in Korea. The school was closed for students, but we got ordered in to “protect the school”, even though it was clearly dangerous outside. There were crosses off church steeples smashing into cars, roof tiles flying around, power was going out, heavy rain, sparks coming off electrical stuff, and the water in the street was really deep.

I had to walk through all that to get there, then there was basically nothing to do – just mop a bit – and hardly anyone around. Then they told me there was no lunch, so I had to walk back home through it, then back to school again, then home again, four times in total. It was about twenty minutes each way, in those conditions. It felt properly dangerous and just incredibly frustrating.
