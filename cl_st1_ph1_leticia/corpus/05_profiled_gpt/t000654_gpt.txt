so yeah after everything got burnt down this bloke actually sent this big statement to the Gaming Society, like this manifesto thing, saying that games shouldn’t be about friendship and co‑operation, they should be about competition and conflict and all that, and that what we’d built was somehow wrong because people were just being nice to each other. And that really put me off, to be honest. I just kind of lost the heart for it after that and stopped playing Minecraft for a while, because you spend all that time building something communal and then someone comes along and says you’re doing gaming wrong and proves it by wrecking everything.

what made it worse was that it all came out of him being annoyed that nobody wanted to do player versus player with him. He’d built his castle and was sat there waiting for battles that never happened, and instead of just accepting that people were more into building and co‑operating he escalated it into this big destructive performance. He even used this lava trap I’d set up – which was meant as a sort of defensive or playful thing – and turned it into a way to burn the whole place down.

later on I ended up giving a talk to my class about the whole incident, using it as an example. they were really interested in how he’d reacted, and especially in how, when I changed my approach completely on the next map, it made him just lose it even more. that contrast – me going one way, him going the opposite way and just freaking out – was something they found really intriguing.
