Russia did feel quite scary at the time, really. Everything was very tightly controlled, you see. We had a set list of places we were allowed to go, and you couldn’t just wander off where you fancied. There were checkpoints along the roads, and when we got to Moscow we had to have an Intourist guide with us, so you always felt slightly watched. The food wasn’t terribly inspiring either, especially in the summer – mostly cabbages, and endless cups of tea, and none of the nice fresh fruit you’d hope for. Poland, by contrast, looked much better off. The fields seemed more productive, the crops bigger, everything looking that bit more prosperous.
