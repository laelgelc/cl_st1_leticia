When I got into the bank about nine in the morning, the very first job was always the post. We’d open all the envelopes with customers’ cheques in them, which had been sent on to us from a central clearing office or from head office. Their job was to sort all the cheques and then post them out to the various banks on which they were drawn. So every morning our bank would receive this batch of post, and we’d sit down and start working through the cheques that had come in.
