They’d had these enormous wreaths with the person’s name all over them, very full-on, whereas we’d gone for quite small, simple flowers that Mum actually liked. As we were going in, they were still moving the previous family’s flowers out, so it all felt a bit awkward, like an overlap of grief. I instinctively went to shift ours out of the way, and the funeral director stopped me, saying no, no, don’t touch them, that’s our job. I remember thinking that was a bit strange, almost officious.
