it must have been so strange for people before they really knew anything about astronomy, trying to work out what on earth the sun and the moon and the stars actually were. Like, how did they explain eclipses, or why the moon seems to change size or just vanish and then come back? They must have had all these stories and theories to make sense of it, just going on what they could see with no real science behind it. And in Old English they had these brilliant compound words for things like that – they’d talk about “sky-candles” for the stars, and “swan-road” or “whale-road” for the sea. The sun’s a “sky-candle” as well, which I think is a really lovely image, sort of poetic but also quite literal.
