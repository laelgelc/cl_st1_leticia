you can't get a mortgage just on a set of foundations, it actually has to resemble a house first. The lenders need it to be at least a proper shell, kind of inhabitable, before they'll consider it. That's why on things like Grand Designs they nearly always use their savings to buy the land outright first. Then they’ll take out loans on the building work itself, and as each stage of the house gets completed and signed off, they can apply for a mortgage in stages. As the property’s value goes up with each phase, they’re able to increase the mortgage accordingly.
