The reason you see so many of the old classics everywhere is that they’re out of copyright, so anyone can publish them. What really bugs me is how copyright works in the States – it’s kind of insidious. Big corporations keep getting the term extended by leaning on the legislators, over and over. In other countries it’s generally much simpler: copyright lasts for the artist’s life plus a few years after. But in the U.S., in 2015, the newest music that’s actually in the public domain is only from about 1920 to 1928, which is madly out of date.
