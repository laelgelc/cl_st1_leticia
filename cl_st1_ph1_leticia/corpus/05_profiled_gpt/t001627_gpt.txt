Well, I’ve been in this job three years now, after nearly twenty years of being self‑employed, which still feels a bit odd to say. The transition was, in a funny way, both difficult and easy. Difficult because I absolutely hate having to be at work for eight o’clock. I’m not used to that lack of flexibility at all, and it was quite a shock to have to fall into someone else’s routine rather than my own.

But it’s been easy in other respects, because I have adjusted to that routine now. I quite like knowing where I am, and doing four longer days so that I can finish at lunchtime on a Friday is actually rather nice. That part feels like a real bonus at the end of the week.

What I really don’t miss is the constant pressure that came with being self‑employed – always having to look for the next bit of work, all the networking, putting yourself out there and, frankly, always having to be nice whether you felt like it or not. That was exhausting in its own way. So although I do sometimes look back at the old flexibility, I don’t regret stepping away from that relentless side of it at all.
