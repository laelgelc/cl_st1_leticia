I’d got it into my head that something had been sorted for the beer festival on Friday, but when I actually thought about it, nothing had ever really been confirmed. It was just one of those things that had been mentioned ages ago, back in September, when someone asked if you wanted to go and you talked about getting tickets. After that, though, there was nothing – no clear arrangement, no plan.

Then the conversation turns to me being asked to give someone a lift from work, which would basically mean I couldn’t have a proper drink at the festival anyway. I said I wasn’t that bothered about going, to be honest. If there’s no firm plan, I’m not going to bend over backwards.

And the big thing for me is getting home. A taxi from there to here is about twenty-five quid. I’m just not prepared to pay twenty-five pounds to get back when I’ve only had three halves of beer. I’m not doing that, it’s not worth it.
