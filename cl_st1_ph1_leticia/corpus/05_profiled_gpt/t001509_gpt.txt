Well, there are a few basic theories that underpin careers guidance, and they’re really about how people make decisions. The one that’s most commonly used in careers services is psychologically based. It works on the assumption that once you’ve got to a certain academic level, you’re then in a position to be more independent, to stand back a bit and think, “What are my skills, what do I value, what actually interests me?” and then base your career choices on that.

But you can’t just assume everybody is operating like that. You have to recognise that some students are making decisions on quite a different basis altogether. So, while the psychological model is very useful and very widely used, if you’re working in careers you’ve always got to keep in mind that not everyone’s choices are driven primarily by their own skills, values and interests.
