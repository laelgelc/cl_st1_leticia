In the evenings they have to go back, don’t they, there’s a curfew and they’re monitored, so it’s not as if they’re just roaming about. It’s more like a kind of safe house, and while they’re there they can do courses and things. I don’t understand why that sort of arrangement isn’t used more, and why they’re not allowed to earn a proper living while they’re inside. As it is, when they’re released they just get their fare back to wherever they came from and about forty quid, and that’s it. That’s nowhere near enough to get any sort of housing sorted.
