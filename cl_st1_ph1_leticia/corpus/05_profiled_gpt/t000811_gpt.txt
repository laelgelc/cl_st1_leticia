he was this Chinese artist, I cannot remember his name at all, but he’d been imprisoned for something like forty years for speaking out against the Chinese regime, and is now apparently on the run. His installation in the turbine hall at Tate Modern was incredible. The whole floor was covered in these absolutely tiny grains, and each one had been hand-painted by Chinese workers. I can’t even remember if it was sand or rice, but either way it was so, so small, and it filled that massive space. People were walking on it at first, and then health and safety stepped in because of the lead paint they’d used. They removed it all, which was really sad, but it sort of turned into this statement about Westerners literally treading on Eastern labour and all the time and effort that goes into it.
