they're not like a massive delivery that just all turns up in one go, you know, they come in in little bits every day, so you might only get say ten pipes or something like that on a wagon, and each one of those pipes has its own number on it, stamped on the end or on a tag, and what we do is we stand there while they’re offloading them and we write every single pipe number down on the delivery note as it comes off, so you’ve got this proper list of exactly what’s arrived that day. It’s not a complicated job at all, it’s literally just watching them come off the wagon and ticking them off, but it makes a massive difference later on because you can match that delivery note to what’s been laid in the ground and what’s been paid for. If someone turns round and says, “oh we never had that pipe” or “you’ve charged us for too many”, you can go straight back to the note and the numbers and prove what was there. And if anything goes wrong with a pipe in the future, you can trace it right back from that paperwork.
