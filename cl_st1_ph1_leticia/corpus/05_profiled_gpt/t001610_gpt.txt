It’s more like we end up having these big debates than proper rows, really. No one’s screaming or anything, it’s just that everyone’s so far apart on what they think that it turns into this massive back-and-forth. We went down recently – it was just me, my dad and my brother, my mum stayed at home with the dog – to see my dad’s sister and her husband. His brother was there as well, with his partner who’s Hungarian.

That brother’s a total school dropout, bit of a hippy, he’s even got his hair in braids now and he lives over in America. My dad’s kind of left-wing, but I’m definitely further left than he is. Then my aunt is completely right-wing, and so is this other relative. So you can imagine, once everyone gets talking, it’s just clash after clash because the views are so different. It doesn’t feel like “family argument” in the dramatic sense, more like constant political sparring whenever we all end up in the same room.
