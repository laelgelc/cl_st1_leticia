I do sometimes think, looking back, that if I’d been a bit more assertive I might actually have challenged that doctor about her reaction. I could have said, “Look, this is your job, you shouldn’t be pulling a face like that.” But I didn’t. I just felt mortified, and I left it. And that sort of thing does stay with you.

Later on, when I started developing cancer, I think it fed into the way I behaved. I delayed going to the doctor, and when I did go I spaced the visits out, because I kept doubting myself – wondering if my symptoms were really serious enough, or interesting enough, to bother anyone with. I went back several times, reporting that I was still bleeding, that it was ongoing, and each time the doctor just seemed mildly puzzled rather than actually getting to the bottom of it. There was never that sense of real resolution.
