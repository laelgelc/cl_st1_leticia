Well fruit trees along a wall are a good idea, you know, they make good use of the space and you can train them nicely. Our own apple trees are a bit hit and miss though. This year most of the apples came off the Golden Delicious, and they were really rather poor quality, not very nice at all. The James Grieve hardly produced anything, just a few, but when it does do well they’re superb, really large apples if you get them at just the right time, before the pests get in or they drop off. 

Last year the cooking apple tree was marvellous, we had an excellent crop and we were giving them away all over the place, you had some, and lots of other people did too. This year there’ve been very few apples on that tree, almost nothing by comparison. It’s so inconsistent from one year to the next.
