some of those more esoteric or mystical strands of Christianity aren’t so hung up on moral rules, they’re much more about that inner, direct experience, like actually finding God within yourself rather than ticking boxes. later on, especially with some of the German Christian mystics, you can really see how they’d been influenced by Eastern religions – they start to shift how they talk about things like the Holy Spirit and even the Holy Trinity. instead of taking them as these literal, external doctrines, they reinterpret them quite metaphorically, as symbols for different aspects of your own self, or different dimensions of inner experience, rather than separate beings floating about somewhere outside you.
