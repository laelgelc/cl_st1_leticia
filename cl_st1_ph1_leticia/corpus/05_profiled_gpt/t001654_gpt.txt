I was saying that, looking back, I’m pretty sure my grandmother had endometrial cancer, or possibly ovarian, years and years ago. But it never once occurred to me that I might get cancer myself. A lot of that was because my mother always said, very firmly, “there’s no cancer in our family.” I think I just absorbed that. On some subliminal level I took it as a kind of guarantee that I wasn’t really at risk. So when people talked about family history and genes, it seemed perfectly logical to me that, since we “didn’t have” cancer in the family, there couldn’t be any genetic element in my case – nothing like the BRCA gene or anything like that. It all felt very reassuring, and I simply didn’t question it.
