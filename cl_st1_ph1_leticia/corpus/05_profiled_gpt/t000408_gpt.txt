From what I’ve seen helping people with academic skills, the ones who really “get” what an academic argument looks like usually do fine, as long as they don’t try to be too clever about it. It’s the ones who have no structure in their heads and just think it’s all descriptive writing, blah blah blah, who tend to fail. What I learnt to do was stick to very clear signposting: “my argument is…”, “my rationale is…”, that sort of thing. They’ll often pass you whether you’ve actually read that book in depth or not. You can always go back and read it properly in your spare time; for now, you just need to get through the assignment.
