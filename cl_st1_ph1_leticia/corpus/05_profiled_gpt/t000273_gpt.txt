I was at Swan for about two years, you know, the girls’ school, and I was always doing what they’d call “crazy” things. We had this blind date competition once, and I turned up done up as a woman. I’d stuck three balloons down me front, had me wellies on, an apron, me hair all piled up, and I was carrying a mop. When I walked in, the girls just went absolutely mad, screaming and laughing. Afterwards they all chased me round the place with their compasses, trying to pop the balloons. I was genuinely frightened. It really brought it home to me how scary girls can be together – in some ways even worse than boys.
