I was only an apprentice at the time, but I came up with this little shortcut jig, a special tool for doing the bearing housings. Instead of doing them the long, standard way, I could turn them out ever so quick. Because of that jig I was producing loads of parts, and the bonus money just rolled in. My basic was about six and six an hour – thirty-odd pence – but the bonus was half a crown an hour on top, so it bumped it up no end. In the end I was on about ten bob an hour plus the half-crown bonus. I had to hide the tool every night so no one else would pinch the idea. Me and my cousin were making so much we’d stash the extra work, then go off fishing while the foreman clocked us in and out.
