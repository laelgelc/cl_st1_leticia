It is lovely talking to you like this, it really is. I actually went to the library and suggested running what I called “art of conversation” classes, where people would simply sit and talk about chosen topics. I do feel a lot of people want real conversation, something a bit more stimulating than you often get, especially after years in a partnership when you’ve said everything ten times over. 

The library told me they already had “knitting and nattering”, but that’s not what I meant at all. I was thinking of proper discussions – what’s going on at the Conservative conference, women priests in the church, anything like that. Of course I’d have to ask for a bit of money to cover the room. Anyway, they preferred origami and knitting, and my idea rather fell on stony ground.
