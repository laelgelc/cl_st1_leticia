As I say, you can always get the AA or the RAC to go and check a car over for you, can’t you? You have to pay for it, but when you’re spending that much money it might be worth it, just for the peace of mind. It’s a big investment if something goes wrong. Years ago you didn’t really have that sort of thing; you’d buy What Car? and sit and read through it, and they’d tell you what to look for. You’d go round the car yourself, checking all the bits they mentioned in the magazine before you decided whether to buy it.
