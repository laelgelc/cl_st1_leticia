So I’d gone along in my own black shift dress, because that’s the style that really makes me happiest, and I wanted her to see straightaway what I usually wear. The personal shopper disappeared off and came back with this black dress that just fitted perfectly, absolutely spot on, and a few others in different colours. One of them was a sort of teal blue, and it actually really suited me, you know, the colour was lovely, but it just didn’t feel like “me” at all, not my style. 

I was quite taken with that black one, thinking, well, that’s the one, really. But then she said, very firmly, that as mother of the bride I simply couldn’t wear black, that it just wasn’t on. So we had to put that black dress to one side, with the idea that I’d buy it separately as a little black dress for myself, rather than for the wedding. 

She then brought out a red dress. I’m usually fine with red, I can wear it, but this one just didn’t do anything for me. I kept finding my eyes going back to the black dress, because none of the others gave me quite the same feeling.
