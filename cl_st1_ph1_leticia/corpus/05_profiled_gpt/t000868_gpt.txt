On our last cruise we met this absolutely lovely couple, and she in particular was a very lively, good-looking woman, probably in her fifties, who came along to my lectures and clearly enjoyed them. We ended up having dinner together, and I began to notice something about her speech. Whenever she asked a question, she’d say “was you?” instead of “were you?”, and she did it quite consistently. It was clearly just her normal London-area way of speaking; she didn’t seem aware of it at all, and I found it rather quaint.
