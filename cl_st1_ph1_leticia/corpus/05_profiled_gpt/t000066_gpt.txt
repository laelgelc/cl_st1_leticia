Well the cake decorating side of it, I sort of fell into that really. I do the sugar craft, the sugar flower work, and at first I didn’t actually need to do the course at all. But the college turned round and said, “If you do it, we’ll pay the fees,” because it would make what I’m doing look a bit more professional on paper. So I thought, well, why not, and I took it up on that basis rather than out of any burning necessity.

What’s been driving me mad though is the other cake decorating teacher. She’s incredibly old‑fashioned in how she works. Everything has to be done her way, to the letter, and she just will not bend, even when it’s obvious that the students would benefit from a different approach. She doesn’t adapt anything to their skills or their circumstances, and she won’t sort out resources for them either – it’s all very much, “This is how I did it, so this is how you’ll do it.”

It’s completely at odds with how I teach, especially in the classes I do with special needs students. I try to be flexible and student‑centred, think about what each group actually needs, and adjust things so they can succeed and enjoy it. Seeing her refuse to move an inch, when a tiny change could make things so much better for them, is really frustrating. It just highlights how differently we see teaching, even though on paper we’re in the same area.
