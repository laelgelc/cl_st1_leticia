I was reading this article about a couple of African countries that were having their first proper democratic elections, and it really stuck with me. They were talking to people who were just ordinary workers – like, literally people sweeping the streets – and these people knew exactly who was standing, what they stood for, what their policies were on the economy, on the prison system, all that kind of thing. They were genuinely excited and properly engaged with it.

It made me think that in places where things are more unjust, people sometimes end up being more politically aware and active, because it really matters to them in a very immediate way. And then you compare that with here, where you see the BBC go out on the high street and interview random people, and often they don’t seem to know or care much about who’s standing or what the issues are. The contrast just feels really stark.
