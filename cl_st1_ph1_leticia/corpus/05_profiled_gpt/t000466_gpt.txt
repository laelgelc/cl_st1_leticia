We had to put the hours in back then, you know. I was doing forty‑eight hours a week as a matter of course, Saturdays as well, and the only bit of a breather you got was a half‑day in the middle of the week. Getting to work wasn’t easy either – no cars about like there are now. You’d be walking, or on your bike, or maybe jump a tram if you were lucky, sometimes hanging on the back of a wagon to get there. And now you hear people moaning about their hours, sitting in offices all day, hardly lifting a finger compared to what we did. They don’t realise how good they’ve got it these days.
