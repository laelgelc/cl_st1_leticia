When we finally moved, I said to the kids, “Right, this is it, this is going to be our permanent home now.” I didn’t want it to feel like yet another sudden upheaval. I also told them, “Look, you’ve got a choice. If you want, we can think about changing to the local school.” I really wanted them to have some say in it, because up to then it had all been, “Now we’re going into the refuge, now we’ve been found, now we’ve got to move again,” and they’d just been shunted between relatives and refuges without any control over what was happening.
