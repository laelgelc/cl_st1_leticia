I think a lot of traditional Labour voters just walked away after Iraq. People felt really cheated because Tony Blair so blatantly lied about the weapons of mass destruction, and everyone could see it was really about oil. Once that came out, Labour completely lost the moral high ground they’d always claimed to have. For some people it became a case of, well, if they’re going to behave like that, you might as well just vote Tory instead. It sort of blurred the lines between the parties, in terms of trust and principles, and that did huge damage to Labour at the time.
