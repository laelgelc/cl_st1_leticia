We had school dinners for quite a long time, and the food always came out in these big metal dishes. You didn’t go up to a hatch or anything; you all sat at the tables and the oldest kid on each table had to do the serving for the younger ones. They’d go round asking everyone what they wanted, dishing it out from these tins. The mashed potato was the worst bit – it didn’t come as a big heap, it was already portioned out, almost like ice cream scoops of mash. You’d just get these solid scoops plonked onto your plate, which always looked a bit grim.
