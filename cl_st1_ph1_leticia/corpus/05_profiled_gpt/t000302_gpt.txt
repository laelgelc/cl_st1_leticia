I found it quite difficult to adjust when I first came back, because in the Middle East it was absolutely routine to be shaking hands all the time. In Oman, in the boarding school, you’d be shaking hands with the shop staff, and then every morning you’d go round the dorm and shake hands with every child. That was just what you did, part of the daily pattern. Interestingly, though, when they actually came into lessons you didn’t shake hands then; it was in all the other situations that you did it.
