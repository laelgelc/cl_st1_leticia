Although we were taken over by the parent company nearly three years ago, we stayed on our original contracts, so we kept our twenty‑five days’ holiday plus bank holidays. The parent company’s starting allowance is lower and only goes up after you’ve done a full calendar year, and they’re quite strict about that, so in comparison we’ve always looked quite good. It’s meant that when we’re recruiting, we can genuinely say our benefits package is better than if you went straight into the group. It’s been a bit of a selling point for us, really, when we’re trying to attract people.
