I do think, though, that alongside all this complexity there are some quite unmistakable patterns. You do get cases where a poet or novelist starts off in absolutely total thrall to somebody else, almost paralysed by admiration. There was that piece in the New York Review the other week about Beckett, for instance, describing his early “enslavement” to Joyce. And then, over time, you can see him gradually breaking free of it, finding a way of writing that isn’t just a faint echo. That trajectory—from initial worship to a real breakthrough—does seem to be a genuine, recurring story.
