So after that little egg timer activity, I just rattle through a few of the pronunciation and word‑level things I want them to notice. I use simple contrasts like long and short vowels – you know, “shot” versus “short”, “chip” versus “cheap” – just to show how one tiny sound can change the word completely. Then I pick up consonant clusters, like in “comfortable”, where there are a lot of sounds together that can trip people up. And I spend a bit of time on “look”, because they often struggle both with saying it and with the different phrasal uses – “look after”, “look for” and so on – to show that even a really simple word can actually be quite difficult.
