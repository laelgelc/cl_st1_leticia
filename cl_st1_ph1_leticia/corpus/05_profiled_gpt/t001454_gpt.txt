In the water industry they work on this sort of four‑year cycle, so you’ll have a spell where it’s all planning and sorting things out, then there’s the tendering bit, then everybody’s buying and it’s all go, and then they pull back again and there are cutbacks and it all goes quiet.

and what happened was, in one of those quiet patches they let a lot of people go, because there just wasn’t the work to keep them on. so at that point there wasn’t much travel, not many jobs, everything kind of shrank back. but now a load of new jobs have come in again, and the work’s there, but of course the staff aren’t, because they’ve already been got rid of.

so you’ve ended up with this daft situation where there’s nobody left to send, and the managing director’s the one having to go and do things like offloading pipes and all that sort of thing, which he really wouldn’t normally be doing. that’s the sort of job you’d expect the lads to be doing, not the MD, but because of the way that four‑year cycle’s gone, they’ve trimmed right down, and now there’s all this work and no one left in place to actually do it, so he’s picking it up himself.
