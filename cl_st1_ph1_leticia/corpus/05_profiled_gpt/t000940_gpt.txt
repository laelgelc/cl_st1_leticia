Higher education institutions would actually want to buy these real-life moving image case studies because their own online content just can’t keep up with that kind of dynamic narrative and storytelling. What we’re talking about is something that’s almost as engaging as a film, but better, because it’s genuinely real. It isn’t pre-scripted in the way documentaries are, even when they pretend to be “real life”.

Think of someone like Louis Theroux – it’s sold as real, but there’s a preconceived narrative, there’s a structure written in advance, and everything that happens gets folded back into that storyline. That’s exactly what I don’t want. These case studies are grounded in an actual situation and develop as they go, rather than being forced to fit a script. That’s why they’re valuable: they’re real, responsive, and educational, not just entertainment dressed up as reality.
