We were only about twelve vehicles away from this absolutely devastating landslide in Nepal, and I keep thinking how close in time we must have been to it. When we got there, the entire side of the mountain had just gone. Where there’d been a village, there was basically nothing, just bits of roofs sticking out of the rubble. You could tell people had died there; it was horrible, just knowing that.

And then there was another really grim experience in Nepal. We ended up travelling straight into an area that had recently flooded, without realising what we were heading into. It had turned into a sort of refugee camp, with aid tents everywhere and people just trying to cope. There were dead buffalo floating in the water, which was really shocking to see. Moving through it all was incredibly difficult; the roads were in a terrible state and it took us something like twelve hours to get through that area.
