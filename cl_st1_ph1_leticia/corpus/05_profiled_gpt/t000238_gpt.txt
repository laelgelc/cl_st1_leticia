So a project manager, the way I see it, is someone who can operate at lots of different levels and in different sectors because the core skill is project management itself. You’re not necessarily the technical expert – that’s more like when I was doing engineering, where you actually need that deep, specific knowledge to do the work yourself. In a project management role, you don’t have to have that same technical expertise. Instead, you use the generic project management skills and bring in the people who do have the specialist knowledge. You hire the right engineers, IT people, whoever, and your job is to coordinate them and get the work delivered, rather than doing the detailed technical stuff yourself.
