yeah so with Healy you can just tell it’s coming from somewhere real, like he’s actually lived all that stuff he’s talking about, all the homelessness and the drinking and the violence and everything. It doesn’t feel like someone imagining what it might be like, it feels like someone remembering it, if you know what I mean. He ended up in this tiny little studio in London, just this really bare space where he was writing, and you get that sense of him sort of clawing his way there from absolutely nothing. There are loads of technically good writers, but with some people there’s this depth that comes from what they’ve actually been through, and you can’t really fake that, it just comes out in the way they tell the story.
