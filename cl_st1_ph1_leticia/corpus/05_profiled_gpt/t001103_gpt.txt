I was talking to my dad about it once, actually, about whether I should go in as an officer or a rating, and he was very clear about it. He was like, “You always want to be in a position where you’re giving the orders, not taking them,” and he really pushed that side of it. But I just couldn’t get my head round it, because what I wanted out of it was that really close-knit friendship thing, that proper camaraderie, and you don’t really get that in the same way when you’re the one in charge. 

For him it was completely different. He always found it really easy to keep that separation, to have that line between him and the people he worked with. He was quite happy with those more distant, acquaintance-type relationships with colleagues. That was just normal for him, and he’s basically done the same job his whole life, so he’s always operated like that, keeping that distance and that clear hierarchy.
