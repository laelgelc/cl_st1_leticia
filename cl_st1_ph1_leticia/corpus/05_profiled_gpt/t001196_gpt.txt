I was saying that for me it’s this really special, almost magical experience just to see those planes, or get inside them, or, you know, to fly in them. It feels like a big “wow” moment. But then I was talking to my auntie’s sister, she’s eighty‑eight, and she’d actually worked on them in the factory during the war. And for her, that wasn’t anything special at all, it was just everyday life, just her normal job. It really struck me how easy it is for us to forget that side of it.
