So in the warm-up before Christmas we were just messing about, and this really light lad on our team was marking me from a throw-in. I just sort of nudged him, knocked him off balance, and he was like, “Don’t embarrass me in front of everyone.” Then later I literally picked him up and threw him with one arm, just to show how little he weighs.

Anyway, that kind of summed up the contrast with our back three. We’ve got these three really slow centre backs, and one of them is just hopeless with a dropping ball. There was this one moment where the ball’s coming down and instead of just heading it or taking it down, he backs off and lets it bounce. Soon as it bounces he’s off balance, the striker’s thinking, “I’ve struck gold here,” nicks it off his head and we’re in trouble. That sort of thing just kills us, because it’s so basic – you don’t let the ball bounce there, you deal with it early. It just shows how slow and indecisive they are at the back.
