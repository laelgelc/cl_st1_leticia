well it was during the war, when they were in eastern Poland, they were taken prisoner by the Russians, and they nearly shot my father, because he was a land agent and he was in uniform, and it was only because my mother begged for his life that they didn’t, and then they were all sent off to Siberia, and it was just awful, no shoes and all that, and there was a baby died on the train, and then when the Russians became allies the Polish men, my father among them, were allowed to form an army, and they were sent out to North Africa, and they fought right up through Italy, at Monte Cassino and so on, and the women, like my mother, were sent a different way, through Kazakhstan and down to North Africa as well, and she ended up working in a hospital out there, and there were just so many people all over the place, all these displaced people, and somehow, with the Red Cross helping, they managed to find each other again, my parents, in the middle of all that, and get reunited after everything that had happened, all that moving about and not knowing where anyone was.
