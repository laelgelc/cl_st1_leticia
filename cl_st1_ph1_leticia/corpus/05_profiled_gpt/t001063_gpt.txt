oh yes, we had to buy the whole lot ourselves, and they were very expensive uniforms indeed. As an officer you were expected to pay for it, that was part of the role, really. When I was an officer cadet I had to choose a tailor, and I remember quite deliberately picking one that nobody had ever heard of in our circle. I thought I was being rather obscure and economical.

It turned out, of course, that he was the tailor for all the very top brass – he did the most senior officers in the service. So my little unknown chap was actually frightfully smart. He was very kind to me, and a bit mischievous too. He quietly put a bright red silk lining into my uniform. Technically it wasn’t allowed – it certainly wasn’t regulation – but he slipped it in all the same.

You’d only see it when the coat moved, just a flash of red, but I knew it was there and it felt rather special. It was exactly the sort of thing that went on, especially in those days with Guards officers and so on – a lot of unspoken class and money in the background, and one-upmanship about kit and social life. They needed a private income to keep up with it all. I imagine it’s all rather more democratic now, or at least I hope so.
