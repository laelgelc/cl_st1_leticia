it was when I first went out to China with the British Council, and we’d gone via Hong Kong, which of course was still British then. I’d ended up there with virtually no money, no luggage, and only a day to try and sort everything out before flying on to Beijing. It was the beginning of September, absolutely boiling hot, and I was getting more and more wound up about the whole business with the missing case.

By the time I finally got to Beijing I was shattered, and still in the same clothes, and I remember waking up on that first morning with blood everywhere from a nosebleed. It was all over the pillow and down my face. In the student accommodation there we didn’t have any of the things you’re supposed to use – no ice, and not even properly cold water from the tap, just this sort of tepid water, which felt completely useless in that heat.
