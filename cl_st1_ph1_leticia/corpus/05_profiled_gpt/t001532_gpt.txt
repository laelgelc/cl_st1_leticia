A lot of our prisons are incredibly old and really ought to be shut down, because they were built at a time when nobody was thinking about things like family support and its impact on reoffending. Dartmoor’s a perfect example. It was deliberately put out in the middle of nowhere, really remote, and it used to be a high‑security prison. Now they’ve got lower‑risk prisoners there, people who could probably be rehabilitated if they had proper contact with their families. But their families just can’t get to them easily. The whole set‑up ignores how important that support is in helping people not to offend again. It feels like we’re clinging on to these old Victorian‑style institutions, instead of asking whether the location and design of the prison actually help people to change when they come out.
