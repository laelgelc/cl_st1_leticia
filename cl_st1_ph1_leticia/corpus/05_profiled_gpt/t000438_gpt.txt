yeah it basically meant affordable housing, that was the gist of it, but they had some fancy term for it I just can’t remember. When we were doing the riverside bridge, you could really see the difference. The ones right on the river, the proper expensive ones, they’d clearly thrown good money at them – decent materials, nice finishes, they actually looked quite smart. Then you’d look over at the cheaper units that were going up as part of the same development and they just looked awful, really shoddy. You could tell they’d cut corners on all the materials, it was just cheap and nasty. I remember saying to one of the blokes on site, you know, “oh maybe I should look at getting one of these,” and he just went, “no, don’t. Seriously, don’t even think about buying one of those.”
