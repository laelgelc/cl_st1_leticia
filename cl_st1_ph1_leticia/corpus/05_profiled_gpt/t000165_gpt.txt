well each wagon only comes in with four pipes on it, and it can take up to two hours just to get those off. They either use a crane or those big suction pads to lift them, and while that’s going on you’ve got to check each pipe properly. If there’s a nick or any damage you’ve got to photograph it, and sometimes they’ll repair it with those heated patches they stick on. Every single pipe’s got a number, so you have to write all the numbers down on the delivery sheet, and then someone from the authority has to sign that off so the payment can go through.

It’s all really slow, it’s not like you’re rushing about. You end up with loads of time hanging around, having a coffee, reading, that sort of thing. It’s not difficult work at all, it’s just that you’re only ever dealing with a few pipes at a time, so I’d imagine it gets quite boring more than anything else.
