I was only twenty-three then, it was 1956, and there I was in charge of a cypher office. We handled both Air Force and British government codes and cyphers, and some of it was very highly restricted material. Certain things were only to be seen by the governor and the top RAF officers, so when that came in I had to lock myself away to deal with it. I remember it all with a good deal of pleasure, actually. The governor would send a car for me, no number plate, just a silver crown on it, and I must say it made me feel quite important.
