He should be paying under a letter of credit, that’s how it’s meant to work. The pipes come in from Turkey, they’re sat there in the docks, all the documents are in place – proof they’ve arrived, insurance, certificate of origin, acceptance, the lot. At that point the bank steps in and enforces payment, usually eighty‑five percent of the four million quid order. The customer can literally see their pipes, their name on them, all lined up ready, so there’s no excuse. That eighty‑five percent is what releases them, pays the supplier, pays the dock, covers the VAT, everything, and then the last fifteen percent comes later.
