There really are published resources for absolutely everything nowadays. When we moved schools they gave us this big whiteboard package that everybody raved about – supposedly wonderful, all singing and dancing, came with lesson plans and all the materials ready-made. On paper it sounded perfect, you know, exactly what you’d want: you just click it on, follow the plan, and off you go.

But in reality it was completely useless for our pupils. The reading level of the texts was for five- to six-year-olds, which was technically where our kids were at in terms of decoding. But the content of those texts was aimed at that age group too, so for fifteen- to sixteen-year-olds it was totally inappropriate. You just couldn’t sit teenagers down and give them that kind of material with pictures and topics clearly designed for infants. So this thing that was meant to save us time and effort just didn’t work at all with our classes.
