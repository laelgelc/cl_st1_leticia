When I first started there it really did feel like a proper partnership, everyone pulling together and getting on, and that made the work enjoyable. Then management changed, roles shifted, and suddenly there were all these different people and characters to deal with. A big chunk of the job became about adapting, fitting in with whoever you were put with, and just getting on with it. You stay professional, you’re polite, you work well with them because you’re at work. But it’s only when you get home that you actually let off steam about it all.
