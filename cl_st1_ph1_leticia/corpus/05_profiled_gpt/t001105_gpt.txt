So with translation memory, most people just don’t know it exists, but it’s actually really simple. What happens is the translator runs the text through this bit of software, and it chops everything up into manageable chunks, usually sentences or short phrases. Every time you translate one of those chunks, it stores both the original and the translation.

Then, when the same or a very similar phrase comes up again later on, the software automatically pops the previous translation into place. The translator can then either accept it as it is or tweak it if it needs changing. That way, the work goes a lot faster, it’s more accurate and consistent across the whole document, and it’s cheaper for the client as well, because you’re not paying full whack for the same thing over and over again.
