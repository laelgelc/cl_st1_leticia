It was just such a rough school, I couldn’t ignore it any more. I’d be dropping the boys off while my own life was in bits, trying to sort myself out and going to counselling, and then I’d hear the things they were coming out with. The way they were talking, the stuff they were seeing and repeating back – it really worried me. I was already feeling so fragile, but I just thought, I can’t leave them there any longer. However much chaos I was in, they needed to be somewhere better than that, somewhere that wasn’t dragging them down. That was really the turning point for me, when I decided I had to get them out and into a different school, whatever it took.
