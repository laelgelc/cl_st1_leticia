Yeah, I mean, I did end up going in the height of summer once, but only because Lou had a placement out there. We went to Greece in July a couple of years ago, and even though it’s that really dry heat, it was still just too hot. You end up feeling absolutely exhausted all the time, it just drains you.

Everyone we spoke to – people in the hotel, people in the shops – kept saying, “Don’t come in August, whatever you do,” because it’s even worse then. And they were also saying if you want to come later in the year, make sure it’s before October, because after that there’s nothing really open. In Pefkohori, where we were, everything more or less shuts down at the beginning to the middle of October. All the people who run the bars and the shops just pack up and go back to Chalkidiki, so the place kind of winds right down.

It’s a shame in a way, because Pefkohori itself is really lovely. It’s just this small, beautiful little coastal town with a nice square and loads of mini supermarkets. You’d see kids wandering down to the beach with these free cool bags full of cold beer, all happy as anything, and it just had a really nice feel to it.
