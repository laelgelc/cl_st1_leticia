I think a lot of teachers have this attitude that they’re somehow owed a living, you know, like the world owes them because they’re in education. You hear it when they start moaning about having to work a few extra years before they get their pension. And I just think, well, look at people in business – they’re working much longer, with far fewer benefits, and no one’s telling them they’re special for it. Teachers already get these really long holidays compared to most other jobs, so when you weigh it all up, it’s actually not a bad deal at all. I’ve been on both sides, in business and in schools, so I do feel I can say that with some confidence. What strikes me as well is that some teachers never really grow up; they’re still like schoolkids themselves, stuck in that same childish mentality even though they’re meant to be the adults in charge.
