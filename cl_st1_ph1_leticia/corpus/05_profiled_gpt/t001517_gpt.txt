I was thinking about that programme, “Big Fat Gypsy Weddings”, and one of the things they showed was gypsies marrying non-gypsies. It really struck me how different the expectations are, especially for the women. In gypsy culture, the women are very much expected to stay at home, do the cleaning, look after the children, that sort of thing, while the men go out and drink and fight and deal with horses. That’s just the way it seemed to be laid out. So I ended up thinking that those relationships are probably most likely to work when it’s a gypsy woman marrying a non-gypsy man. He probably wouldn’t mind her staying at home, and might even be quite pleased with that arrangement, given how some men feel about traditional roles.
