well when I was out there with them I didn’t do this hovering thing, I just tried to get them going and then leave them to it. I set them up with potato printing and leaf printing, showed them how to make patterns with stuff from the kitchen, and in the sandpit I got them to build these long meeting tunnels in the damp sand and then let them carry on. And they really did, once they’d got the idea they just disappeared into their own little world. Afterwards they apparently went back and said that auntie and uncle were better at playing than granny and grandad, which rather proved my point. If you’re constantly doing everything with children, they never get the chance to use their own imagination or work things out. You give them a few ideas, a bit of a start, and then you should step back and let them do it themselves.
