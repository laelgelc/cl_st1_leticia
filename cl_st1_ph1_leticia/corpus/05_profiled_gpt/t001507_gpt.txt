Well, the language students certainly had the option to do industrial or commercial placements abroad, but in practice very few actually did. The difficulty was that, in most European countries, there’s an expectation that your degree subject closely matches the job you go into. So if you’ve done a language degree, you can’t easily walk into something like accountancy or marketing in, say, Germany or France. That’s quite different from the UK, where we’ve had this long tradition of treating graduates as general trainees, going right back to when most degrees were in classics or theology.
