If people don’t value education, they’re not going to accept it, no matter how much you try to push it at them. You can’t really force education on someone; they have to want it themselves. A lot of the lack of achievement you see is because people don’t take the opportunities that are there. There are plenty of people who aren’t particularly academic who’ve gone on to do very well, and equally there are very intelligent people who just don’t care and don’t bother.

So, yes, part of the situation is down to the government, but not all of it. If you grow up on a council estate with parents who don’t work and don’t value education, you’re very unlikely to achieve much in life, and that, I’d say, isn’t really the government’s fault – it’s down to the individuals.
