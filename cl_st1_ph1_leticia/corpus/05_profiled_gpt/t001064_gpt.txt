When I was about twelve, it was just at the time of the invasion, and I was in this tiny little ballet school. We had a little troupe, and we were sent out to perform for the troops, mostly the Marines down in Portsmouth. Looking back, it does make me laugh rather grimly, because these poor chaps, many of them doomed really, and instead of having some glamorous ladies in sequins turning up to cheer them, they got a bunch of ten- and twelve-year-olds in little ballet frocks pirouetting about.

I remember it all so clearly because my mother was absolutely furious with me afterwards. I’d managed to lose one of my brand new white stage socks somewhere along the way. Of course, in those days with the coupons and all that, a pair of proper white socks was quite a thing. She didn’t see the funny side at all; she was livid about the waste of the coupons.
