There were church steeples down everywhere, not just that one cross I saw – loads of them had come off in the storm. They still made me walk in, through all of that, to this almost empty school where there were just a few teachers hanging about. Palm trees outside were absolutely thrashing about in the wind, tiles flying, everything going mad, and I’m trudging back and forth in it. I ended up having to do that twenty‑minute walk through the typhoon about four times in the end, for basically nothing, just to be there. It felt completely pointless and properly dangerous, but they insisted.
