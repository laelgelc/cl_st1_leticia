From what I’ve seen in loads of films, the scriptwriters seem to take underlying truths and then transplant them into their own versions of reality. They build these worlds where, even if everything is exaggerated or stylised, there’s something in there that people subconsciously recognise as believable. It’s like they’re borrowing bits of real life, twisting them slightly, and then weaving them into the story so the audience feels, “Yes, that could happen,” even when they know it’s all made up.
