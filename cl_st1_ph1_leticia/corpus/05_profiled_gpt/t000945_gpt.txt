What I’m trying to get across is that there’s this thing called the International Phonetic Alphabet, the IPA, which is a single phonetic chart. It’s made up of symbols – some of them look a bit like Greek letters – and each one stands for a particular sound. The idea is that all the sounds in all the world’s languages can be written with these symbols, so in principle you can pronounce any language if it’s written in IPA.
