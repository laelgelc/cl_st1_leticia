Since uni I’ve actually got a lot more fearless, you know. Before, I’d sit there overthinking every little thing, especially with job applications, writing and deleting and getting all nervous. But this time I just thought, right, I’m going to go for it. I decided to email a bunch of people out of the blue to ask about doing some freelance checking and proofreading work. I even went for one of the big ELT publishers, Macmillan, just properly bold, not agonising over every word like I used to.

So I sent this email off and only afterwards I realised what I’d done. I’d actually written “Macmilla” instead of “Macmillan” in the email I’d already sent. I hadn’t put the N on the end. It was only when I looked back at the sent message that it hit me. And the worst thing about it is that this is me trying to sell my proofreading skills to them, saying I’m good at this kind of thing, and I can’t even spell the company’s name right in the email.
