There are so many different varieties of English, both internationally and even just within Britain, so it raises that big question: whose English are we actually supposed to use in international business? I don’t think it has to be any particular “standard” as such. For me, it’s absolutely fine for people to use their own accents, as long as they’re speaking clearly and are a bit careful about very strong regional features or dialect.

I’m from the north-east myself, so I’m thinking of the sort of very local expressions I’d avoid in an international context. I wouldn’t, for example, say something like “I’m going down the to by myself a paper” in a really Geordie way. Not everybody speaks Queen’s English, and that’s perfectly normal, but clarity really matters.
