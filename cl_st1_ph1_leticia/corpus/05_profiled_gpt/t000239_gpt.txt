if you were going into a completely new field, you’d want to engineer it so you’re constantly in situations where you can ask the right questions. The way I see it, you’d pull together a small, unfamiliar but carefully chosen team – people who actually know what they’re doing in that space – and then give them one clear, simple task to deliver in a week.

The key is that you’d all be in the same place, working side by side for that week. That way, you’re not just delegating and waiting for a result; you’re watching, listening, and engaging as the work happens. In that short, intense period, you’d soak up a huge amount of knowledge very quickly, just by being embedded with them while they figure it out and execute.
