In Korea they do grow a lot of what they can themselves, but it’s still not enough to match how much people eat. For example, Koreans eat loads of pork, but I’ve never actually seen a pig there, so I just assume most of it must be imported from China. You see loads of these little local farms everywhere, though. Any bit of free land, someone’s stuck a few crops in it. They tend to grow a fairly limited range of things, and it’s all quite similar: pumpkin, courgette, chillies, green onion, that sort of thing. And they use it in a really straightforward way, just for food, not loads of different fancy variations or anything – it’s quite simple and direct in how it all gets used.
