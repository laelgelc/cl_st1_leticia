Chomsky argued back in the 60s that children can’t be learning language purely from what they hear around them. They come out with sentences they’ve never heard before, and they also somehow avoid loads of possible sentences that would be wrong, even though nobody’s explicitly correcting every mistake. So the idea is there must be some underlying linguistic framework already in the brain. All children really do is set certain parameters: like whether a language is head‑first or head‑last, or whether it allows null subjects, so in English you have to say “I speak” rather than just “speak.”
