well my initial idea, the one I was trying to explain before you went off on the other thing, was to actually build proper, real-life case studies rather than us just chatting and leaving it at that. I was thinking in terms of characters, situations, problems that unfold over time and need to be managed or solved, almost like following people through a process. And instead of it being a dry written case in a textbook, it would be co-created with the people involved, so they’re part of shaping it, and we’re capturing it as moving images.

So you’d have these rich, authentic stories, filmed and structured, that show how issues develop and how people respond, rather than something made up after the fact. The moving image part is really important – not just a video stuck on top of some text, but the core of the case study itself, with all the messiness and detail of real life. Then those could be turned into proper educational resources, rather than just free content floating around, and they’d have far more value because they’re grounded in actual experience.
