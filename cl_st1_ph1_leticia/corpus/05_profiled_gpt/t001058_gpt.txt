Well, one of the excuses they used for the massacre and expulsion of the Jews was that they were supposed to be shaving the silver coins. And then, of course, in this partly demolished building they find this absolutely perfect silver coin, very carefully placed in a niche in one of the columns, which rather gives the lie to that, doesn’t it. The whole place had been filled in with rubble, then much later it was all dug out again and then filled back in. 

We had a historian who’d been working on the immigrant tower, and he took our so‑called synagogue to a conference in Germany. When he described it, the German historians and archaeologists said, “That sounds exactly like such‑and‑such a building in northern Germany,” which I must say I found very pleasing.
