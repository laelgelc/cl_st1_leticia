They literally weren’t using their class books at all. The book would be left at school because, you know, carrying a proper textbook around just wasn’t considered cool. So they’d turn up with virtually nothing to show for what they’d done. At best, there’d be a few scrappy A4 worksheets half‑stuck into the book, but those were never really looked at again. There was no sense of building anything up over time, no proper record of their work, no visible sense of progress, nothing they could flick back through. And in the moment, during lessons, they had nothing solid to refer to or rely on.
