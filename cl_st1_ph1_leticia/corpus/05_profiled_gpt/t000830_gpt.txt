Kevin Brownlow wrote *The Parade's Gone By* out of sheer frustration, really. He was seeing all these terrible, poor-quality silent film prints being shown, projected at the wrong speed, looking ridiculous, and it wound him up. On top of that, both the broader cultural memory and many of the filmmakers themselves had started to treat the silent era as if it was all a bit amateurish, as though those people didn’t really know what they were doing.

So Brownlow basically launched a kind of one-man crusade. He wanted to prove that the filmmakers of that period weren’t idiots, that they were serious, inventive artists who deserved respect. He devoted his entire life to tracking down, preserving, and properly presenting these films, really championing them against that dismissive attitude.
