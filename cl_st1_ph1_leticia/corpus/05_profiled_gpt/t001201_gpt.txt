I just feel like driving is draining me now. The car’s so unreliable that every commute feels like a bit of a mission, and those dark drives with all the headlights are horrible – I’m knackered before I even get to work, and then again coming home. When I take the train, even though it’s slightly more inconvenient and I have to get up earlier, I actually arrive at work with more energy, and I’ve still got plenty left when I get home to do stuff.

The train means I’m getting a bit of incidental exercise as well, so I don’t feel quite so bad about not getting to the gym. That’s part of why I’m thinking I just want to get rid of the car. There’s no point holding onto it. I know I’ve said I’ll take you out in it tonight so you can listen to it, but I can just tell it’s about to cost me more money. I was surprised they even offered six or seven hundred quid, with the mileage, the age, and how battered it is. If it needs more work, the value won’t justify spending two or three hundred at a time on it.
