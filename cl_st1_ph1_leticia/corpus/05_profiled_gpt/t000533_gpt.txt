if you've got a system like, say, China, they’re not just doing national exams for the sake of it, they’re doing them because they serve a really clear administrative purpose. If everyone sits the same paper, you can standardise all those different syllabuses across the country, so you actually know what a mark means wherever the child’s come from. And then you can reliably pick out, you know, the top five, top ten percent who are really flying academically. That means when those students go on to university, the universities can be pretty confident they’re investing all that intensive teaching and resources in people who will actually cope intellectually. Whereas here, in the UK, because we don’t have that same kind of national standardisation, universities can’t be sure in advance which students are genuinely going to be able to manage the level.
