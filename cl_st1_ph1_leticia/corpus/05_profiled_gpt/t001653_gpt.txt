It’s terrifying, really, because with things like breast cancer and cervical cancer there’s been so much publicity, and we all know you check your breasts, you go for your smear, and there’s a sort of clear script you follow. But with ovarian, and even more with vulval and vaginal cancers, I think most women, certainly me, just don’t know what you’re supposed to be looking out for at all.

And the awful thing is the symptoms are so vague that they’re very easily put down to something else – irritable bowel, or “it’s just your age”, or “it’s your hormones”, that sort of thing. So the doctors don’t necessarily think, oh, this could be cancer, let’s get a CA125 done, let’s send her for a scan. They’re following what, statistically, is most likely to be the explanation, which is not cancer, and so they don’t order the tests or the ultrasound. And meanwhile, if it is ovarian or something like that, it’s just quietly getting worse in the background, which is a really frightening thought.
