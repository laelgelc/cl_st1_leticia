I’d really rather nobody used “you know” at all. From a teaching point of view it’s hopeless, because if a student asked me what it actually means, I’d struggle to give a clear answer. It doesn’t seem to carry any real meaning or serve much purpose in the sentence. Technically it’s what we’d call a discourse marker, and you might argue it’s a shortened version of “do you know what I mean?”, but even then it behaves more like a tag question tacked on at the end than anything genuinely necessary.
