I cashed in that three hundred and thirty-three quid pension when I was younger because, honestly, I couldn’t see the point of saving for some future that might never actually turn up. In my head it was all, “what if everything’s gone a bit Mad Max by then?” so I just took the money. Later on though, when I was about twenty‑eight, I got automatically enrolled into a pension at work, six per cent of my salary going in. Looking back, saying no to that would’ve been a really daft move. I can’t pretend it was some clever, planned decision on my part, but I’m glad it happened.
