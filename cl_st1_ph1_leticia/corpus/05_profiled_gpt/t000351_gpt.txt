In the First World War, the medics found something really quite astonishing with some of the badly wounded soldiers. The ones whose wounds had become infested with maggots actually ended up with much cleaner, better‑healed injuries than others. The maggots were busily eating away at all the germs and bacteria in the damaged tissue, so instead of making things worse, they were effectively cleaning the wound up. As a result, the wounds healed more quickly and more cleanly, which must have seemed absolutely extraordinary at the time.
