Well apart from that very first placement in Henley‑in‑Arden, which was only at the beginning, all my actual teaching posts have been in city schools. I haven’t really worked in those quieter non‑city areas people talk about. I did have that school abroad though, and there the behaviour was actually manageable; the pressure came from elsewhere. The head was very difficult, and the parents were incredibly pushy, so you were under constant scrutiny from adults rather than battling chaos in the classroom.

I had a class of thirty‑three, mixed ability, and there were no extra teachers in with me, so it was just me and them, all day, every day. That was hard in a different way. But I’ve always deliberately chosen to work in challenging schools. That’s where I feel good teachers are most needed, really – where the kids don’t have many advantages and the job isn’t easy.
