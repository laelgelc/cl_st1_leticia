well what I meant by “literary predictive” is that science fiction often works as a kind of model for where societies might go. It’s not just wild stories; a lot of it turns out to be oddly accurate in predicting how things evolve. Sometimes it even nudges scientists and engineers into actually developing the technologies that were imagined in the books. 

A big recurring theme is this shift from a scarcity model to a post‑scarcity model. In the scarcity model, everyone’s fighting over limited resources, especially energy – oil, coal, whatever – and that drives wars, politics, the whole lot. Then you’ve these imagined futures where suddenly there’s abundant, cheap energy. Once that happens, the whole geopolitical landscape changes because you’re not scrambling for fuel or basic resources anymore. 

But what’s interesting is that even in those post‑scarcity worlds, the basic human characteristics don’t magically change. People are still people – with the same ambitions, flaws and quirks – just operating in a different material context.
