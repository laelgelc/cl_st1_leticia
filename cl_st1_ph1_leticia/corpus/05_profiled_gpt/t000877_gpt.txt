Well, I think one of the reasons I've had such a wide‑ranging life is that I was on my own for a very long time before I married. I didn't marry until my mid‑forties, so I had all those years to try different things, and I did rather make the most of it. 

I was very influenced by a book called *Anybody Can Do Anything*, about this woman who just kept changing her job, going from one thing to another, always looking for something more interesting or better. That really struck a chord with me, and I suppose I took it a bit to heart and thought, well, why shouldn’t I do the same?

Like everybody else, of course, it hasn’t all been marvellous. I’ve had some very big highs, moments that were absolutely wonderful, but I’ve also had some very big lows. I do think the two tend to go together: if you have very big highs in life, you usually get very big lows as well.
