So in my head this different housing system is quite simple. You’d still effectively own your home – you live in it, you can change it, make it how you want – but you’re not under pressure to move just to release money, and you’re not selling it on the open market. If, say, you decided you really fancied living in Devon, you’d go onto a website and look for someone in Devon who wanted to leave there and move to where you are. You’d just swap houses with them, directly. No messing around with estate agents or huge mortgages, just a straightforward exchange. While you’re in the house, it’s yours to live in and modify, but when you leave you’re not cashing in, you’re just moving within a shared system. The whole idea is that the housing stock is collectively ours, and we just move around within it as our lives change, rather than treating houses as things to buy and sell for profit.
