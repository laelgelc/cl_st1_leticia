During the war, when lots of men went off to fight, women stepped into loads of the jobs men had been doing. They went into factories, onto farms, doing all sorts of work that had been seen as “men’s jobs” before. For many women it was actually quite exciting – they had more freedom, they were earning their own money, and doing important, interesting work. But when the war ended and the men came back, the men wanted their old jobs back. Women were then expected to give up those jobs and go back to looking after children and doing domestic work at home. That made many women really upset, because they wanted to carry on working, so it created a very difficult situation.
