In the last ten or eleven years I was really more of a manager than a front‑line adviser. Most of my time was spent managing the staff who actually delivered the careers service, and only about ten per cent of my week was still seeing students myself. That’s why I think it’s actually more interesting to talk about what a careers adviser does than what a manager does. Universities can define the role pretty much as they like, and this was the fourth university I’d worked in, so I’d seen it done in quite a few different ways.
