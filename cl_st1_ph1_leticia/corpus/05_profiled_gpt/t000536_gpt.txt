I think the “earn as you learn” idea only really works if it’s properly structured from the start. So you’d still pay for your first degree, but it would be part of a clear programme where you’re alternating between time at university and time in an industry-based learning role with a partner organisation. 

You’d really come to university to do your studies, but your so‑called free time wouldn’t actually be free time; it would be built into the curriculum that you go out and earn some money with this partnership we’ve arranged. Then you’d come back and tell us what you’ve learned. 

We’d look at everything together – your exams, your portfolio, your coursework, some odd bits – as a broader picture of what you’ve actually learned.
