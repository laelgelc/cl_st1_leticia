it’s not just about the academic merit, though, is it, when you’re choosing a university. You’ve got to actually like where you’re living – the campus, the city, the feel of the place – because that’s your home for several years. And that whole business of moving around, that kind of geographical mobility, is really part of the European student experience, and the wider international one too. You think of students coming from China or Vietnam, say, and they might arrive in somewhere like London and realise they absolutely hate it and would actually be much happier in a smaller place. But they’re effectively prevented from moving, because they have to pay all the fees up front, and then they’re locked into that payment scheme, so they can’t just change their minds and transfer.
