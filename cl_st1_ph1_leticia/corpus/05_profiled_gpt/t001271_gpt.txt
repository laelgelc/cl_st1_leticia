It feels as though they’re much more interested in whether we tick the right demographic boxes than whether we’ve actually got the right people to run a business properly. 

What I find quite difficult is this whole business of trying to quantify our “social value” in their terms. They want it all written down and measured, and that’s just not how we’ve ever operated. We’ve never gone after grants before, so we haven’t had to frame what we do in that language. 

Our basic principle has always been very straightforward: we have to earn more than we spend. That’s how we’ve kept going, by making sure the money coming in covers what goes out, rather than relying on someone else’s funding criteria. So suddenly being asked to present ourselves as a set of demographics and social outcomes, it’s quite an alien way of looking at what we do.
