The trouble for overseas students was that the government kept changing the rules about whether they could legally work in the UK, so there was this constant instability. Most of them desperately wanted to stay on and work here after their degrees, and they were very hopeful about the post-study work visa, which at that point allowed them to work for two years. But the fact that they had that legal entitlement didn’t actually mean employers were keen to take them on. Employers generally recruit graduates with the idea of developing them over the long term, not just for a short period. So from the employers’ point of view, someone who was almost certainly going to have to leave after two years just wasn’t an attractive prospect, whatever the visa technically allowed.
