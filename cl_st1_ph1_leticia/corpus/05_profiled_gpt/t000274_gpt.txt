With a new top set of first years, that first week I wouldn’t bother with maths at all. I’d tell them straight, “You’re not doing maths this week, we’re doing behaviour.” I made it clear I was only interested in how they behaved in my lessons. What they did elsewhere, that was between them and whoever was teaching them, but in my room it was my rules.

I’d get them talking, saying, “You know how to behave, don’t you? And you know how to muck about as well.” And they’d admit it, of course. Once they’d said it themselves, I’d push them: “Right then, if you know the difference, you don’t play up for me, agreed?” And they’d agree, because they’d more or less talked themselves into it.

Doing that in the first week set the pattern. I used to say to them, “If we get this sorted now, this is how it’ll be for the next five years.” And it generally was.
