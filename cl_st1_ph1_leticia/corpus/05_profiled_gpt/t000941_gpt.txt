So once you’ve edited the material, it actually becomes a narrative case study. You’d have clearly defined characters, who they are, what their jobs are, and the particular problem they’re facing. Then you map out what they can do about it – the possible actions they might take – which often ties into their relationships with other people.

For example, you might end up with a “Friends of Science” director who’s also a leading chiropractic academic. That combination itself gives you a rich, real-life problem to build around, with professional pressures, personal beliefs, and network connections all feeding into the story and the choices they could make.
