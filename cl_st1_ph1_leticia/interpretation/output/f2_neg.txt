Interpretation of Factor f2 (negative pole)

1. Which group drives this pole?

- Mean scores:
  - human: **-0.15**
  - profiled_gpt: +0.09
  - unprofiled_gpt: +0.07  

The **negative** side of Factor 2 is therefore **driven by human speakers**. Both GPT conditions sit on the positive side (i.e. *away* from this pole), suggesting that this dimension captures something that humans do much more than the LLMs.

2. Linguistic profile of the f2 negative pole

High negative loadings:
- god (-.67)
- fuck (-.66)
- pound (-.60)
- shit (-.46)
- gonna (-.45)
- single (-.41)

Key observations from the loadings:

- **Expletives / taboo items**: god, fuck, shit  
  These are classic markers of:
  - emotional stance and evaluation
  - informality and colloquial speech
  - spontaneous, low-monitoring production

- **Colloquial future marker**: gonna  
  - Strongly associated with **spoken, informal, conversational** style.
  - Often co-occurs with stance-taking and narrative projection (“what’s gonna happen”).

- **pound, single**  
  - Likely to be:
    - “pound” as currency or weight, often in everyday talk about money, shopping, etc.
    - “single” as relationship status, or as a count noun (“every single time”), both common in informal, personal narratives.
  - These are less obviously about stance, but they fit **everyday, personal-life, informal topics**.

Overall, the loading set points to:
- **highly informal, colloquial spoken style**
- **strong affective / evaluative stance**, often negative or intense
- **personal, everyday-life content** (money, relationships, complaints, gossip)

3. Evidence from the example excerpts

All 10 high-scoring texts are **human**. GPT does not appear in the top examples, reinforcing that this is a **human-conversational** pole.

Across the excerpts, the loading items are used in very similar ways:

a) “oh my god” as an affective discourse marker

Examples: t001195, t001258, t001261, t001351, t001354

- “oh my god you're gonna get brownie points now”
- “oh my god and erm so we were in shopping”
- “oh god I know what this is gonna be about now”
- “oh my god when we were in I didn't see many couples”
- “oh my god she's gonna be s she did give me such a hard time”

Functions:
- Express **surprise, amusement, anxiety, anticipation**.
- Used as **turn-openers** and **story climaxes**.
- Very typical of **informal, emotionally engaged storytelling** among peers.

b) “gonna” as colloquial future and stance projection

Examples: t001195, t001258, t001261, t001351, t001354, t000454, t000452

- “you're gonna get brownie points now”
- “I'm not gonna get it from here cos it's too expensive”
- “I know what this is gonna be about now”
- “we was gonna watch erm the Kingsman”
- “she's gonna be s she did give me such a hard time”
- “we are gonna like put us kids in school for a year”
- “you always just gonna be there so that you can just keep the peace”

Functions:
- Everyday **future reference** in speech.
- Often embedded in **reported speech** and **narrative projection** (“what’s going to happen”, “what someone is going to do”).
- Strongly indexes **informal, spoken, interpersonal** discourse.

c) Strong negative evaluation and venting (fuck, shit, god in evaluative contexts)

Examples: t000453, t000452, t000450

- “my mum god her life just sounds so fucking sad”
- “is your life actually that fucking sad”
- “what the fuck is actually happening”
- “can I not actually have an opinion in this house”
- “that god is a thing that people can talk to and pray to”

Functions:
- **Intensified negative evaluation** of people and situations.
- **Venting, frustration, criticism** of family dynamics, religion, or life circumstances.
- Highly **stance-laden**, with strong personal involvement.

d) Everyday, personal-life narratives

Across the excerpts, topics include:
- family arguments and long-term tenants (t000454, t000452)
- gossip about parents and book club (t000453)
- relationship drama and Boxing Day plans (t001261)
- going to Nando’s and the cinema (t001351)
- shopping for Christmas pudding (t001258)
- medical appointments and back pain (t001354)
- casual talk about planes and films (t001195)

These are:
- **first-person, experience-based stories**
- rich in **reported speech** and **dialogue**
- full of **stance markers** and **emotional reactions**

4. Integrating loadings and examples

Taking loadings and examples together, this pole is best characterised by:

- **Register/style**:
  - Highly **informal**, **spoken**, **colloquial**.
  - Low monitoring, with frequent **discourse markers**, **fillers**, and **expletives**.
  - Strong presence of **gonna** and formulaic exclamations (“oh my god”).

- **Discourse function**:
  - **Stance-heavy**: speakers evaluate, complain, gossip, and react.
  - **Narrative and anecdotal**: recounting events, especially interpersonal conflicts and everyday mishaps.
  - **Affective alignment**: inviting the listener to share emotional reactions (“oh my god”, “what the fuck”).

- **Content domain**:
  - **Personal and interpersonal**: family, partners, friends, social outings, health.
  - Often **problem-focused** or **drama-focused**: arguments, frustrations, disappointments, gossip.

- **Source pattern**:
  - Strongly associated with **human** turns.
  - GPT outputs (both profiled and unprofiled) sit away from this pole, suggesting that LLMs are comparatively more restrained in:
    - expletives and taboo language
    - highly colloquial forms like “gonna”
    - raw, emotionally charged venting.

5. Possible labels for f2 (negative pole)

Focusing on this pole alone (no versus label), plausible discourse-dimension labels are:

1. **Emotionally Charged Colloquial Storytelling**
   - Captures:
     - the informal spoken style (gonna, oh my god)
     - the narrative structure (stories about family, relationships, outings)
     - the strong affect (fuck, shit, god as expletives and stance markers).

2. **Affective Venting and Gossip in Everyday Talk**
   - Emphasises:
     - the **venting/complaining** function (family arguments, “what the fuck is actually happening”)
     - **gossip-like evaluation** of others (mum’s “fucking sad” life, book club drama)
     - the everyday, interpersonal focus.

3. **Informal Stance-Heavy Personal Narratives**
   - Highlights:
     - the **stance** dimension (intense evaluation, emotional alignment)
     - the **personal narrative** frame (first-person experiences, reported speech)
     - the **informal spoken** character (gonna, expletives, discourse markers).

Any of these three would be defensible. If a single label is needed, a compact formulation that reflects both the loadings and the examples is:

**“Emotionally Charged Colloquial Personal Talk”**

This label:
- foregrounds **emotionally charged** (fuck, shit, oh my god; venting, frustration, excitement),
- **colloquial** (gonna, expletives, informal syntax),
- **personal talk** (family, relationships, everyday experiences),
and aligns with the fact that this pole is **driven by human conversational turns** rather than LLM-generated ones.
