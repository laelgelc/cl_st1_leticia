Interpretation of Factor f1 (negative pole)

1. Which group is driving this pole?

- Mean scores (higher = more positive f1; lower = more negative f1):
  - profiled_gpt: 1.04  
  - unprofiled_gpt: 1.02  
  - human: 0.57  

For the negative pole, we look at the *lowest* mean scores. Humans are clearly lowest, so **the negative pole is human‑driven**. GPT texts (both profiled and unprofiled) cluster much more towards the positive side of this factor.

So this pole is picking out a discourse style that humans use much more than the LLMs.

2. Linguistic loadings

Negative pole loadings (strongest first):

- shit (−.78)
- horrible (−.42)
- bloke (−.41)
- minute (−.33)

Key points:

- The dominant marker is **shit**, a high‑frequency, strongly evaluative swear word in colloquial British English.
- **horrible** is another strongly negative evaluative adjective.
- **bloke** is an informal, colloquial term for ‘man’, strongly associated with spoken British English and everyday talk.
- **minute** in conversation often appears in expressions like “in a minute”, “wait a minute”, “give me a minute”, which are informal, interactional timing devices.

Taken together, the loadings point to:

- **Colloquial, informal British conversational style**
- **Strong negative evaluation and complaint**
- **Emotionally charged, stance‑heavy talk about people and situations**

3. What the examples show

All 10 high‑scoring texts are human, and all are anchored by **shit**. Across the excerpts, shit is used in several ways:

- As a general negative evaluation of a state/situation:
  - “be in a shit mood”
  - “it's just shit like rude in general”
- As part of a dismissive/hostile stance:
  - “all that sort of shit”
  - “they just don't give a shit”
  - “I were inviting this shit”
- As an insult or negative label for a person:
  - “he were a violent shit”
  - “they're shit at people skills”
- As a negative evaluation of someone’s behaviour/character:
  - “it shit about my sister”
  - “not give a shit”

Across the excerpts, the surrounding discourse consistently involves:

- **Narratives of interpersonal conflict or frustration**  
  – partners working too much, ex‑boyfriends being violent, landlords being rude, parents not caring, colleagues/managers being bad at people skills, social media drama, etc.

- **Strong stance‑taking and alignment**  
  – speakers position themselves morally and emotionally: “I'm not that sort of person”, “they just don't give a shit”, “he were a violent shit”, “I'm so proud of you… it shit about my sister”.

- **Highly informal, speech‑like style**  
  – lots of discourse markers and fillers (“like”, “you know”, “sort of thing”, “cos”, “erm”), first‑person narratives, and unplanned spoken syntax.

Crucially, the **loading word shit is doing the core discourse work**: it intensifies negative stance, marks emotional involvement, and indexes a casual, intimate register.

4. Integrating loadings and examples

Weighting loadings (whole corpus) and examples (top 10 texts) equally, the negative pole of f1 can be characterised as:

- **Register/style**: highly informal, colloquial, speech‑like, with British flavour (shit, bloke).
- **Function**: expressing **strong negative evaluation**, complaint, and emotional reaction to people and events.
- **Stance**: involved, subjective, often morally charged; the speaker is not neutral but clearly judging others’ behaviour or situations.
- **Source**: overwhelmingly **human**; GPT outputs, even when profiled, are much less likely to use this kind of swearing and raw negative stance, hence their higher (more positive) mean scores.

5. Possible labels for this pole

Focusing on this pole alone (no versus label), plausible discourse‑dimension labels include:

1. **“Colloquial Negative‑Stance Complaint Discourse”**  
   - Captures:
     - colloquial register (shit, bloke, minute as timing device)
     - negative stance (shit, horrible)
     - the dominant function of complaining about people/situations.

2. **“Emotionally Charged Interpersonal Evaluation”**  
   - Emphasises:
     - strong emotional involvement
     - evaluation of others’ behaviour and relationships (partners, exes, landlords, managers, parents).

3. **“Informal Swearing‑Based Evaluation Style”**  
   - Highlights:
     - the central role of shit as a resource for evaluation and stance
     - the specifically informal, taboo‑lexis‑based way of doing that evaluation.

Any of these would be defensible. If a single label is needed, the most comprehensive, loadings‑ and example‑driven formulation is:

**Colloquial Negative‑Stance Complaint Discourse**

This label reflects:

- **Colloquial**: indexed by shit, bloke, minute and the spoken style in the excerpts.
- **Negative‑Stance**: encoded lexically by shit, horrible and pragmatically by the narratives of frustration and conflict.
- **Complaint Discourse**: the recurrent discourse function across the high‑scoring texts is to complain about, criticise, or vent about people and situations.

6. Group driving the pole

- The **human group** is clearly driving this negative pole:
  - They have the lowest mean f1 scores.
  - All top negative‑pole examples are human.
  - The key lexical marker (shit) is typical of unconstrained human conversation but strongly dispreferred in GPT outputs under standard safety and politeness constraints.

So, Factor f1 (negative pole) can be interpreted as a **human‑driven dimension of colloquial, emotionally involved complaint and negative evaluation, heavily realised through swearing and informal British conversational lexis.**
