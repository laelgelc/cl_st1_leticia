This pole is best interpreted as a **lexicalised stance of strong negative evaluation**, centred on the repeated use of a small set of high‑intensity evaluative adjectives.

### 1. What drives the pole?

- Mean scores:
  - profiled_gpt: +0.057  
  - unprofiled_gpt: +0.056  
  - human: −0.122  

So **LLM turns (both profiled and unprofiled) are slightly more positive on this factor than human turns**, and the *very* high‑scoring texts shown are all from **profiled_gpt**. Humans are, on average, lower on this dimension. That suggests the pole reflects a style that GPT (especially with profiling) tends to amplify.

### 2. Loadings and their semantics

Positive loadings:

- **awful (1.05)** – very strong, prototypical negative evaluation
- **horrible (.48)** – similarly strong negative evaluation
- **single (.52)** – here not numerically literal, but often in collocations like “every single X”, i.e. intensifying focus
- **pick (.31)** – appears in “pick it out”, “picking up bits”, “picked apart”, “picked me up”; often part of constructions that highlight difficulty, burden, or scrutiny

The core of the pole is clearly **awful/horrible**, with *single* and *pick* contributing a pattern of **intensification and focus on specific problematic items or experiences**.

### 3. Patterns in the high‑scoring excerpts

Across the examples, the loading words are used to:

- **Evaluate experiences or situations as strongly negative**:
  - “which is just **awful**” (abuse from people you know)
  - “it makes the whole story feel really **horrible** and properly dark”
  - “sing the bass part … which is just **awful**”
  - “the grammar in this thing is actually **awful**”
  - “that **awful** hangover”
  - “she’s always been **awful** with her, really manipulative, calling her **horrible** names”
  - “something about Homs, we do register it as **horrible** … ‘God, that’s **awful**’”

- **Intensify or single out problematic elements**:
  - “I can’t think of a **single** person who is [happy with it]”
  - “every **single** day is crammed”
  - “every **single** event is a decision made by God”
  - “everything you say online gets **picked** apart”
  - “I can’t **pick** it out properly”
  - “driving in this **awful** weather”
  - “picking up bits that used to be someone else’s job”

- **Frame moral or emotional judgement**:
  - Abuse from acquaintances on Twitter is “just **awful**”
  - Religious group’s beliefs about child death are “as **horrible** as that”
  - Cutting off a daughter is framed as morally shocking
  - Indifference to distant suffering: “we do register it as **horrible** … ‘God, that’s **awful**’”

The discourse function is not just describing bad states; it is **positioning the speaker affectively**: shocked, appalled, overwhelmed, burdened, or critical.

### 4. Discourse characteristics of this pole

Taken together, the loadings and examples point to a dimension of:

- **High‑intensity negative stance**: frequent use of “awful”, “horrible” as stance markers.
- **Affective evaluation of events and people**: abuse, horror stories, difficult exams, over‑full itineraries, manipulative parents, extreme religious beliefs, war coverage.
- **Intensifying focus on totality or uniqueness**: “every single day”, “every single event”, “not a single person”, which heightens the sense of extremity or pervasiveness of the negative situation.
- **Narratives of burden, pressure, or scrutiny**: “picked apart”, “picking up bits”, “can’t pick it out properly” – these verbs help construe situations as demanding or oppressive.

The pole is therefore less about a specific topic (e.g. politics, religion, travel) and more about a **recurrent evaluative style**: narrating experiences through **strongly negative, often moral or emotional, judgements**.

### 5. Role of GPT vs humans

- GPT (especially profiled) appears to **over‑supply high‑intensity evaluative adjectives** in conversational narrative, which pushes its texts up this factor.
- Human speakers, on average, are lower, suggesting they either:
  - use a wider range of evaluative resources, or
  - are less prone to repeated “awful/horrible” as default stance markers.

So this pole is **LLM‑driven**, reflecting a somewhat stylised, emphatic negative stance that GPT adopts when dramatising problems or expressing sympathy.

### 6. Possible labels for this pole

Any label should capture: (i) strong negative evaluation, (ii) affective/moral stance, (iii) intensification.

Some plausible labels:

1. **Intensified Negative Evaluation**
   - Highlights the repeated use of “awful”, “horrible”, “every single” to evaluate situations and people.

2. **Affective Complaint / Appalled Stance**
   - Captures the discourse of being appalled, burdened, or put upon, often in narrative form.

3. **Emphatic Moral–Emotional Critique**
   - Reflects that many targets are morally charged (abuse, extreme religion, parental cruelty, war), and the stance is both emotional and evaluative.

If a single label is needed, a concise option that fits both the loadings and examples is:

> **“Intensified Negative Stance and Complaint”**

This foregrounds:
- the **stance** (awful, horrible),
- its **intensity** (every single, picked apart),
- and its typical discourse function in these conversations: **complaining about or morally condemning situations and behaviours**.
