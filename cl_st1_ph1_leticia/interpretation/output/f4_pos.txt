This pole is best understood as a **discourse of constructive change and evaluative framing**, strongly associated with human speakers.

## 1. Who drives this pole?

Mean scores are very similar, but humans are slightly higher:

- human: 0.228  
- profiled_gpt: 0.190  
- unprofiled_gpt: 0.188  

All three groups use this discourse, but **human turns are the main driver** of the positive pole.

---

## 2. Linguistic profile of the pole

High-loading items:

- **build (.71)**
- **fact (.67)**
- **become (.61)**
- **huge (.43)**
- **great (.41)**
- **change (.37)**
- **set (.32)**
- **somebody (.32)**

Two clusters stand out:

1. **Process / transformation verbs**  
   - build, become, change, set  
   These encode *development, construction, and transition* (building things, becoming something, changing, setting things up).

2. **Evaluative and evidential framing**  
   - fact, great, huge, somebody  
   - fact: marking information as real, important, or evidential  
   - great/huge: positive, often emphatic evaluation  
   - somebody: generic human reference, often used to frame examples or roles (“somebody did the biopsy”, “we had somebody who…”)

So lexically, this pole combines:

- **talk about processes of building/setting up and becoming/changing**, with
- **explicit evaluation and evidential marking**, and
- **generic human roles (“somebody”) in those processes**.

## 3. What the examples show

All top-scoring texts are human. Across them, the loading words pattern in two main discourse functions.

### a) Personal and psychological transformation

Ex. t001038 (become, change, set, somebody):

- “somebody who is evolving… we are always evolving we always changing”
- “I don't think a personality is ever set”
- “you do become a different person”
- “you are reinvented in every now”
- “life changing… recreating or creating something new”

Here, **become/change/set** are used to theorise identity as fluid and developing. The speaker constructs a discourse of **ongoing personal transformation**, with “somebody” as a generic person undergoing change.

Ex. t000190, t001682 (fact, become):

- “you do become better at it”
- “I do get a bit concerned about that fact that his world's smaller so this has become a big thing in his life”

Again, **become** marks gradual change in ability or perspective; **fact** highlights a salient, somewhat problematic reality. This is **reflective talk about how people and situations develop over time**.

### b) Building, setting up, and institutional/organisational processes

Ex. t000652 (build, set, great):

- “started building this town”
- “I just sort of set up the basics”
- “he made it look beautiful… a huge huge great barn”

Ex. t001629 (build, great):

- “I had to sort of build it up for myself”
- “didn't exactly give me a great deal of direction”
- “start and build it all myself”

Ex. t001501 (great, set, somebody):

- “we had somebody who… covered all science and engineering disciplines”
- “that's a quite a different set of responsibilities”
- “this is where the greatest variation is”
- “we would set up… group work sessions” (implied by “set” in context)

These are **procedural, institutional, or project-oriented narratives**: building towns, building roles, building a job remit, setting up modules and services. “Somebody” is used to refer to role-holders in an organisation. “Great” often evaluates scale or importance (“great barn”, “greatest variation”).

### c) Evaluative framing and evidential “fact”

Ex. t000005, t000417, t000942, t001662 (fact, great, somebody):

- “in fact they even organised…”
- “that would be great because you need challenging”
- “the fact that you've putting it in your online course…”
- “in fact actually in a way it was really bad news to me”
- “it was a great big tome… a real good medical handbook”

Here **fact** introduces or highlights key points, often contrasting expectations or underlining significance. **Great/huge** provide **strong positive or scalar evaluation** (great big balloon, great idea, great big tome). “Somebody” again marks generic participants in scenarios (somebody did the biopsy; somebody’s sitting there going…).

Across these examples, the discourse is:

- **about how things and people are built up, set up, and change over time**, and
- **framed with explicit evaluation (great/huge) and evidential marking (fact)**, often via
- **generic human roles (somebody) in those processes**.

## 4. Proposed labels and justification

Any label should capture both:

- the **processual / developmental** aspect (build, become, change, set), and  
- the **evaluative / evidential framing** (fact, great, huge, somebody).

Possible labels:

1. **“Constructive Change and Evaluation”**  
   - “Constructive” reflects *build, set* (constructing projects, roles, situations).  
   - “Change” reflects *become, change* (personal and situational transformation).  
   - “Evaluation” reflects *fact, great, huge* and the way speakers frame these processes as significant, positive, or problematic.

2. **“Developmental Narratives of Building and Becoming”**  
   - Emphasises the narrative, temporal aspect: people recount how they or their work “build up”, “become better”, “change”, “set up” things.  
   - Fits both personal-identity talk (t001038, t000190, t001682) and institutional/professional accounts (t000652, t001501, t001629).

3. **“Evidential, Evaluative Talk about Growth and Construction”**  
   - “Evidential” foregrounds *fact* as a discourse marker.  
   - “Evaluative” captures *great, huge*.  
   - “Growth and construction” covers *build, become, change, set*.

Among these, the most compact and accurate for this pole is:

> **Constructive Change and Evaluation**

Because:

- It generalises across **personal growth**, **professional/institutional building**, and **project construction**.
- It captures the **lexical core**:  
  - constructive: build, set  
  - change: become, change  
  - evaluation: fact, great, huge, somebody (as generic human anchor in evaluative scenarios)
- It matches the **discourse functions** seen in the excerpts:  
  - explaining how identities, skills, and roles develop  
  - narrating how things are built or set up  
  - marking key points as factual and/or “great”, “huge”, “big”, often with a positive or emphatic stance.

---

## 5. Summary

- This positive pole is **human-led** and encodes a discourse where speakers:
  - talk about **building and setting up** things (projects, jobs, institutions),
  - describe **becoming and changing** (selves, others, situations),
  - and frame these processes with **evidential and evaluative language** (“in fact”, “great”, “huge”), often via generic human roles (“somebody”).

A suitable label for this pole is:

> **Constructive Change and Evaluation**
