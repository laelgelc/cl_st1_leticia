what I mean by “literary predictive” is that science fiction often acts like a kind of model for the future, you know, it sets up these worlds and assumptions and then runs them forward, and a lot of the time it turns out to be surprisingly accurate about how societies actually evolve. Sometimes it even pushes science along, because scientists grow up reading this stuff and then go off and try to build what they’ve imagined. A big recurring theme is this shift from a scarcity model, where everyone’s fighting over limited resources, especially energy, into a post‑scarcity model where you’ve got really abundant, cheap energy. Once you’ve got that, the whole geopolitical picture changes, because you’re not scrambling over oil or food or whatever in the same way. But even with that transformation, the basic human characteristics, the way people are, don’t magically change; it’s the context that shifts, not the core of human nature.
