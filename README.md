# Corpus Linguistics - Study 1 - Leticia

## Phase 1 - Oral presentation at IVACS 2026

### Overview

This project investigates how social identities are represented in conversation by comparing human interaction with AI-generated dialog. It is motivated by the question of whether AI can simulate human identities in ways that go beyond surface-level role performance and instead reflect broader discourse patterns and language-use choices.

While recent studies suggest that large language models can imitate social roles in conversation, this research focuses more specifically on how such identity simulations are realised linguistically. To address this, the study adopts a variationist, corpus-based approach centered on language use in spoken interaction.

The project draws on spoken corpus data to examine conversational structure, speaker participation, and recurring linguistic patterns that may distinguish human and AI-generated dialog. In this phase, the work is oriented toward preparing data and analytical resources for the study and for the oral presentation at IVACS 2026.

### Methodology

1. We will segment the conversations from the BNC 2014 Spoken corpus into 20-turn segments. This segmentation is necessary because LLMs perform better with shorter texts.

2. For each conversation, we will identify the speaker with the highest number of turns in order to select them to be replaced by a GPT-generated speaker. We may assign this artificial speaker a speaker ID that does not match any existing one, for example, “S0999”.

3. The utterances selected to be generated by GPT will be summarised by GPT itself.

4. The sociodemographic profiles of the original speakers can be obtained from the metadata of each conversation.

5. Each 20-turn segment will be submitted to GPT so that it can generate the turn of the replaced speaker according to the corresponding turn summary.

6. There will be two types of turns generated by GPT:

   - A prompt that provides the sociodemographic profile of the original speaker to be simulated
   - A prompt that does not provide such a profile

7. The study corpus will contain about 4,900 turns. One third will consist of turns from the original human speakers. Another third will consist of synthetic turns guided by sociodemographic profile. The final third will consist of synthetic turns not guided by those profiles.
